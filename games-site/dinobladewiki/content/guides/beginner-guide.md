# Dinoblade Beginner Guide — Survive Your First Hunt

Welcome to Dinoblade, a dinosaur-themed Soulslike developed by Team Spino LLC where you wield massive weapons in your dinosaur's jaws and battle through prehistoric biomes. Launching July 23, 2026 on Steam, Dinoblade challenges you to survive a world where every raptor, pterosaur, and towering tyrant wants you for lunch.

This guide covers everything a new player needs to know: core combat mechanics, stat allocation, and how to survive your first expedition through the Jurassic Plains.

---

## Core Combat Mechanics

### Stamina Management

Stamina is the most critical resource in Dinoblade. Every action — attacking, dodging, sprinting, and parrying — drains your stamina bar. If it empties, you enter a **fatigued state** where you cannot attack or dodge for roughly 2 seconds, leaving you completely vulnerable.

| Action | Stamina Cost |
|--------|-------------|
| Light Attack | 12-15% of bar |
| Heavy Attack | 25-30% of bar |
| Roll / Dodge | 18% of bar |
| Sprint (per second) | 8% of bar |
| Parry | 15% of bar |
| Block (per hit) | 10-20% of bar |

**Rule of thumb:** Never let your stamina drop below 20% in combat. Always leave enough to dodge or parry.

### Light vs Heavy Attacks

- **Light Attacks** are fast, cost less stamina, and chain into 3-5 hit combos. Use them to punish small openings and build pressure.
- **Heavy Attacks** are slow, deal 1.5x-2x damage, and excel at **staggering** enemies and breaking their guard. They leave you vulnerable during the wind-up, so time them carefully.

Hold the heavy attack button to perform a **charged heavy** — this increases damage and stagger potential significantly, but costs more stamina.

### Roll / Dodge

Your dodge roll grants **invincibility frames (i-frames)** during the first portion of the animation. The timing takes practice, but mastering it is essential.

- **Base i-frames:** 13 frames at 60 FPS (roughly 0.22 seconds)
- **Equip load below 30%:** 16 i-frames (fast roll, lowest recovery)
- **Equip load 30-60%:** 13 i-frames (medium roll)
- **Equip load above 60%:** 9 i-frames (fat roll, slow recovery)

Keep your equip load under 60% at all times. Under 30% is ideal for Dexterity builds.

### Parry Basics

Parrying is a high-risk, high-reward mechanic. Press the parry button just before an enemy's attack lands to deflect it and leave the enemy open for a **riposte** — a critical attack that deals massive damage.

- **Active parry frames:** 5 frames (very tight — practice is essential)
- **Perfect parry:** Timing the parry in the exact center of the active window grants a longer riposte window.
- **Failed parry:** You take full damage and lose stamina.

See the [Parry Guide](/guides/parry-guide) for detailed frame data on every boss.

## Stat Breakdown

Dinoblade has five core stats. Each point invested increases the associated attributes.

| Stat | Primary Effect | Secondary Effect | Best For |
|------|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Strength (STR)** | Weapon damage (heavy weapons), stagger power | Equip load capacity | Greatsword, War Hammer, Battle Axe |
| **Dexterity (DEX)** | Weapon damage (light weapons), critical hit damage | Cast speed for special skills | Claws, Scythe, Spear |
| **Endurance (END)** | Maximum stamina, stamina recovery rate | — | All builds (minimum 12-15) |
| **Vitality (VIT)** | Maximum HP, physical defense | Poison resistance | All builds (minimum 15) |
| **Spirit (SPI)** | Special skill damage, Boss Soul Summon duration | Special skill cooldown reduction | Hybrid and magic-focused builds |

### Recommended Starting Stats

For your first character, aim for a **balanced melee build** that can survive mistakes while dealing solid damage:

| Stat | Points | Reasoning |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Vitality | 15 | Survivability is king for new players. Extra HP lets you learn boss patterns without dying in one hit. |
| Endurance | 12 | Enough stamina for 2-3 attacks + a dodge roll. Stamina management is easier with a larger bar. |
| Strength | 14 | Unlocks the Greatsword and most early-game heavy weapons. Good damage output. |
| Dexterity | 10 | Minimum for equipping backup weapons and medium armor. |
| Spirit | 8 | Base level sufficient for basic special skills. Invest later if desired. |

This stat spread lets you wield the **Greatsword** found early in the Jurassic Plains and wear medium armor comfortably.

## First Area Tips: Jurassic Plains

The Jurassic Plains is the first biome you'll explore. It serves as a tutorial zone — but don't underestimate it.

### What to Do First

1. **Find the first campfire (bonfire equivalent).** Resting restores HP and respawns enemies. Light every campfire you find — they're permanent fast travel points.
2. **Practice parrying on small raptors.** The small raptors in the opening area have slow, telegraphed attacks. Use them to practice parry timing. See the [Parry Guide](/guides/parry-guide) for drills.
3. **Collect ancient bones and flora.** These are crafting materials used to upgrade your weapons at the Blacksmith.
4. **Explore thoroughly.** Two hidden items in the Jurassic Plains are easily missed:
   - **Ancient Guardian Boots** — tucked behind a waterfall northeast of the first campfire
   - **Fossil Greatsword** — a hidden weapon in a secret cave (see [Secrets Guide](/guides/secrets-guide))

### Enemy Priority

| Enemy | Threat Level | Strategy |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Small Raptor | Low | Parry practice fodder. 2-3 light attacks kill. |
| Pterosaur Scout | Low-Medium | Ranged enemy. Close distance quickly and use heavy attack to stagger. |
| Horned Herbivore | Medium | High HP, charges. Dodge sideways and punish from the flank. |
| Alpha Raptor | High | Fast, aggressive, can grab. Keep your distance and parry its leap attack. |
| Fallen Warrior (miniboss) | Very High | Slow but hits hard. Learn its 3-hit combo timing for parries. Rewards an Ancient Bone. |

### What to Prioritize

- **Upgrade your weapon first.** Weapon upgrades (using Ancient Bones) provide more damage than leveling stats early on. Find the Blacksmith at the Plains Outpost.
- **Level Vitality to 20** before tackling the first boss, Kira the Exile. Extra HP is a safety net.
- **Gather at least 5 Healing Herbs** before each boss attempt. They're scattered throughout the Plains — look for glowing green plants near water sources.
- **Find and equip the Ancient Guardian armor set.** It provides excellent defense for the early game and is found in chests around the Plains.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

1. **Spamming attacks.** Every attack costs stamina. Spamming leaves you exhausted and dead. Breathe between combos.
2. **Ignoring stamina.** Watch your stamina bar like it's your health bar — because it might as well be.
3. **Panic rolling.** Rolling too early or too late gets you hit. Learn attack patterns first, then roll through them.
4. **Neglecting parry.** Parrying is optional but transformative. Even learning one parry window per boss cuts fight time in half.
5. **Over-leveling Spirit early.** Spirit is powerful, but new players benefit more from HP and stamina. Wait until you're comfortable with combat.
