# Dinoblade FAQ — 15 Common Questions Answered

Welcome to the Dinoblade FAQ. This page answers the most common questions about Team Spino's dinosaur-themed Soulslike, from basics like release date and price to deeper mechanics like the Ex-Mod system and Boss Soul Summoning.

---

## 1. What is Dinoblade?

**Dinoblade is a dinosaur-themed Soulslike action RPG developed by Team Spino LLC.** You play as a dinosaur hunter who wields weapons held in your dinosaur companion's mouth. The game blends traditional Soulslike combat — stamina management, light and heavy attacks, rolling with invincibility frames, and parrying — with a prehistoric setting and unique companion mechanics. You fight through five biomes (Jurassic Plains, Ancient Forest, Volcanic Wastes, Crystal Caverns, and more) against massive creatures and dinosaur-riding enemies.

---

## 2. When is the release date?

**Dinoblade launches July 23, 2026 on Steam (PC).** The game has surpassed 500,000 wishlists on Steam ahead of launch. A free demo is available now for those who want to try the game before buying.

---

## 3. How much does it cost?

| Edition | Price | Contents |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Standard Edition** | $29.99 USD | Base game |
| **Deluxe Edition** | $44.99 USD | Base game + digital art book + original soundtrack + exclusive cosmetic skins (dinosaur companion armor set and weapon skin) |

There are no microtransactions. All in-game items are earned through gameplay. The Deluxe Edition content is bonus material and cosmetics only — nothing gameplay-affecting is locked behind a paywall.

## 4. Is there a demo?

**Yes! A free demo is available on Steam right now.** The demo includes:

- The first biome (Jurassic Plains) — approximately 2-3 hours of content
- The Kira the Exile boss fight and the Alpha Raptor mini-boss
- 3 of the 6 main weapon types (Greatsword, Battle Axe, Claws)
- A level cap of 15
- The Fossil Greatsword secret weapon (fully obtainable in the demo)
- Co-op for up to 2 players

**Save data carries over to the full game.** Everything you earn in the demo — levels, weapons, items, upgrades — transfers to your full-game save on July 23.

## 6. How many bosses are there?

**8 bosses total.**

| Boss Type | Count | Examples |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| **Story bosses** | 6 | Kira the Exile (raptor-like), Axe (ankylosaur with club tail), Kasei the Tyrant (T-Rex final boss), and 3 others |
| **Optional secret bosses** | 2 | The Ancient One (hidden super-boss), plus one more |
| **Mini-bosses** | Several | Alpha Raptor, Vine Guardian, and others |

The 6 story bosses are required to complete the game. The 2 secret bosses are entirely optional and contain some of the best rewards, including the Soul Reaper scythe.

## 7. Is it a Soulslike?

**Yes.** Dinoblade features all the core Soulslike mechanics:

| Mechanic | How It Works in Dinoblade |
|----------|--------------------------|
| **Stamina-based combat** | Attacking, sprinting, and rolling consume stamina. It regenerates slowly when not in use. |
| **Light and heavy attacks** | Light attacks chain up to 4 hits. Heavy attacks can be charged for bonus damage and stagger. |
| **Rolling with i-frames** | 11 invincibility frames (at 60fps) at the start of each roll. |
| **Parrying** | 5 active frames. Successful parries open enemies to critical ripostes. |
| **Bonfire-style save points** | "Roosts" serve as rest points, healing you and respawning enemies. |
| **Risk-reward progression** | Dropped currency ("Fossil Bones") is lost on death unless you retrieve your corpse. |
| **Boss fights** | Large, multi-phase encounters with unique move sets. |

## 9. How does the dinosaur companion work?

Your dinosaur companion is a constant presence throughout the game. It carries weapons in its mouth and fights alongside you.

**Companion mechanics:**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Carrying weapons** | Your companion holds your primary weapon in its mouth. Attacks are performed by the companion striking with the weapon. |
| **Companion stats** | Different dinosaur companions have different stat bonuses (e.g., a Raptor companion gives +2 Dexterity, a Triceratops companion gives +2 Endurance). |
| **Special commands** | You can command your companion with special skills that consume Spirit points. |
| **Companion customization** | Cosmetic skins are available, including the Deluxe Edition armor set for your companion. |
| **Companion switching** | You can change your companion at Roosts. Each companion species has unique idle animations and attack patterns. |

## 10. Is there multiplayer or co-op?

**Yes — up to 2-player co-op is supported.**

| Mode | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Campaign co-op** | Play the entire story with one friend. Progress counts for both players. |
| **Demo co-op** | Available now in the Steam demo. |
| **Player limit** | 2 players maximum (host + 1). |
| **Cross-platform** | No. PC Steam only. |
| **PvP** | Not available at launch. Team Spino has not ruled it out for future updates. |

To invite a friend, open the in-game menu and select "Co-op Session." Your friend can join via Steam invite or by searching for your session by name.

## 11. What is the Ex-Mod system?

**Ex-Mod is Dinoblade's weapon upgrade system.** Throughout the world you find Ex-Mod modifiers that can be applied to weapons at blacksmiths or the secret Magma Forge. Each Ex-Mod changes a weapon's stats and can add special effects.

| Ex-Mod Type | Effect | Rarity |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| **Sharp** | +15% base damage, -10% durability | Common |
| **Heavy** | +25% base damage, +20% stamina cost | Common |
| **Fire** | Adds fire damage (25 flat + 10% scaling) | Uncommon |
| **Ice** | Adds slow debuff on hit | Uncommon |
| **Toxic** | Adds poison buildup | Rare |
| **Lifesteal** | Heals 3% of damage dealt | Rare |
| **Eternal Flame** | Adds fire damage + infinite durability | Legendary (Boss Rush reward) |

Ex-Mods can be removed and reapplied to different weapons, but the removal consumes a rare item (Removal Catalyst).

## 12. How do save points work?

Save points in Dinoblade are called **Roosts**. They function similarly to bonfires in the Dark Souls series:

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Resting** | Restores HP, Estus flasks (refills), and consumables |
| **Enemy respawn** | All non-boss enemies respawn when you rest |
| **Leveling up** | Level up and allocate stats at any Roost |
| **Fast travel** | Unlocked after a certain story point — teleport between discovered Roosts |
| **Companion change** | Switch your dinosaur companion at any Roost |
| **Weapon storage** | Access your item storage box |

Death sends you back to the last Roost you rested at, and you drop your accumulated Fossil Bones (currency). You have one chance to retrieve them by returning to your death spot without dying again.

## 13. Is there New Game+?

**Yes.** New Game+ (NG+) is unlocked after defeating the final boss, Kasei the Tyrant.

| NG+ Feature | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Enemy difficulty** | Enemies have 40% more HP and deal 30% more damage |
| **New enemy placements** | Some areas have different enemy compositions and ambushes |
| **Exclusive rewards** | NG+ drops upgrade materials (Fortified Bones, Ancient Sharpening Stones) not found in the first playthrough |
| **Boss souls** | Boss souls can be exchanged for unique NG+-only weapons and skills |
| **Level cap** | Increased from 50 to 99 |
| **Boss Rush** | Available from the main menu regardless of NG+ cycle |

You can enter NG+ with all your gear, levels, and items from the first playthrough. There is no level squish or gear reset.

## 14. What are the special skills?

Each weapon type has a unique special skill activated by pressing the Special Skill button (R on keyboard, B on Xbox, Circle on PlayStation). Special skills consume Spirit points.

| Weapon | Special Skill | Effect | Spirit Cost |
|--------|--------------|--------|-------------|
| **Greatsword** | Meteor Slice | AoE frontal slash with shockwave | 3 |
| **Battle Axe** | Whirlwind Spin | 360-degree spinning attack, hits multiple times | 2 |
| **War Hammer** | Earth Shatter | Ground pound AoE, knocks down enemies | 3 |
| **Scythe** | Reaping Sweep | Wide horizontal arc, hits all enemies in range | 2 |
| **Spear** | Impaling Charge | Long-range thrust, pierces through enemies | 2 |
| **Claws** | Rending Strike | Rapid multi-hit combo, builds status effects quickly | 1 |

**Universal skills:**

| Skill | Effect | How to Unlock |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Alpha Roar** | Temporarily buffs your damage by 20% for 30 seconds | Story progression |
| **Boss Soul Summon** | Summons a defeated boss for a single powerful attack | Requires a collected boss soul |

## 15. Can I respec my character?

**Yes.** Respeccing is available after a certain story milestone.

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Unlock condition** | Defeat Axe (the ankylosaur boss) in Ancient Forest |
| **Respec location** | Ancient Forest Roost — an NPC called the Memory Keeper appears |
| **Required item** | 1 Memory Shard (consumed on respec) |
| **Memory Shard locations** | Found as rare loot in Volcanic Wastes. Also purchasable from a late-game NPC for 5,000 Fossil Bones. |
| **Respec effect** | Refunds all invested stat points to their base values. You reassign them freely. |
| **Limit per playthrough** | 3 Memory Shards are obtainable per playthrough (more in NG+) |

**Tip:** Save at least one Memory Shard for the endgame. If you want to switch from a Strength build to a Dexterity build for Boss Rush mode, you will need it. Memory Shards carry over into New Game+.
