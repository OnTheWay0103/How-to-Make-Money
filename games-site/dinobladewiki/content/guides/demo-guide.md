# Dinoblade Demo Guide — Content, Progression, and Save Transfer

The Dinoblade free demo on Steam is your first chance to step into the prehistoric world of Team Spino's dinosaur-themed Soulslike. With over 500,000 wishlists on Steam ahead of the July 23, 2026 launch, the demo gives players a generous slice of the full game — and best of all, your progress carries over. This guide covers exactly what is in the demo, how save transfers work, and how to maximize your head start.

---

## What Is in the Demo

The demo covers the opening portion of the game. Here is a full breakdown of available content:

### Playable Area

| Area | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Jurassic Plains** | The first biome in its entirety — lush grasslands, rocky outcrops, river systems, and hidden caves |
| **Approximate playtime** | 2-3 hours for a first playthrough; 1-1.5 hours on replays |
| **Hidden areas accessible** | The Nest — including the Fossil Greatsword secret |
| **Full-game areas locked** | Ancient Forest, Volcanic Wastes, Crystal Caverns |

### Bosses and Enemies

| Encounter | Type | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| **Alpha Raptor** | Mini-boss | Found in the central plains. Teaches parry timing and basic combat. |
| **Kira the Exile** | Main boss | The demo's final encounter. Raptor-like boss with fast combos and a leap attack. |
| **Standard enemies** | Various | Raptors, Compies (swarm enemies), Shield Turtles (armored), Pterosaur flyers |

### Weapons Available

The demo gives you access to 3 of the 6 main weapon types:

| Weapon | Type | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| **Greatsword** | Strength weapon | Slow but hard-hitting. Available from the first weapon cache. |
| **Battle Axe** | Balanced weapon | Medium speed, medium damage. Good for beginners. |
| **Claws** | Dexterity weapon | Fast attacks, low stamina cost. Best for aggressive play. |
| **Fossil Greatsword** | Secret weapon | Hidden in The Nest. 135 base damage, applies slow debuff. Fully obtainable in the demo. |

Weapons not in the demo: War Hammer, Scythe, Spear, and hidden weapons Soul Reaper and Volcanic Hammer.

### Character Progression

| Feature | Demo Limit |
|---------|------------|
| **Level cap** | 15 (roughly 8-10 hours of grinding if you want to max out) |
| **Stat soft caps** | None enforced at level 15 — all stats available |
| **Special skills** | Meteor Slice (Greatsword) unlockable; other skills require weapons not in the demo |
| **Ex-Mod system** | Basic upgrades available from the Jurassic Plains blacksmith; limited to Tier 1 Ex-Mods |
| **Boss Soul Summoning** | Not available in the demo (requires souls from bosses beyond Kira) |

## Save Transfer

Your demo save carries over to the full game when it launches on July 23. Here is everything you need to know.

### What Transfers

| Item | Transfers? | Details |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Character level | Yes | All experience and levels carry over |
| Weapons | Yes | Including the Fossil Greatsword |
| Armor and gear | Yes | All equipment in your inventory |
| Upgrade materials | Yes | Including any Ex-Mod materials farmed in the demo |
| Consumables | Yes | All items in your inventory |
| Lore Tablets | Yes | Progress toward the true ending carries over |
| Roosts unlocked | Yes | All Jurassic Plains Roosts remain unlocked |
| Quest progress | Yes | Completed quests carry over |

### What Does NOT Transfer

- **Demo save deleted and reinstalled:** If you uninstall the demo and reinstall it (or if you clear your local save data), you lose your progress. Back up your save folder before making any changes.
- **Full game and demo installed simultaneously:** The full game reads the demo's save file on first launch. If both are installed, the full game imports the demo data on startup. After import, the demo save is no longer used.
- **Cross-platform:** No. PC Steam save only.

### Save File Location

```
%USERPROFILE%\Documents\Dinoblade\Demo\SaveData\
```

On Steam Deck / Linux:
```
~/.local/share/Dinoblade/Demo/SaveData/
```

**Recommendation:** Manually back up this folder before the full game launches. Steam Cloud sync protects the save, but a local backup is an extra safety net.

## Tips for Demo Players

Maximize your demo time with these strategies:

### 1. Farm Upgrade Materials

The Jurassic Plains are rich in early upgrade materials. Before the full game launches, spend an extra hour or two farming:

- **Sharpening Stones** — dropped by Shield Turtles. Needed for weapon upgrades.
- **Raptor Claws** — dropped by raptor packs. Used for early armor upgrades.
- **Fossil Fragments** — found in The Nest and in caves. Used for Ex-Mod crafting.

Having a stockpile of these when the full game launches lets you upgrade your gear immediately rather than grinding basic materials.

### 2. Get the Fossil Greatsword Early

The Fossil Greatsword hidden in The Nest is fully accessible in the demo. It is one of the best early-game weapons and its Fossilized slow debuff remains useful even against late-game enemies. Getting it in the demo means you start the full game with a powerful weapon already in hand.

### 3. Practice Parrying

The demo's Alpha Raptor mini-boss is the perfect parry practice dummy. Fighting it repeatedly until you can parry its attacks consistently will make the full game dramatically easier. The parry window in Dinoblade is 5 frames — tight, but learnable.

### 4. Level Vitality to 12

Vitality governs your HP pool. Getting it to 12 before investing in other stats gives you enough survivability to learn boss patterns without being one-shot. This is especially valuable against Kira the Exile, whose leap attack can catch new players off guard.

### 5. Read Every Tutorial Message

Dinoblade's tutorial system explains mechanics that differ from other Soulslikes — most importantly the dinosaur companion commands, special skill usage, and Ex-Mod system. Do not skip them. Each tutorial message is replayable from the menu if you need a refresher.

### 6. Experiment With Both Weapon Types

The demo gives you Greatsword, Battle Axe, and Claws. Try all three against the Alpha Raptor to decide which playstyle suits you. Your choice determines your stat priorities for the full game.

| Playstyle | Recommended Weapon | Primary Stat |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Heavy hitter | Greatsword | Strength |
| All-around | Battle Axe | Strength / Dexterity |
| Fast and aggressive | Claws | Dexterity |
| Status effect focused | Fossil Greatsword | Strength |

### 7. Explore Every Corner

The Jurassic Plains has several hidden alcoves and caves that are easy to miss. Beyond the Fossil Greatsword and The Nest, there are:

- **3 Lore Tablets** — collect them for lore and true-ending progress
- **2 armor sets** — basic raptor hide set and the better hunter set
- **An NPC quest giver** — a wounded hunter near the central Roost who starts a side quest that continues into the full game

## After the Demo

Once you have exhausted the demo's content, here is what awaits in the full game on July 23:

- **5 additional biomes/areas** (Ancient Forest, Volcanic Wastes, Crystal Caverns, plus two bonus areas)
- **3 more weapon types** (War Hammer, Scythe, Spear)
- **2 hidden weapons** (Volcanic Hammer, Soul Reaper)
- **5 more bosses** (Axe, Kasei the Tyrant, 2 optional story bosses, 1 secret super-boss)
- **6 additional special skills**
- **Full Ex-Mod system** with Tiers 2 and 3 upgrades
- **Boss Soul Summoning**
- **New Game+**
- **Boss Rush mode** (unlocked after beating the game)
