# Dinoblade Endings Guide — All Endings & How to Unlock

**Launch Version / Updated: July 23, 2026**

Dinoblade features multiple endings determined by choices made throughout the game, items collected, and optional bosses defeated. This guide covers every ending, the exact requirements to unlock each one, and the consequences of your decisions in the final confrontation with Kira, the Exile.

---

## 1. Ending Overview

| Ending | Name | Requirement Difficulty | Time Investment |
|--------|------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Ending A | The Exile's Peace | Easy | Main story only (~5 hours) |
| Ending B | The Empty Throne | Medium | Main story + 1 optional area (~6 hours) |
| Ending C | The True Ending | Hard | Main story + all optional areas + hidden items (~8 hours) |

The game does not explicitly tell you which ending you are working toward. The choice is determined by your actions during the playthrough, culminating in a critical decision during the final boss fight.

---

## 2. Ending A: The Exile's Peace

**Easiest to achieve — simply play through the main story without exploring optional content.**

### Requirements
- Defeat Kira, the Exile in Crescent Canyon
- Do not collect the Purified Aether Crystal from the Abyssal Depths
- Do not interact with all three of Solara's Reflections

### How It Plays Out
You fight Kira through all four phases. At the end, as she lies defeated, she looks at you and says: *"You did not understand. But perhaps that is for the best."*

The scene cuts to the player standing alone in Kira's arena. The camera pulls back to show the ruined world. A voiceover (Kira's voice) says: *"The cycle continues. Another soul bound to Aether. Another exile walking the wasteland."*

The player character walks toward the horizon. The Aether crystals in the background pulse faintly — the instability continues.

### Consequences
- The World Core remains damaged
- Aether continues to poison the land
- The cycle will repeat — another amnesiac wanderer will awaken in the future
- Credits roll with no post-credits scene

**Why you might choose this ending:** It is the simplest to achieve and provides narrative closure. The story works as a straightforward tragedy.

---

## 3. Ending B: The Empty Throne

**Requires exploration beyond the main path but skips the deepest secrets.**

### Requirements
- Collect the Purified Aether Crystal from the Abyssal Depths
- Or: Interact with all three of Solara's Reflections
- Defeat Kira, the Exile

You need at least **one** of the two prerequisites. Having both unlocks nothing extra — only ending C requires both.

### Purified Aether Crystal Location
Found in the **Abyssal Depths**, an optional endgame area accessible after defeating the Canyon Guardian. The entrance is a glowing fissure in the wall of the collapsed bridge area in Crescent Canyon.

The crystal is in a chest behind a sealed door that requires defeating **Thorn, the Warden** (an optional boss with 6,000 HP, weak to fire). See the [Boss Guide](/guides/boss-guide) for Thorn's attack patterns.

### Solara's Reflection Locations
Three locations, each requiring some exploration:
1. **Jurassic Plains** — Behind the waterfall near the first campfire (the one with the Ancient Guardian Boots)
2. **Crescent Canyon** — On a broken pillar overlooking the canyon (requires a precise jump from the upper platform)
3. **Abyssal Depths** — In a hidden alcove behind Thorn's arena

### How It Plays Out
During the fight with Kira, an additional dialogue option appears: **"I found the crystal."** or **"I spoke with Solara."**

Kira pauses. She recognizes the crystal or Solara's message. She says: *"You know. Then you understand why I must die. Strike true."*

After defeating her, the player places the crystal — or Solara's message — at the center of the arena. The Aether energy stabilizes momentarily. The screen fades to white.

**Ending cutscene:** The player character sits on Kira's throne, looking over the valley. The Aether crystals have stopped pulsing. Life is returning — grass grows, flowers bloom. But the player character is alone.

**Voiceover:** *"The wound is closed. But the healer has no one left to heal."*

### Consequences
- The Aether instability is temporarily stabilized
- The world begins to heal
- The player character stays in Kira's arena, alone
- Post-credits scene: A small dinosaur approaches the player — the first sign of natural life returning

## 4. Ending C: The True Ending

**The most difficult and narratively complete ending. Requires full exploration and all hidden interactions.**

### Requirements (ALL of the following)
1. **Collect the Purified Aether Crystal** from the Abyssal Depths
2. **Interact with all three of Solara's Reflections**
3. **Obtain the World Core Fragment** — a hidden item found only after completing both of the above
4. **Learn the Forbidden Knowledge** — from a hidden book in the Abyssal Depths
5. **Choose the correct dialogue option** during the Kira fight

### World Core Fragment Location
The World Core Fragment only spawns after you have both the Purified Aether Crystal and have seen all three Solara Reflections. Return to the **Abyssal Depths** sealed sanctum (Thorn's arena). A new passage has opened — a narrow crawlspace behind Thorn's throne.

The Fragment is on a pedestal, glowing with golden light. Interacting with it triggers a cutscene: Solara's ghost appears and says: *"This is the last piece. The Core can be remade, but the sacrifice must be willing."*

### Forbidden Knowledge
In the Abyssal Depths library (north wing, third bookshelf from the entrance), there is a book titled *"On the Nature of the World Core."* Reading it unlocks the knowledge that the World Core was a living being, not a machine, and that restoring it requires **a willing soul to merge with it**.

You must read this book for the True Ending dialogue option to appear.

### The Final Choice
During the Kira fight, after reducing her to approximately 10% HP, she will stop attacking and speak to you. At this point, a new dialogue option appears:

> **"I know what the World Core needs. I will do it."**

Selecting this triggers the True Ending path.

### How It Plays Out
Kira stands down. She says: *"You are the first to understand. I could not do it alone — the Council was right to fear me. But together..."*

She hands you the **Exile's Aether Shard** — the piece of Aether energy she had been channeling. Combined with the Purified Aether Crystal and the World Core Fragment, it completes the Aether restoration set.

**Extended cutscene:**
1. The three items merge in the player's hands, forming a new, stable World Core
2. The sky clears. The Aether poisoning in the air evaporates
3. All Fallen Warriors across the world collapse — the Aether leaves their bodies, and they return to human form
4. The Spirit Dinosaurs fade peacefully, their souls released
5. Kira, weakened from giving up her Aether Shard, becomes mortal
6. The player character and Kira stand together as the world heals

**Final voiceover:** *"The cycle is broken. Not by power — but by understanding."*

**Post-credits scene:** The player character and Kira walk through the Jurassic Plains. Flowers bloom. Small dinosaurs approach without aggression. The camera pulls back to show the world in full color for the first time.

### Achievements/Trophies
- Ending A: "The Exile's Peace"
- Ending B: "The Empty Throne"
- Ending C: "The Cycle Broken" (True Ending)

## 5. Decision Tree

```
Start Game
├── Play through main story only
│   └── Defeat Kira → Ending A
├── Explore optional areas
│   ├── Find Purified Aether Crystal OR Solara's Reflections
│   │   └── Defeat Kira → Ending B
│   └── Find ALL three items + World Core Fragment + Forbidden Knowledge
│       └── Defeat Kira → Choose correct dialogue → Ending C
```

### Can You Lock Yourself Out?

**Yes, you can lock yourself out of higher endings.**

- **Ending C lock:** If you defeat Thorn, the Warden and leave the Abyssal Depths without reading the book *"On the Nature of the World Core,"* you cannot return to read it. Thorn's arena is only accessible once. If you miss the book, Ending C becomes inaccessible in that playthrough.
- **Ending B lock:** Solara's Reflections can only be interacted with before defeating Kira. After the final boss, the reflections fade. Miss them, and you cannot access Ending B.

**Recommendation:** Save your game before entering the Abyssal Depths and before starting the Kira fight. This gives you fallback points if you miss a requirement.

## 6. New Game+ Considerations

Dinoblade offers New Game+ after completing any ending:

- **You keep:** All stats, all gear upgrades, all unlocked campfires
- **You lose:** Key items (crystals, fragments, reflections) — they must be collected again
- **Ending C reward:** If you achieved Ending C in your first playthrough, your New Game+ file starts with a unique item — **Kira's Journal** — which provides additional lore entries and dialogue options with NPCs.

The True Ending is generally easier to achieve on New Game+ because you retain your high stats and gear, making the optional bosses less punishing.
