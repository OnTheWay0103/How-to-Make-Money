# Tears of Metal Weapon Upgrade Guide — Merge System, AP, and Priority Paths

> **Applies to Early Access v1.0 / Updated: July 23, 2026**

Weapon progression in Tears of Metal revolves around two systems: the **Weapon Merge** system (combining duplicates for higher tiers) and the **Ability Point (AP)** system (spending points on weapon skill trees). Both are gated by the Smithy, and both have traps that can waste a run's worth of resources if you do not plan ahead.

---

## The Weapon Merge System

The Weapon Merge system lets you combine two identical weapons to create a stronger version. This is how you push a basic weapon into endgame territory.

### Merge Tiers

| Tier | Combine | Result | Stat Bonus |
|------|---------|--------|------------|
| Base | Default drop | Base | 100% baseline |
| Reinforced+1 | Base + Base | Reinforced+1 | +25% damage, +10% attack speed |
| Reinforced+2 | Reinforced+1 + Reinforced+1 | Reinforced+2 | +50% damage, +20% attack speed |
| Reinforced+3 | Reinforced+2 + Reinforced+2 | Reinforced+3 | +80% damage, +30% attack speed |

### Merge Rules

- **Must be identical weapons** — Two Iron Greatswords merge. An Iron Greatsword + Steel Greatsword does not work
- **Rarity does not matter for merge** — A Common Iron Greatsword + Uncommon Iron Greatsword = Reinforced+1 Iron Greatsword
- **Merge cannot combine different tiers** — Reinforced+1 + Base = does not work. Both must be the same tier
- **Smithy required** — Merging requires at least Smithy Level 1

### The Exponential Problem

Each merge tier requires **double** the number of base weapons:

| Target Tier | Base Weapons Needed |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Reinforced+1 | 2 |
| Reinforced+2 | 4 |
| Reinforced+3 | 8 |
| Reinforced+4 (if added later) | 16 |

This means you should **specialize early**. If you try to upgrade two different weapons, you will run out of duplicates. Pick your endgame weapon by Act 2 and merge every duplicate you find into it.

## Ability Points (AP) System

Each weapon has a skill tree that costs AP to unlock. AP is earned through combat and is specific to each run — it does not carry over between runs.

### The AP Trap Explained

The AP system's biggest trap is **partial investment**. Weapon skill trees have branches that require multiple points to reach the useful node at the end. Spending 1-2 points on a branch you cannot finish is wasted AP.

**Example of the trap**:

Imagine a skill tree branch:
- Node 1: +10% damage (cost: 1 AP) — decent
- Node 2: +15% damage (cost: 2 AP) — better
- Node 3: +50% damage on heavy attacks (cost: 3 AP) — the payoff

If you spend 3 AP on Nodes 1 and 2 but cannot afford Node 3 (6 AP total), you wasted the path bonus. You would have been better off putting those 3 AP into a different branch with an earlier payoff.

### AP Spending Rules

- **Always calculate the full branch cost before spending**
- **Save AP until you can unlock at least two nodes in a branch** — Partial investment rarely pays off
- **Prioritize branches that end with a unique effect** over flat stat bonuses
- **Rush the capstone node** of your preferred branch before spending on secondary branches

### Best AP Investments by Hero

| Hero | Branch 1 (Rush) | Branch 2 (Secondary) | Branch 3 (Optional) |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Vanguard | Heavy Attack Mastery (capstone: 360 cleave) | Block Efficiency (capstone: parry = free heavy) | Stamina Recovery |
| Berserker | Dual Wield Expert (capstone: dual strikes apply 2 emblem procs) | Attack Speed (capstone: +40% atk speed for 5s after kill) | Crit Chance |
| Marksman | Precision Shot (capstone: headshots deal 3x emblem damage) | Reload Speed (capstone: automatic reload on emblem proc) | Piercing Shot |

## Upgrade Priority Path

### Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Act 1 — Find your primary weapon**
   - Focus on finding one weapon type and stick with it
   - Do not switch weapons mid-run unless the new one is significantly better
   - Start merging at any Smithy station (Smithy Level 1 required)

2. **Act 1 Boss — First merge opportunity**
   - By the Ironclad Commander, you should have 2+ duplicates of your weapon
   - Merge to Reinforced+1 before the fight
   - The damage boost makes a significant difference in phase 2

3. **Act 2 — Push to Reinforced+2**
   - The Mining Complex has more loot containers than Act 1
   - Full clear every zone for weapon drops
   - Aim for Reinforced+2 before the Excavation Juggernaut

4. **Act 2 Boss — Evaluate your weapon**
   - At Reinforced+2, your weapon is strong enough for Act 3
   - If you have enough duplicates (8 total), push for Reinforced+3
   - Otherwise, save duplicates and use Reinforced+2

5. **Act 3 — Endgame weapon**
   - Reinforced+3 should be your goal before the Corrupted Warlord
   - The final boss has high HP — every bit of damage matters

## How the Smithy Affects Weapon Quality

The Smithy is not just for merging. Higher Smithy levels unlock additional weapon services:

| Smithy Level | Service | Cost | Effect |
|-------------|---------|------|--------|
| 1 | Weapon Merge | Free (per merge) | Combine base weapons |
| 2 | Weapon Reroll | 200 gold | Change weapon's secondary stat |
| 2 | Reinforced+2 Merge | Unlocked | Higher tier merging |
| 3 | Stat Reroll | 500 gold | Reroll all stats on a weapon |
| 3 | Reinforced+3 Merge | Unlocked | Maximum tier merging |

### When to Reroll

- **Weapon Reroll (Level 2)** — Use when your weapon has a stat that does not match your build. For example, a Vanguard weapon with "+10% attack speed" is less valuable than "+10% damage reduction"
- **Stat Reroll (Level 3)** — Use sparingly. 500 gold is significant. Only reroll your endgame weapon (Reinforced+2 or higher)

## Farming Upgrade Materials

| Material | Source | Best Farm |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Weapon duplicates | Enemy drops, chests | Act 1 full clear for common weapons |
| Gold | Chests, selling artifacts | Act 1 speedrun (20 min) |
| Smithy unlock materials | Dragon Stone Shards | Act 1 Boss farming |
| Reroll gold | Selling unused artifacts | Any act |

### Best Farming Route for Weapons

1. Equip Gold Find emblem
2. Run Act 1 full clear
3. Break every crate and barrel (high weapon drop rate from destructibles)
4. Kill all Shield Bearer packs (best source of Iron Greatsword)
5. Sell all artifacts you do not need
6. Clear Act 1 Boss for Dragon Stone Shard
7. Repeat until you have 8 duplicates of your target weapon
