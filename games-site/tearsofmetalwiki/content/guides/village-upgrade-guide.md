# Tears of Metal Village Upgrade Guide — Priority Order and Costs

> **Applies to Early Access v1.0 / Updated: July 23, 2026**

The Village Hub is your permanent progression system in Tears of Metal. Every gold coin and Dragon Stone Shard spent here carries forward to every future run. This guide breaks down the five buildings, their upgrade costs, and the optimal priority order so you never waste resources.

---

## Upgrade Priority Summary

**Optimal order: Armory -> Recruitment Tent -> Smithy -> Apothecary -> Mystic Altar**

This order maximizes your power-per-gold ratio. Armory first because artifact storage is the single most impactful upgrade. Recruitment Tent second because better soldiers make every run easier. Smithy third because weapon merging requires it. Apothecary and Mystic Altar are last because they help in specific situations but do not offer universal power gains.

---

## Building 1: Armory

**Purpose**: Stores artifacts and emblems between runs so you can carry them into new expeditions.

The Armory is the most important building in the Village. Without it, every run starts with zero artifacts. With it, you can carry 2-3 powerful artifacts into every run, dramatically increasing consistency.

### Upgrade Tiers

| Level | Cost | Slots | Unlock | Priority |
|-------|------|-------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Free (starting) | 1 slot | Starting equipment | — |
| 2 | 500 gold | 2 slots | Inter-run artifact carry | **Do first** |
| 3 | 1,200 gold + 3 Dragon Stone Shards | 3 slots | Rare artifact equipping | **Do second** |
| 4 | 3,000 gold + 8 Dragon Stone Shards | 4 slots | Legendary artifact equipping | Later |

### Why Level 2 is Mandatory

At Level 2, the Armory unlocks the ability to carry artifacts between runs. This is the biggest power spike in the entire village upgrade tree. Before this upgrade, you lose all artifacts when you die or complete a run. After it, you can bank at least two strong artifacts and start every expedition with a known advantage.

### When to Upgrade

- **Level 2**: As soon as you have 500 gold. Sell unused artifacts if necessary.
- **Level 3**: After Recruitment Tent Level 2. 1,200 gold is significant, so prioritize soldier quality first.

## Building 2: Recruitment Tent

**Purpose**: Recruits and upgrades battalion soldiers for each expedition. Higher levels mean better starting soldiers and more recruitment options.

### Upgrade Tiers

| Level | Cost | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| 1 | Free | Basic recruits (Pikemen, Grunts) |
| 2 | 400 gold | Unlocks Shield Bearer recruits, +1 starting soldier |
| 3 | 1,000 gold + 2 Dragon Stone Shards | Unlocks elite recruits (Blood-Steel prospects), +2 starting soldiers |
| 4 | 2,500 gold + 5 Dragon Stone Shards | Guaranteed veteran soldier each run |

### Why Prioritize This After Armory

Soldiers with permanent skills are your most valuable units. A single veteran Shield Bearer can tank an entire boss phase while you deal damage. The Recruitment Tent determines the quality of soldiers available at the start of each run. Level 2 is cheap at 400 gold and immediately improves your survival rate.

### Tips
- Recruit every run, even if you do not plan to use the soldier — having extra bodies for chokepoints never hurts
- Check the "Veteran" tag when recruiting — veterans have pre-existing skills that survived previous runs
- Do not send veterans on suicide missions — their skills are irreplaceable

## Building 3: Smithy

**Purpose**: Unlocks the weapon merge system, improves weapon quality, and allows you to craft higher-tier weapons.

### Upgrade Tiers

| Level | Cost | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| 1 | 300 gold | Unlocks weapon merging (Base+Base=Reinforced+1) |
| 2 | 800 gold + 2 Dragon Stone Shards | Unlocks Reinforced+2 merges, weapon reroll |
| 3 | 2,000 gold + 5 Dragon Stone Shards | Unlocks Reinforced+3 merges, stat reroll |

### Why Smithy is Third

Weapon merging is powerful, but it only helps if you have good weapons to merge. In early runs, you are unlikely to find duplicate weapons of the same type. Focus on the Armory and Recruitment Tent first — by the time you reach Act 2, you will have found enough duplicates to make Smithy worthwhile.

## Building 4: Apothecary

**Purpose**: Crafts potions and consumables — healing, stamina recovery, buff potions, and corruption cures.

### Upgrade Tiers

| Level | Cost | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| 1 | 200 gold | Unlocks basic healing potions |
| 2 | 600 gold + 1 Dragon Stone Shard | Unlocks stamina potions, corruption cure |
| 3 | 1,500 gold + 4 Dragon Stone Shards | Unlocks buff potions (damage, defense, speed) |

### Why Apothecary is Low Priority

Potions are consumables. Every gold spent on a potion is gold not spent on a permanent upgrade. The Apothecary is valuable for Act 3 (Corruption cure is almost mandatory), but you do not need it for Acts 1 and 2. Leave this until after Armory Level 3 and Recruitment Tent Level 3.

### What to Craft

| Potion | Cost | Use |
|--------|------|-----|
| Basic Healing | 50 gold | Emergency health recovery |
| Corruption Cure | 100 gold | **Mandatory for Act 3** |
| Damage Potion | 150 gold | Boss fights |
| Stamina Potion | 75 gold | Extended fights |

## Building 5: Mystic Altar

**Purpose**: Unlocks new emblems, upgrades emblem rarity, and reveals emblem synergy combinations.

### Upgrade Tiers

| Level | Cost | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| 1 | 500 gold | Unlocks 3 basic emblems |
| 2 | 1,500 gold + 3 Dragon Stone Shards | Unlocks 3 advanced emblems |
| 3 | 3,500 gold + 10 Dragon Stone Shards | Unlocks all remaining emblems, synergy preview |

### Why Mystic Altar is Last

The Mystic Altar is expensive and its benefits are conditional. Unless you are specifically building around a particular emblem synergy, the early emblems found during runs are sufficient. The Altar becomes important in the lategame when you need specific emblem combinations for Act 3, but it should not be your early priority.

## How to Farm Gold and Dragon Stone Shards

### Gold Farming

- **Full clear Act 1** — Every chest, breakable object, and optional area contains gold. Do not rush the boss.
- **Sell unused artifacts** — Common artifacts you do not need sell for 25-50 gold each. This adds up quickly.
- **Complete side objectives** — Each act has 2-3 optional objectives that reward bonus gold
- **Co-op bonus** — Playing in co-op increases gold drops by approximately 20%

### Dragon Stone Shard Farming

Dragon Stone Shards are the rare currency in Tears of Metal. They are required for higher-tier upgrades.

| Source | Shards | Reliability |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Act 1 Boss (Ironclad Commander) | 1 guaranteed | High |
| Act 2 Boss (Excavation Juggernaut) | 2 guaranteed | High |
| Act 3 Boss (Corrupted Warlord) | 3 guaranteed | High |
| Elite enemy drops | 0-1 (random) | Low |
| Hidden chests | 0-2 (rare) | Very Low |
| Daily challenge reward | 1 (once per day) | Guaranteed |

**Best farm strategy**: Run Act 1 boss repeatedly. The Ironclad Commander is the easiest boss and guarantees 1 Dragon Stone Shard per kill. A full Act 1 run takes approximately 20-25 minutes once you know the route.
