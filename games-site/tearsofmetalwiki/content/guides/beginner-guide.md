# Tears of Metal Beginner Guide — First Expedition Tips

> **Applies to Early Access v1.0 / Updated: July 23, 2026**

Starting your first expedition in Tears of Metal can feel overwhelming. Three heroes, a village with five upgradeable buildings, 100+ artifacts, and a permadeath battalion system — there is a lot to learn. This guide covers exactly what to do in your first few runs so you build good habits from the start.

---

## Choosing Your First Hero

Your first hero choice matters because early progression is about learning mechanics, not optimizing DPS. Here is how the three options stack up for a new player:

| Hero | Difficulty | Best For | Recommendation |
|------|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Vanguard | Easy | Learning combat fundamentals | **Best for first run** |
| Berserker | Medium | Aggressive playstyle | Adept+ players |
| Marksman | Hard | Ranged preference | Co-op only |

### Why Vanguard is the Best Starter

The **Vanguard** wields a massive claymore and has the highest base health and armor of all three heroes. Their kit includes a block/parry mechanic and a crowd control slam that creates space when you get surrounded. This gives you room to learn enemy attack patterns without dying to a single mistake.

If you prefer ranged combat, save **Marksman** for co-op runs — it struggles solo because enemies close distance faster than you can kite. **Berserker** is excellent once you understand positioning but its low durability punishes new players harshly.

## Combat Basics: Block, Parry, and Dodge

Tears of Metal uses a three-layer defensive system. Mastering it is the difference between dying in Act 1 and reaching the final boss.

### Block
- **How**: Hold the block button with your weapon raised
- **Effect**: Reduces incoming damage by 60%, costs stamina
- **Use when**: You cannot avoid an attack, or you need to hold a position
- **Limitation**: Does not work against unblockable attacks (red-glowing strikes)

### Parry
- **How**: Tap block just before an enemy's attack lands
- **Effect**: Negates all damage, staggers the enemy, opens them to a critical hit
- **Use when**: Fighting humanoid enemies with telegraphed swings
- **Risk**: Mistimed parries leave you staggered instead

### Dodge Roll
- **How**: Tap dodge in any direction
- **Effect**: Brief invincibility frames (i-frames), costs stamina
- **Use when**: Evading AoE attacks, repositioning, escaping surrounds
- **Tip**: You can chain two rolls before stamina recovery pauses

**Pro tip**: Against bosses, dodge roll is safer than parry. Against regular enemies (Pikemen, Grunts), parry is faster and lets you kill them before reinforcements arrive.

## Understanding the Village Hub

Between expeditions, the Village Hub is where you spend your spoils. There are five buildings, each with a specific purpose:

| Building | Purpose | Priority |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Armory | Stores artifacts and emblems between runs | **Highest** |
| Recruitment Tent | Recruits and upgrades battalion soldiers | High |
| Smithy | Upgrades weapon quality and unlocks merges | Medium |
| Apothecary | Crafting potions and consumables | Low early |
| Mystic Altar | Emblem unlocking and synergies | Low early |

Your first gold and Dragon Stone Shards should go into the **Armory upgrade**. This unlocks artifact storage slots, letting you carry powerful items between runs. Without it, every run starts from scratch.

## First Expedition Tips

### What to Do in Your First Run

1. **Explore thoroughly** — Every chest, breakable barrel, and side path in Act 1 contains potential artifacts or gold. Do not rush to the boss.
2. **Pick up every weapon** — Even weapons you do not plan to use can be merged later. The Weapon Merge system is how you create endgame gear.
3. **Recruit at least one soldier** — Your starting battalion is small. Use the recruitment banners you find to add soldiers. Even a single extra Pikeman helps.
4. **Test different emblems** — Your first run gives you a random emblem drop. Try it. Learn what elements do. Lightning stuns, Freeze slows, Fire burns over time.
5. **Do not fear death** — Your first few runs are for learning, not winning. Every run teaches you something about enemy patterns, artifact synergies, or emblem timing.

### What to Avoid

- **Ignoring your battalion** — Your soldiers deal significant damage and can draw aggro. Use them.
- **Hoarding artifacts** — Equip artifacts immediately. A small bonus now is better than a perfect build you never achieve.
- **Charging into groups** — Tears of Metal punishes recklessness. Pull enemies with a crossbow shot or let your battalion engage first.
- **Neglecting Threat Level** — The Threat Level meter in the top-right corner tells you how dangerous the area has become. When it fills, harder enemies spawn. Plan your route accordingly.

## How Permadeath Works for Your Battalion

Your battalion soldiers are not expendable. Each soldier has a name, a rank, and potentially valuable skills that took multiple runs to unlock. When a soldier dies in combat, they are **gone permanently**. Their skills, upgrades, and experience are lost.

**Veteran soldiers** — ones that have survived multiple expeditions — are extremely valuable. By Act 2, a veteran Pikeman with +3 damage and a health bonus outperforms a fresh recruit by a wide margin. Protect them. If a veteran is low health, send them to the back line or use the "Retreat" command.

## What to Upgrade First

Here is a quick priority list for your first 5-10 hours:

1. **Armory (Level 2)** — 2 artifact slots -> unlocks carrying artifacts between runs
2. **Recruitment Tent (Level 2)** — Better soldier quality at start of each run
3. **Smithy (Level 1)** — Unlocks weapon merging
4. **Apothecary (Level 1)** — Basic healing potions
5. **Armory (Level 3)** — 3 artifact slots for build consistency

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Spending Ability Points Without Planning
The AP (Ability Point) system in the weapon tree is a trap if you do not plan ahead. Always calculate the full branch cost before committing points. A single mistake can cost you an entire run's worth of AP.

### 2. Ignoring Corruption Resist for Act 3
Act 3 introduces Corruption zones that drain your health rapidly. If you reach Act 3 without any Corruption Resist emblems or artifacts, you will struggle. Plan for this from Act 1.

### 3. Using Single-Bar Battalion Commands at the Wrong Time
Single-bar commands use one resource bar but trigger instantly. Multi-bar commands are stronger but leave you vulnerable during the wind-up. Against fast enemies, single-bar is safer. Against bosses, multi-bar is worth the risk.

### 4. Not Saving Gold for Village Upgrades
It is tempting to spend all gold on consumables before a run. Do not. Village upgrades are permanent. Every gold coin spent on a one-time potion could be a permanent stat boost from the Armory.

## Quick Tips

- **Artifact synergy beats rarity** — A common artifact that synergizes with your build outperforms a legendary that does not.
- **Buy the Haggis recipe** — Found in Act 1, this consumable gives a permanent HP buff for the run. Worth every penny.
- **Talk to every NPC** — Some companions are unlocked through hidden dialogue options, not combat.
- **Two emblems are better than one** — Equipping two emblems of the same type doubles the trigger chance. Equipping different types unlocks synergies. Experiment.
