# Tears of Metal Co-op Guide — Team Composition and Strategy

> **Applies to Early Access v1.0 / Updated: July 23, 2026**

Tears of Metal supports up to 4-player co-op, and the game changes significantly when you bring a team. Enemies scale with player count, but so do rewards. A well-coordinated team can clear content that would be impossible solo. This guide covers team composition, role distribution, and the communication habits that separate wipe parties from victory screens.

---

## Best Team Compositions

### The Standard Meta (Most Consistent)

| Role | Hero | Emblems | Primary Job |
|------|------|---------|-------------|
| Tank / Leader | Vanguard | Physical + Armor | Hold aggro, control chokepoints |
| Melee DPS | Berserker | Fire + Attack Speed | Boss damage, elite elimination |
| Melee DPS | Berserker | Lightning + Crit | Crowd clear, spawn duty |
| Ranged Support | Marksman | Freeze + Lightning | Crowd control, conduit destruction |

**Why this works**: The Vanguard holds the line while two Berserkers burn down priority targets. The Marksman controls approach lanes with Freeze and handles objectives that require ranged attacks.

### The Double Vanguard (Safe but Slow)

| Role | Hero |
|------|------|
| Main Tank | Vanguard (Physical + Armor) |
| Off-Tank | Vanguard (Physical + Regen) |
| Melee DPS | Berserker (Fire + Crit) |
| Ranged DPS | Marksman (Lightning + Freeze) |

**Best for**: First-time Act 3 attempts where survival matters more than speed.

### The Speedrun Comp (High Risk, High Reward)

| Role | Hero |
|------|------|
| Aggro Holder | Vanguard (Physical + Crit) |
| Glass Cannon | Berserker (Fire + Crit) |
| Glass Cannon | Berserker (Fire + Attack Speed) |
| Glass Cannon | Marksman (Lightning + Crit) |

**Best for**: Experienced teams farming materials. Everyone runs damage. Do not attempt for first clears.

### The Beginner Friendly Comp

| Role | Hero |

## Role Distribution and Responsibilities

### Vanguard (Team Leader)
- **Call the shots** — The Vanguard should be the team's primary shot-caller. You control engagement timing.
- **Hold chokepoints** — Position in doorways and narrow corridors where your wide swings hit everything.
- **Mark targets** — Use the ping system to highlight priority enemies (elites, spotters, banner bearers).
- **Watch the team's back** — In co-op, enemies often spawn behind the group. The Vanguard is tanky enough to peel.

### Berserker (Damage Dealer)
- **Focus the marked target** — The Vanguard marks, the Berserker kills. Do not chase random enemies.
- **Flank during boss fights** — Position behind the boss to avoid cleave attacks aimed at the Vanguard.
- **Handle spawns** — When the Marksman calls out a spawn, be the first to react.
- **Do not overextend** — The Berserker is the squishiest hero. Do not chase enemies into unknown rooms.

### Marksman (Support / Objective)
- **Cover the approach** — Shoot approaching enemies before they reach the Vanguard. This reduces pressure on the frontline.
- **Destroy ranged threats** — Trebuchet Spotters, Overseers, and other ranged enemies are your primary targets.
- **Handle conduits** — In boss fights, you are the fastest conduit destroyer. Prioritize this.
- **Freeze priority targets** — When an elite or boss enemy telegraphs a dangerous attack, land a Freeze proc to interrupt it.

## Communication Tips

### Essential Ping Uses
- **Ping enemy** (default: middle mouse) — Highlight a priority target
- **Ping location** (double-tap ping) — "Go here" or "danger here"
- **Ping item** (look at item + ping) — "Pick this up" or "I need this"

### Callouts to Use

| Situation | Callout | Response |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Elite spotted | "Elite [direction]" | Everyone stops, focus fire |
| Reinforcements incoming | "Reinforcements [direction]" | Berserker peels off, handles spawn |
| Low on stamina | "Stamina low, need 5 sec" | Vanguard holds aggro, team backs off |
| Boss telegraph | "[Attack name] incoming" | Everyone dodge or block |
| Shield up (Act 3 boss) | "Conduits up, switch!" | Everyone destroys conduits immediately |

### Pre-Run Checklist

Before entering the expedition, confirm with your team:
1. Everyone has Corruption Resist for Act 3? (If no, reset planning)
2. Emblem preferences — avoid doubling roles (two Fire build Berserkers are fine; two tank Vanguards are not)
3. Who leads (shot-caller) — usually the Vanguard
4. Ammo check — Marksman needs to know where ammo drops are

## Shared Progression Mechanics

### How Progression Works in Co-op

| Mechanic | Shared? | Details |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Gold earned | Yes — each player gets full gold drops | Everyone earns independently |
| Artifact drops | No — each player sees their own drops | No fighting over loot |
| Dragon Stone Shards | Yes — each player gets their own shards | Boss kills grant shards to all |
| Village upgrades | No — each player upgrades their own village | Progression is individual |
| Battalion soldiers | Partially — each player has their own battalion | Reinforcements are individual |

### The Host Advantage

The player who hosts the session gets slightly more control:
- **Threat Level** visibility is shared, but host controls when to advance
- **Rest at bonfires** requires host confirmation
- **Village access** between runs is per-player, but host can kick/pause

## How Battalion Works in Multiplayer

Each player commands their own battalion of soldiers. Battalions do not share units — your Pikeman is yours, and your teammate's Shield Bearer is theirs.

### Multiplayer Battalion Tactics

- **Combine chokepoints** — Two battalions in the same corridor create an impassable wall. Use this for boss fights.
- **Stagger commands** — Do not use multi-bar commands simultaneously. One player using a multi-bar while others cover creates more value than everyone shouting at once.
- **One player manages spawns** — If the Vanguard holds the frontline, the Marksman's battalion handles incoming spawns from behind.
- **Veteran protection** — In co-op, veterans are even more valuable because the team covers each other. If a veteran is low, call for cover.

## Boss Fight Coordination

### General Boss Rules for Co-op

1. **Vanguard gets first hit** — Let the Vanguard engage first to establish aggro
2. **Position in a half-circle** — Vanguard at center, Berserkers flanking, Marksman at range
3. **Rez priority** — Vanguard > Berserker > Marksman (tank rezzed first to restore aggro control)
4. **Stagger coordination** — Count down to 3 for synchronized heavy attacks to stagger bosses
5. **Save multi-bar commands for stun windows** — Stunned boss + 4 multi-bar commands = massive burst

### Recommended Team for Each Act

| Act | Team Comp | Key Strategy |
|-----|-----------|--------------|
| Act 1 (Ironclad Commander) | Any balanced comp | Marksman interrupts reinforcement horn from range |
| Act 2 (Excavation Juggernaut) | 1 Vanguard + 2 Berserker + 1 Marksman | Vanguard holds near pillars; Marksman calls pillar destruction |
| Act 3 (Corrupted Warlord) | 1 Vanguard + 2 Berserker + 1 Marksman | Dedicated spawn killer; conduit rush on shield |

## Co-op Etiquette Tips

| Do | Do Not |
|----|--------|
| Ask what emblem/artifact the team is running | Join without checking party composition |
| Share your extra potions if a teammate is struggling | Hoard resources your teammate needs |
| Wait for the team before engaging bosses | Rush ahead and trigger encounters solo |
| Ping items and enemies | Mark everything (ping fatigue is real) |
| Stay in voice chat or text chat | Play silently — coordination matters |

## Advanced Co-op Synergies

### Emblem Stacking in Co-op

When multiple players use the same emblem, effects stack multiplicatively:
- **Two Fire emblem players** = burn stacks apply twice as fast (reaching 10 stacks in half the time)
- **Two Freeze emblem players** = enemies freeze at 3 stacks instead of 5 (60% faster freeze)
- **Two Lightning emblem players** = chain damage jumps to 4 targets (double the normal range)

### Artifact Auras

Some artifacts provide **team-wide auras** that affect all players within 15 meters:
- **Warlord's Banner** (Legendary): +15% damage to all nearby allies
- **Guardian's Ward** (Legendary): +10% damage reduction to all nearby allies
- **Piper's Tune** (Rare): +20% stamina recovery to all nearby allies

These are the most valuable artifacts in co-op because they multiply the entire team's power. If you find one, equip it regardless of your personal build.
