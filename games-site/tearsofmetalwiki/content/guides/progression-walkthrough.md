# Tears of Metal Progression Walkthrough — Act-by-Act Guide

> **Applies to Early Access v1.0 / Updated: July 23, 2026**

This walkthrough covers every act, enemy type, objective, and boss in Tears of Metal. Follow this guide for optimal routing, resource management, and survival strategies. Each section includes enemy behavior notes, loot locations, and the specific tactics that make each act manageable.

---

## Act 1: Scottish Coast

The Scottish Coast is your introduction to Tears of Metal. Rolling green hills, ruined villages, and the remnants of an ancient fortification set the scene. The Threat Level here starts low and rises gradually, giving you room to learn mechanics.

### Recommended Preparation

| Hero | Recommended | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Vanguard | Best first pick | The coastal chokepoints favor wide swings |
| Berserker | Viable | Watch your positioning near cliff edges |
| Marksman | Difficult solo | Tight corridors negate range advantage |

**Emblems**: Physical Damage + Critical Strike (simple, effective)
**Armory**: At least 1 artifact slot (Level 2 recommended but not mandatory)

### Enemy Types

| Enemy | Behavior | Counter | Threat Level |
|-------|----------|---------|--------------|
| Grunt | Charges directly, slow attack | Parry or block, then punish | Low |
| Pikeman | Keeps distance, thrust attack | Side-dodge, attack during recovery | Low |
| Shield Bearer | Blocks front, bash attack | Wait for bash, dodge sideways, hit | Medium |
| Trebuchet Spotter | Ranged, marks target for bonus damage | **Priority target** — close distance or use ranged attack | High (as support) |
| Banner Bearer | Roaming, buffs nearby enemies (attack +20%) | **Priority target** — kill before engaging his squad | Medium |

### Objective Priority

1. **Kill Banner Bearers first** — They are marked by a glowing banner. Any enemy near a Banner Bearer deals 20% more damage. Clear them before engaging groups.
2. **Clear Trebuchet Spotters second** — Their mark ability makes your soldiers take 50% more damage from all sources for 10 seconds. Do not let them mark your Shield Bearer.
3. **Loot every building** — The ruined village has 4-5 buildings with breakable crates. Each crate has a chance to drop artifacts or weapons.
4. **Find the breakable wall** — Near the village well, look for a cracked wall section. Breaking it reveals Fergus the Unyielding's shield (see [Battalion & Companion Guide](/guides/battalion-companion-guide)).

### Optimal Route

1. Spawn at coast entrance -> follow path to village ruins
2. Clear village (all buildings, breakable wall, well chest)
3. Fort approach (Banner Bearer + Pikeman squad)
4. Fort courtyard (Trebuchet Spotter on rampart — shoot or climb)
5. Fort battlements -> Ironclad Commander boss arena

### Loot Locations

| Location | Loot Type | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Village well chest | Guaranteed artifact | Check after clearing well enemies |
| Fort armory (breakable door) | 2-3 weapons | Door is breakable, not openable |
| Rampart chest | Gold (100-150) | Behind Trebuchet Spotter position |
| Boss arena flank chest | Artifact or emblem | Left side of arena before engaging boss |

### Transition to Act 2

After defeating the Ironclad Commander, the path to the Mining Complex opens through the eastern fort gate. **Before moving on**:
- Spend all gold at the Village (prioritize Armory Level 2 if you have 500 gold)
- Check your artifact slots — equip your best two artifacts for Act 2
- Ensure you have at least one healing potion

## Act 2: Mining Complex

The Mining Complex is larger and more dangerous than the Scottish Coast. Industrial machinery, explosive crystals, and tougher enemies await. Threat Level rises faster here — you should be comfortable with your build by the time you reach the Excavation Juggernaut.

### Recommended Preparation

| Requirement | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Reinforced+1 weapon minimum | Act 2 enemies have 50% more HP than Act 1 |
| Survival artifact equipped | Ironheart Pauldrons or similar |
| One emblem unlocked | At minimum, you should have your primary emblem |
| 3+ battalion soldiers | The Complex has wider corridors and more flanking routes |

### Enemy Types

| Enemy | Behavior | Counter | Threat Level |
|-------|----------|---------|--------------|
| Overseer | Ranged, calls reinforcements, debuffs player damage | **Highest priority** — rush with battalion charge | Very High |
| Crystal Brute | Slow, heavy damage, explodes on death | Keep distance, bait the slam, dodge the explosion | High |
| Mining Grunt | Faster than coastal Grunt, explosive pickaxe | Parry-able, stun-lock with heavy attacks | Low-Medium |
| Crystal Shambler | Slow movement, spawns crystal shards on death | Kill from range; shards deal AoE damage | Medium |
| Shield Bearer (Veteran) | Same as Act 1 but +50% HP and block | Two parries to break guard; or flank | Medium |

### Mechanics

#### Explosive Crystals

Purple-glowing crystals are scattered throughout the Mining Complex. They can be:
- **Destroyed by enemies** — Enemies hitting near a crystal cause it to explode (damages everyone nearby)
- **Shot by the player** — The Marksman can detonate crystals from range to clear groups
- **Used as terrain** — Block narrow passages; enemies take damage walking through them

**Pro tip**: Kite enemies toward crystals and detonate them with a ranged attack or by dodging through. A single crystal explosion can clear an entire squad of Mining Grunts.

#### Overseer Reinforcements

When an Overseer spots you, they call reinforcements every 15 seconds until killed. Reinforcements arrive from the direction the Overseer is facing. If you do not kill the Overseer quickly, you will be overwhelmed by endless waves.

### Objective Priority

1. **Overseer elimination** — Whenever you enter a new zone, scan for Overseers. They are the tall, cloaked figures on elevated platforms. Kill them before engaging ground enemies.
2. **Crystal management** — Do not fight in tight corridors near explosive crystals. Bait enemies into open areas or detonate crystals from a safe distance.
3. **Chest routing** — The Mining Complex has hidden alcoves with weapon chests. Look for side passages behind crystal formations.
4. **Excavation key** — Before reaching the boss, you need the Excavation Key (from the Overseer Commander at the central hub). Do not rush to the boss arena without it.

### Optimal Route

1. Entrance cavern -> crystal tunnel (watch for Crystal Shamblers)
2. Overseer platform (kill Overseer first, then clear ground)
3. Central hub -> Overseer Commander fight (drops Excavation Key)
4. Crystal storage room (loot all crates, 3 weapon chests)
5. Excavation site -> Excavation Juggernaut boss arena

### Transition to Act 3

After the Excavation Juggernaut, you enter the Corruption Zone. **Before proceeding**:
- **Corruption Resist is non-negotiable**. If you do not have a Corruption Resist emblem or artifact, seriously consider running Act 1-2 again to find one
- Reinforced+2 weapon is strongly recommended
- Stock up on Corruption Cure potions (Apothecary Level 2 required)
- Save at least 200 gold for emergency supplies

## Act 3: Corruption Zone

Act 3 is the final act of Tears of Metal's Early Access launch. The Corruption Zone is a twisted version of the highlands, where purple Corruption energy seeps from the ground and corrupts everything it touches. This act is significantly harder than the previous two.

### Recommended Preparation

| Requirement | Non-Negotiable? | Why |
|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| Corruption Resist | **Yes** | Without it, DoT damage kills in 30 seconds |
| Reinforced+2 weapon | **Yes** | Act 3 enemies have 2x Act 2 HP |
| 5+ battalion soldiers | Strongly recommended | Final boss fight needs bodies for conduits |
| Full healing potions | Strongly recommended | No health stations in Act 3 |
| Regen emblem | Recommended | Passive healing counteracts Corruption DoT |

### Corruption Mechanics

Corruption manifests in three ways in Act 3:

1. **Corruption Pools** — Purple pools on the ground. Standing in them deals 5% HP/sec as DoT. Avoid at all costs.
2. **Corruption Mist** — Periodic mist that fills the zone. During mist, all players take 2% HP/sec DoT regardless of position. Corruption Resist reduces this by 60%.
3. **Corrupted Enemies** — Every enemy in Act 3 is corrupted. Killing them leaves a temporary Corruption Pool at their death location.

### Enemy Types

| Enemy | Behavior | Counter | Threat Level |
|-------|----------|---------|--------------|
| Corruption Spawn | Small, fast, explodes on death (applies Corruption DoT) | Kill from range; do not let them reach you | High (in groups) |
| Blood-Steel Knight | Elite, heavily armored, applies Corruption with every hit | Parry required; cannot block through armor | Very High |
| Corrupted Pikeman | Faster Pikeman, attacks apply Corruption slow | Same as Act 1 but more aggressive | Medium |
| Corrupted Grunt | Charges, explodes on death (smaller Corruption Pool) | Dodge, do not block (explosion bypasses block) | Medium |
| Dragon Cultist | Ranged, fires Corruption bolts that track | **Highest priority** — bolts are hard to dodge in groups | Very High |

### Objective Priority

1. **Dragon Cultist elimination** — Their tracking bolts are the deadliest attack in the game. Kill them the moment they appear.
2. **Corruption Pool management** — When you kill a corrupted enemy, move away from the corpse. The pool lasts 10 seconds.
3. **Whispering Stone locations** — There are 2 Whispering Stones in Act 3 (out of 5 total). Listen for the hum near stone formations.
4. **Direct path to Dragon's Rest** — Act 3 is more linear than Acts 1 and 2. Do not explore unnecessary side paths — Corruption Mist will drain your resources faster than you regain them.

### Optimal Route

1. Corruption Zone entrance (immediate Dragon Cultist encounter)
2. Blood-Steel Knight patrol zone (3 knights, skip if low on resources)
3. Dragon Cultist camp (kill Cultists first, then loot)
4. Corrupted village (shortcut path — avoid the long way through the swamp)
5. Dragon's Rest approach (final waves before boss)
6. Dragon's Rest -> Corrupted Warlord + Dragon Core boss arena

### Final Boss Strategy

See our [Boss Guide](/guides/boss-guide) for complete Corrupted Warlord and Dragon Core phase breakdowns.

**Quick tips for the final fight**:
- Phase 1: Focus the Warlord. Dodge tendril grabs sideways. Jump over Corruption Waves.
- Phase 2 transition: When the Warlord drops below 40% HP, the Dragon Core activates. Immediately check for conduits.
- Conduit phase: Destroy all 4 conduits as fast as possible. The 8-second stun window on the Warlord is your best damage opportunity.
- Healing management: Save Corruption Cure potions for Phase 2. The Dragon Core's pulses are relentless.

## Post-Game and Replayability

After completing Act 3, Tears of Metal offers several reasons to keep playing:

### New Game Plus (Higher Difficulty)

After your first clear, you unlock **New Game Plus** mode:
- All enemies have +50% HP and damage
- Elite enemies are more common
- Threat Level starts higher and caps at 15 bars
- Reward: Increased Dragon Stone Shard drops (2 per Act boss), higher-rarity artifacts

### Daily Challenges

Each day, a special challenge run is available:
- Fixed seed (everyone gets the same layout)
- Modified rules (e.g., "double emblem trigger chance, 50% less healing")
- Leaderboard tracking
- Reward: 1 Dragon Stone Shard (first clear per day)

### Achievement Hunting

Hidden objectives worth pursuing:
- **No Soldier Lost** — Complete all three acts without a single soldier death (unlocks Morag the Mist)
- **Speed Demon** — Complete all three acts in under 90 minutes
- **Artifact Collector** — Collect 50 unique artifacts
- **Emblem Master** — Unlock all 18 emblems
- **Pure Solo** — Complete all three acts without recruiting any soldiers

### Build Experimentation

Each hero has multiple viable builds. After your first clear, try:
- **Elemental Vanguard** (Fire emblem + attack speed artifacts) — surprisingly effective
- **Tank Marksman** (Armor + Regen emblems, close-range with Repeater Crossbow) — meme build but works in co-op
- **All-Crit Berserker** (Double Critical Strike emblems, crit chance artifacts) — highest burst potential in the game
