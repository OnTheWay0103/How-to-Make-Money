# Tears of Metal Battalion and Companion Guide — Soldiers, Permadeath, and Unlocks

> **Applies to Early Access v1.0 / Updated: July 23, 2026**

Your battalion is your army. These procedurally generated Scottish soldiers accompany you on every expedition, and unlike the heroes, they can die permanently. Managing your battalion effectively — recruiting the right soldiers, protecting your veterans, and unlocking unique companions — is one of the most important skills in Tears of Metal.

---

## Battalion Overview

Your battalion starts small (2-3 soldiers) but can grow to 8+ soldiers by the end of a successful run. Each soldier has:

- **Name and rank** — Determines base stats (higher rank = higher stats)
- **Health bar** — Soldiers that take fatal damage die permanently
- **Skills** — Permanent bonuses unlocked through combat experience
- **Class** — Determines role and equipment

### Soldier Classes

| Class | Role | Base HP | Damage Type | Best Use |
|-------|------|---------|-------------|----------|
| Pikeman | Frontline melee | Medium | Physical (thrust) | Chokepoint defense, interupting enemy attacks |
| Grunt | General melee | Medium | Physical (slash) | All-purpose, flanking |
| Shield Bearer | Tank | High | Physical (bash) | **Best class** — holds aggro, blocks chokepoints |
| Trebuchet Spotter | Ranged | Low | Physical (projectile) | **Support only** — marks targets for bonus damage |
| Blood-Steel Knight | Elite melee | High | Physical + Corruption | **Late game** — high damage, high survivability |

## How to Recruit Soldiers

### During Expeditions

Recruitment banners appear in specific locations throughout each act:

| Act | Banner Locations | Soldiers Available |
|-----|------------------|-------------------|
| Act 1 | 3 banners (Scottish Coast fort, village ruins, coast path) | Pikemen, Grunts |
| Act 2 | 4 banners (Mining Complex entrance, crystal caves, Overseer quarters, excavation site) | Shield Bearers (rare), Grunts |
| Act 3 | 2 banners (Corruption Zone entrance, Dragon's Rest approach) | Blood-Steel Knights (rare), all classes |

### At the Village (Recruitment Tent)

Between runs, visit the Recruitment Tent in the Village. Higher tent levels unlock:
- **Level 1**: Basic Pikemen and Grunts
- **Level 2**: Shield Bearers and better starting stats
- **Level 3**: Elite prospects (potential Blood-Steel Knights after surviving 2 runs)
- **Level 4**: Guaranteed veteran with pre-existing skills

## Permanent Skill Progression

Soldiers gain XP from combat, which unlocks skill points. These skills are **permanent** for that soldier — they carry across runs as long as the soldier survives.

### Skill Trees

| Skill | Effect | Cost | Best For |
|-------|--------|------|----------|
| Iron Hide | +10% HP per level (max 3) | 1 point/level | Shield Bearers |
| Sharpened Steel | +10% damage per level (max 3) | 1 point/level | Pikemen, Blood-Steel Knights |
| Battle Hardened | +5% damage reduction per level (max 3) | 1 point/level | All classes |
| Swift Feet | +10% move speed per level (max 2) | 1 point/level | Grunts, flankers |
| Veteran's Instinct | +10% crit chance per level (max 2) | 2 points/level | Damage dealers |

### Priority Skill Allocation

| Soldier Class | First Skill | Second Skill | Third Skill |
|---------------|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| Shield Bearer | Iron Hide (Lv 2) | Battle Hardened (Lv 2) | Swift Feet (Lv 1) |
| Pikeman | Sharpened Steel (Lv 2) | Battle Hardened (Lv 1) | Veteran's Instinct (Lv 1) |
| Grunt | Battle Hardened (Lv 2) | Sharpened Steel (Lv 1) | Swift Feet (Lv 1) |
| Blood-Steel Knight | Iron Hide (Lv 3) | Sharpened Steel (Lv 3) | Veteran's Instinct (Lv 2) |

## Permadeath Management

### Understanding Permadeath

When a soldier's HP reaches 0, they are **dead permanently**. Their name, skills, and progress are gone. This is not a revival mechanic — it is irreversible.

### How to Protect Veterans

Veteran soldiers — those with 2+ skill points allocated — are the backbone of your late-game power. A veteran Shield Bearer with Iron Hide Lv 2 and Battle Hardened Lv 1 has 30% more HP and 10% damage reduction than a fresh recruit.

**Protection strategies:**

| Strategy | How | Why It Works |
|----------|-----|--------------|
| Back-line positioning | Keep veterans behind fresh soldiers | Fresh units absorb first hits |
| Retreat command | Use the "Retreat" ping on a low-HP veteran | They disengage and return to your position |
| Shield Bearer escort | Assign a Shield Bearer to guard your veteran | Aggro draws away from the valuable unit |
| Heal between fights | Use healing banners and potions on low-HP soldiers | Prevent death by attrition |
| Skip hard fights | If a veteran is low HP, avoid optional elite encounters | Better to miss loot than lose a veteran |

### When to Sacrifice Fresh Recruits

Fresh recruits (no skill points) are expendable. Use them for:
- **Chokepoint holds** — Block enemy paths while your veterans flank
- **Boss aggro** — Send them in first to eat the boss's opening attack
- **Suicide scouting** — Send them into unknown rooms to trigger traps
- **Reinforcement blockers** — Position them at spawn points to delay reinforcements

## Unique Companions

Unique Companions are handcrafted characters unlocked through hidden objectives. Unlike standard soldiers, companions have unique abilities, dialogue, and cannot be replaced.

### Companion Unlock Locations

| Companion | Unlock Requirement | Class | Unique Ability |
|-----------|-------------------|-------|---------------|
| Fergus the Unyielding | Find his lost shield in Act 1 village ruins (behind breakable wall) | Shield Bearer | "Unyielding" — cannot be staggered, +50% block efficiency |
| Morag of the Mist | Complete Act 2 without losing any soldiers (any class) | Pikeman | "Mist Walker" — invisible when not moving, first attack from stealth deals 3x damage |
| Brochan the Piper | Play the bagpipe emote at all 3 hidden cairns (one per act) | Grunt | "Piper's March" — all nearby soldiers gain +20% attack speed |
| Ailsa the Cursed | Defeat the Corrupted Warlord with Corruption Resist emblem equipped | Blood-Steel Knight | "Cursed Blades" — attacks apply Corruption DoT equal to 3% max HP/sec |
| Torcall the Unseen | Find 5 hidden Whispering Stones (scattered across all acts) | Trebuchet Spotter | "True Sight" — reveals hidden enemies and traps in a 30m radius |

### Companion Priority

| Priority | Companion | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Fergus the Unyielding | Best tank in the game; huge survivability boost |
| 2 | Ailsa the Cursed | Excellent damage for Act 3; Corruption DoT scales well |
| 3 | Brochan the Piper | Team-wide attack speed buff = more DPS for everyone |
| 4 | Morag of the Mist | Conditional but powerful; great for boss opening bursts |
| 5 | Torcall the Unseen | Mostly utility; useful for exploration but not combat |

### Tips for Unlocking Companions

- **Fergus is the easiest unlock** — The breakable wall is near the first village ruins in Act 1. Listen for the hollow sound when hitting walls.
- **Morag requires patience** — You must complete Act 2 without any soldier deaths. Consider running solo with just your starter soldiers if your battalion is large.
- **Brochan's cairns** — The bagpipe emote is found in a chest in Act 1. Cairns are marked by small rock piles with a faint blue glow.
- **Ailsa is the Act 3 reward** — Complete the game with Corruption Resist equipped. Do not remove the emblem during the run.
- **Torcall is a collectible hunt** — Whispering Stones make a faint humming sound. Turn your game audio up and listen carefully.

## Battalion Commands: When to Use Each

| Command | Bars | Strength | Best Use Case |
|---------|------|----------|---------------|
| Charge (single-bar) | 1 | Fast, moderate damage | Interrupting enemy attacks, quick burst |
| Charge (multi-bar) | 3 | Heavy damage, staggers | Boss stun windows, elite ganks |
| Shield Wall (single-bar) | 1 | Brief damage reduction | Surviving a single boss attack |
| Shield Wall (multi-bar) | 3 | Long duration, full immunity | Tanking Act 3 Corruption pulses |
| Volley (single-bar) | 1 | Quick ranged attack | Picking off low-HP enemies at range |
| Volley (multi-bar) | 3 | Heavy AoE barrage | Clearing a crowded room |
| Retreat | 0 | Immediate disengage | Saving a low-HP veteran from certain death |

### General Command Rules

- **Single-bar for speed** — Against fast enemies, single-bar commands are safer because the wind-up is nearly instant
- **Multi-bar for power** — Against bosses and slow enemies, multi-bar commands provide massive value
- **Do not overlap multi-bars** — Two players using multi-bar commands simultaneously split aggro and waste resources
- **Retreat is free** — Use it liberally. There is no cooldown and no resource cost

## Tactical Usage by Situation

### Chokepoint Defense
1. Position Shield Bearers at the chokepoint entrance
2. Pikemen behind them (Pikemen attack over Shield Bearers' heads)
3. Grunts on the flanks to catch enemies that break through
4. Use Shield Wall (single-bar) when enemies engage
5. Follow with Charge (single-bar) to clear the clump

### Ranged Threats (Trebuchet Spotters, Overseers)
1. Order all soldiers to hold position (default command)
2. Send Grunts as a flanking squad using the "Move" ping
3. Use Volley (single-bar) from your position to suppress
4. Once Grunts engage the ranged enemy, Charge (single-bar) with the main force

### Boss Opening
1. Send one Shield Bearer with Charge (single-bar) to draw aggro
2. Wait 3 seconds for the boss to engage the Shield Bearer
3. Multi-bar Charge with all remaining soldiers
4. Follow up with your own attacks during the stagger
