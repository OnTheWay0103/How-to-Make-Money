# Witchspire Beginner Guide — First 5 Hours

> **Applies to EA v0.1.1d / Updated: June 29, 2026**

Starting fresh in Witchspire can feel overwhelming. Six Coven options, a sprawling open world, and no hand-holding. This guide covers exactly what to do in your first session so you waste no time.

---

## Your First 5 Hours: Priority Checklist

| Time Mark | Objective | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| 0-30 min | Complete tutorial, pick Coven, explore Hearth | Establishes your core loop |
| 30-90 min | Gather basic resources (wood, stone, fiber) | Unlocks first crafting stations |
| 90 min-2 hr | Build Workbench + Garden Plot | Passive food + gear crafting |
| 2-3 hr | Catch your first Familiar in Vyrs | Combat + gathering boost |
| 3-4 hr | Upgrade Hearth to Tier 2 | Unlocks Furnace + Broom crafting |
| 4-5 hr | Farm Al'uunian Golem materials | First boss kill + region unlock |

---

## Starting Resource Priority

Gather in this order the moment you spawn in Vyrs:

| Resource | Use | Where to Find |
|----------|-----|---------------|
| Wood | Workbench, walls, tools | Trees near Hearth (any forest patch) |
| Stone | Furnace, walls, upgrade parts | Rocky outcrops in Vyrs fields |
| Fiber | Rope, basic armor, garden plots | Tall grass clusters |
| Iron Ore | Tools, weapons (unlocks after Furnace) | Vyrs caves (south-east area) |
| Nectar | Food, healing potions | Yellow flowers, fields |

**Pro tip**: Hold E to auto-collect. Do not manually click each node.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Picking the Wrong Coven for Your Playstyle
Cloudpiercers and Wildroots are the most forgiving for new players. Avoid Nightscribes and Foretellers until you understand combat mechanics — their glass-cannon builds require precise positioning.

See [Best Starting Coven Guide](/guides/best-coven-guide) for detailed breakdowns.

### 2. Spending Luminary Points Recklessly
**You cannot reset your Luminary skill tree.** Do not spend points until you have read the nodes ahead. New players should rush the "+1 Familiar Slot" node — it doubles your early power.

See [Luminary Skill Tree Guide](/guides/luminary-skill-tree-guide) for recommended builds.

### 3. Ignoring the Hearth Upgrade
The Hearth is your main progression gate. Each upgrade tier unlocks critical stations (Workbench at T1, Furnace at T2, Witchcraft Circle at T3). If you are stuck, you probably need to upgrade your Hearth.

See [Hearth Upgrade Guide](/guides/hearth-upgrade-guide) for material requirements.

### 4. Not Using Your Familiar in Combat
Your familiar is not just a pet. It deals damage, gathers materials, and can tank boss aggro. Bind your familiar's active ability to a hotkey and use it every fight.

### 5. Wandering Into High-Level Zones
The Shuddersands and Timberline Basin have level 20+ enemies. Stay in Vyrs (level 1-8) until you have at least Tier 2 Hearth and iron tools.

## Fastest Progression Path

### Step 1: Choose Cloudpiercers or Wildroots
- **Cloudpiercers** (Spellblade + Lux): Best weapon range, good damage, easiest to learn.
- **Wildroots** (Wand + Nature): Healing ability keeps you alive, pets tank for you.

### Step 2: Rush Hearth Tier 1 → Workbench
1. Gather 20 Wood + 15 Stone
2. Build Workbench at your Hearth
3. Craft Stone Axe + Stone Pickaxe
4. Gather 30 Wood + 25 Stone for Hearth Tier 2

### Step 3: Catch a Familiar
Go to the Vyrs meadow (north of Hearth) and catch a **Sproutling** (Nature, Common) or **Emberpuff** (Lux, Common). Both are excellent early-game fighters.

### Step 4: Build a Broom
After Hearth Tier 2, craft a Broom at the Workbench. Flight massively speeds up exploration.

See [Flight Unlock Guide](/guides/flight-unlock-guide) for broom recipes.

### Step 5: Fight Al'uunian Golem (Level 8+)
The first boss is in the eastern Vyrs ruins. Bring healing potions, a level 5+ familiar, and watch for the ground slam AoE.

## Quick Tips

- **Food buffs stack**. Eat before boss fights for stat increases.
- **Night = danger**. Enemies get tougher after dark. Build a bed to skip to morning.
- **Drop your Hearth flag** when exploring new areas to set a fast-travel point.
- **Salvage old gear** at the Workbench instead of discarding it — you get partial materials back.
