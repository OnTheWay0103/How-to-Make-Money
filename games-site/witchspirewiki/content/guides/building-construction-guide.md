# Witchspire Building & Construction Guide — Houses, Hearths, and Base Layout

> **Applies to EA v0.1.1d / Updated: July 18, 2026**

Building in Witchspire is how you establish a permanent base, unlock progression, and protect your workstations. This guide covers the full construction system from your first foundation to a fully upgraded Hearth base.

---

## Building System Overview

Witchspire uses a snap-point construction system. Every structure piece connects to existing pieces via visible blue snap points. You can build anywhere in the open world, but all functional stations must be placed within your Hearth's influence radius.

### Building Categories

| Category | Pieces | Function |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Foundation | Floor tiles, pillars, stairs | Structural base for all builds |
| Walls | Full walls, half walls, window walls | Enclosure and defense |
| Roof | Sloped roof, flat roof, skylight | Weather protection |
| Decor | Fences, lanterns, signs, rugs | Aesthetics and lighting |
| Stations | Workbench, Furnace, Garden Plot, etc. | Crafting and progression |

All pieces are crafted at the **Workbench** after the required materials are unlocked.

---

## How to Place Structures

Enter building mode by pressing **B** (default key). A radial menu appears with all available piece categories.

### Basic Placement Steps

1. Press **B** to enter build mode
2. Select a category from the radial menu
3. Hover over a valid snap point — it glows green
4. Left-click to place, scroll wheel to rotate
5. Right-click to cancel or exit build mode

**Snap point tips**: Foundation pieces snap to terrain and other foundations. Walls snap to foundation edges. Roof pieces snap to wall tops. Pillars can be placed independently to support larger structures.

### Rotation and Precision

| Action | Default Key |
|--------|-------------|
| Rotate piece 45 degrees | Scroll wheel |
| Rotate piece 90 degrees | R key |
| Toggle snap mode | Z key |
| Raise/lower piece | Hold Shift + scroll |
| Move piece (already placed) | Hold E + drag |

| Piece | Materials | HP | Unlock |

## Hearth Building Requirements

Your Hearth is the centerpiece of your base. Every upgrade tier increases the build radius and unlocks new stations.

### Hearth Upgrade Material Table

| Tier | Materials | Build Radius | Unlocks |
|------|-----------|--------------|---------|
| 1 | 10 Wood, 5 Stone | 15m | Workbench, basic pieces |
| 2 | 20 Wood, 15 Stone, 10 Fiber | 20m | Garden Plot, broom crafting |
| 3 | 30 Refined Wood, 20 Stone Block, 10 Iron Ingot | 25m | Furnace, reinforced pieces |
| 4 | 40 Refined Wood, 30 Stone Block, 15 Steel Ingot, 5 Crystal Shards | 30m | Witchcraft Circle, stone pieces |
| 5 | 50 Enchanted Steel, 40 Stone Block, 20 Ethereal Dust, 10 Astral Essence | 40m | Enchantment Table, advanced decor |

### Hearth Influence Radius

Your Hearth creates an invisible sphere of influence. Within this radius:
- Crafting stations remain active when you leave the area
- Enemies do not spawn
- Placed items and furnishings persist across server restarts

You can see the radius boundary as a shimmering blue ring when holding your Hearth Stone item.

## Base Layout Optimization

Good base design saves time and keeps you safe. Here are the most effective layouts.

### The Compact Tower (Solo Players)

Recommended for solo play. Build vertically to minimize material cost while accessing all stations.

```
Floor 1: Hearth + Workbench + Furnace
Floor 2: Garden Plots (sunlight access)
Floor 3: Broom Parking + Chest Storage
Floor 4: Witchcraft Circle + Enchantment Table
Roof:  Lookout platform + Landing pad
```

**Why it works**: Vertical builds use fewer foundation pieces and are easier to defend. Single staircase keeps you oriented.

### The Sprawling Compound (Co-op / Groups)

Best for 3+ players. Build wide with dedicated buildings for each function.

```
Center:  Open courtyard with Hearth
North:   Furnace + Workshop building
East:    Housing wing (1 room per player)
South:   Garden + Farm plots
West:    Storage + Witchcraft Circle
Perimeter: Stone walls with gate
```

**Why it works**: Multiple players can work simultaneously without blocking each other. Designated areas prevent organization chaos.

### Defense-First Bunker (PvP Servers)

If playing on a PvP-enabled server, fortification matters.

- Build on elevated terrain or cliff edges (limits attack angles)
- Surround base with **reinforced walls** in concentric layers
- Place Hearth on the topmost floor, not ground level
- Use **Stone Gate** as your main entrance — weakest part of any base
- Keep backup chests hidden outside the base (emergency stash)

## Creative Building Ideas

### Treehouse Base

Build around the massive ancient trees in Timberline Basin. Use the tree trunk as your central pillar and build platforms at multiple heights.

**Materials needed**: ~100 Refined Wood, 40 Stone Block, 20 Sturdy Fiber
**Advantage**: Natural defense — most ground enemies cannot reach you

### Floating Island Base

Using elevated terrain in Mira Isles, build a bridge-connected island base. Requires precise foundation placement.

**Materials needed**: ~200 Stone Block, 60 Steel Ingot, reinforced pillars
**Advantage**: Aesthetic views + complete ground immunity

### Underground Base

Excavate into cliff faces using the terrain as natural walls. Place your Hearth inside a cave or carved-out hillside.

**Materials needed**: Minimal walls required (terrain serves as walls)
**Advantage**: Nearly invisible from outside, excellent PvP concealment

## Common Building Mistakes

### 1. Building Too Large Too Early
Each piece costs materials. A 10x10 foundation costs 400 Wood before you add a single wall. Start compact and expand only when you have a material surplus.

### 2. Ignoring Structural Support
Roof pieces and upper floors require pillars or walls below them. If a piece turns red during placement, you need support underneath. Place a pillar every 4 foundation tiles for large rooms.

### 3. Placing Hearth in an Open Field
Your Hearth should be near resources but also defensible. Cliff edges, forest clearings with natural barriers, and elevated plateaus are ideal. Avoid open plains.

### 4. Forgetting to Build a Broom Hangar
You will land your broom at your base constantly. Without a dedicated flat roof or platform area, you will clip through walls or get stuck. Build a landing pad at least 3x3 tiles on your roof or in your courtyard.
