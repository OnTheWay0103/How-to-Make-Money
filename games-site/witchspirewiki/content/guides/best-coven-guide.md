# Best Starting Coven Guide — Which Class to Pick

> **Applies to EA v0.1.1d / Updated: June 29, 2026**

Your Coven choice determines your weapon, magic school, and starting spells. There are 6 Coven classes. Pick the wrong one and your first 10 hours will be a struggle. This guide ranks them for solo and co-op play.

---

## Coven Quick Reference Table

| Coven | Weapon | Magic School | Role | Difficulty |
|-------|--------|-------------|------|------------|
| Cloudpiercers | Spellblade | Lux (Light) | DPS / Burst | Easy |
| Starsmiths | Spellblade | Crystal | CC / Utility | Medium |
| Foretellers | Spellblade | Ethereal | Ranged DPS | Hard |

## Coven Breakdown

### Cloudpiercers (Spellblade + Lux) — ★ Best for New Players
- **Weapon**: Spellblade (melee + magic hybrid)
- **Magic**: Lux — light-based burst damage
- **Role**: Frontline DPS with AoE clear
- **Why pick it**: Your Spellblade beam attacks hit multiple enemies. Lux spells provide excellent burst for both packs and bosses. Simple rotation: close distance → beam → Lux explosion.
- **Best for**: Solo beginners, co-op DPS role
- **Weakness**: No self-heal; relies on potions

### Starsmiths (Spellblade + Crystal)
- **Weapon**: Spellblade
- **Magic**: Crystal — slow, stun, area control
- **Role**: Utility / Support DPS
- **Why pick it**: Crystal walls block enemy paths. Your slow fields make boss mechanics trivial. Excellent for co-op teams that need crowd control.
- **Best for**: Co-op support, experienced players controlling the battlefield
- **Weakness**: Lower raw damage; requires team coordination for max value

### Foretellers (Spellblade + Ethereal)
- **Weapon**: Spellblade (projectile mode)
- **Magic**: Ethereal — teleportation, debuffs, magic damage over time
- **Role**: Mobile assassin
- **Why pick it**: Teleport behind enemies, apply Ethereal marks, detonate for big damage. Highest skill ceiling of the Spellblade classes.
- **Best for**: Veterans who want mobility-focused gameplay
- **Weakness**: Very squishy; one wrong teleport = death

### Wildroots (Wand + Nature) — ★ Best for Survival
- **Weapon**: Wand (ranged magic)
- **Magic**: Nature — healing, pet buffs, thorn armor
- **Role**: Healer / Off-tank
- **Why pick it**: Nature spells include a heal-over-time and a thorn shield that reflects damage. Your familiar gets significantly stronger. You can face-tank most early content.
- **Best for**: Solo players who want safety, co-op healers
- **Weakness**: Lower burst damage; fights take longer

### Tomekeepers (Wand + Nox)
- **Weapon**: Wand
- **Magic**: Nox — poison, decay, damage over time
- **Role**: Sustain DPS
- **Why pick it**: Stack poison on enemies and watch them melt. Excellent for boss fights where you can keep distance. Nox clouds damage enemies in an area.
- **Best for**: Players who enjoy DoT gameplay loops, co-op sustained damage
- **Weakness**: Slow ramp-up; weak against fast-moving enemies

### Nightscribes (Wand + Astral)
- **Weapon**: Wand
- **Magic**: Astral — high critical damage, self-damage mechanics
- **Role**: Glass cannon burst
- **Why pick it**: Astral spells trade health for damage. Your critical hits can one-shot regular mobs. Powerful, but every fight is risky.
- **Best for**: Advanced players who know boss patterns
- **Weakness**: Self-damage can kill you; requires perfect positioning

## Spellblade vs Wand — Which Weapon?

This is the most important choice in character creation. Here is the difference:

| Spellblade | Wand |
|------------|------|
| Melee-hybrid | Pure ranged |
| Higher base damage | Lower base damage |
| Stamina-based combos | Mana-based spells |
| Requires closer positioning | Safe distance |
| Faster clear speed on packs | Better for single-target bossing |

**Our recommendation**: Pick Spellblade if you enjoy aggressive, in-your-face combat. Pick Wand if you prefer kiting and casting from range.

## Verdict

**First-time player? Pick Cloudpiercers or Wildroots.** Cloudpiercers teach you aggressive play. Wildroots keep you alive while you learn. Both are viable all the way to endgame.
