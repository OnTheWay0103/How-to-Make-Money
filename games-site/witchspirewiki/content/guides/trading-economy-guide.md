# Trading and Economy Guide — Currency, Vendors, and Resource Value

> **Applies to EA v0.1.1d / Updated: July 23, 2026**

Witchspire's economy is primarily resource-based with vendor shops providing a steady outlet for surplus materials. Unlike traditional MMO economies, there is no global auction house or player-to-player trading — wealth is measured by your material stockpiles and Hearth upgrades rather than coin. This guide explains how the economy works, how to value resources, and how to avoid wasting limited materials.

---

## Currency System

Witchspire uses a simple two-tier currency system:

| Currency | How to Earn | What It Buys |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Gold** | Selling to vendors, completing quests, loot drops | Vendor items, recipes, some upgrades |
| **Materials** | Gathering, farming, crafting, enemy drops | Hearth upgrades, gear crafting, potions |

Gold is useful but finite in what it can purchase. The vast majority of progression is material-gated, not gold-gated. Do not prioritize gold farming — prioritize material diversity.

### Gold Income Sources

| Source | Typical Payout | Frequency |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Quest completion | 50-200 Gold | One-time per quest |
| Selling crafted items | 10-80 Gold | As often as you craft surplus |
| Selling raw materials | 2-15 Gold per stack | Unlimited |
| Boss loot | 100-500 Gold | Per boss kill |
| Event rewards | 50-300 Gold | Per event |

## Vendor System

Vendors are scattered across each region. They stock a rotating inventory of base materials, seeds, and occasionally rare recipes.

### Vendor Types

| Vendor | Location | Sells | Buys |
|--------|----------|-------|------|
| General Merchant | Vyrs Hub | Seeds, basic tools, fiber armor | All materials, gear |
| Alchemist's Apprentice | Vyrs Hub | Nectar, basic potions, Crystal Shards | Herbs, potions |
| Timberline Trader | Timberline Basin Camp | Moonleaf seeds, advanced tools, coal | Ores, processed materials |
| Mira Isles Provisioner | Mira Isles Dock | Boat repairs, waterwalking items, rare seeds | High-tier materials, artifacts |
| Shuddersands Outfitter | Shuddersands Outpost | Nightvision items, Ethereal Dust recipes | Ethereal items, bones, rare drops |

### Vendor Refresh Cycle

- **Daily reset** (real-time 24h): Basic stock refreshes
- **Bi-weekly reset**: Rare recipes and limited-stock items refresh
- **No player-driven market** exists as of EA v0.1.1d — all trading is vendor-based

### Vendor Reputation

Some vendors offer better prices as you sell to them repeatedly. Track your total gold traded with each vendor:

| Tier | Total Gold Traded | Price Bonus |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| Neutral | 0 | 0% |
| Friendly | 500 | +5% sell price, -5% buy price |
| Respected | 2,000 | +10% sell price, -10% buy price |
| Honored | 5,000 | +15% sell price, -15% buy price |

Focus on reaching **Friendly** with the General Merchant and Alchemist early — these two vendors handle the bulk of early-game transactions.

## Resource Tier System

Materials in Witchspire fall into tiers. Understanding the tier system prevents you from selling materials you will need later.

| Tier | Examples | Used For | Should You Sell? |
|------|----------|----------|------------------|
| **Tier 1 (Common)** | Wood, Stone, Fiber, Bones | Basic tools, early building | Yes — surplus after 100 stockpiled |
| **Tier 2 (Uncommon)** | Iron Ore, Refined Wood, Leather, Nectar | Iron gear, mid-game crafting, potions | Only after 200 stockpiled |
| **Tier 3 (Rare)** | Steel Ingots, Sturdy Planks, Coal, Crystal Shards | Steel gear, advanced potions, structures | Keep — always in demand |
| **Tier 4 (Exceptional)** | Ethereal Dust, Enchanted Materials, Astral Essence | Endgame gear, legendary familiars, advanced alchemy | Never sell |
| **Tier 5 (Legendary)** | Unknown (EA) | Speculative future content | Never sell |

### Safe-to-Sell Materials

These materials are abundant and have limited use beyond early crafting:

| Material | Recommended Stockpile | Sell Rest |
|----------|----------------------|-----------|
| Wood | 200 | Yes |
| Stone | 200 | Yes |
| Fiber | 150 | Yes |
| Bones | 100 | Yes |
| Raw Hide | 50 | Yes |

### Keep-at-All-Costs Materials

## Crafting for Profit

Selling crafted items is almost always more profitable than selling raw materials.

| Raw Materials Value | Crafted Item Value | Profit Margin |
|--------------------|--------------------|---------------|
| 10 Wood (2 Gold) | 1 Refined Wood (8 Gold) | +300% |
| 6 Iron Ore (6 Gold) | 2 Iron Ingots (20 Gold) | +233% |
| 2 Iron Ingots + 1 Coal (18 Gold) | 1 Steel Ingot (45 Gold) | +150% |
| 5 Nectar + 2 Sunbloom (8 Gold) | Minor Healing Potion (25 Gold) | +212% |

**Rule of thumb:** Always process raw ore into ingots and raw wood into refined wood before selling. The furnace time is minimal and the profit increase is substantial.

## Wealth-Building Strategies

### Early Game (Vyrs)

1. **Sell only after stockpiling.** Keep 100 Wood, 100 Stone, and 50 Fiber minimum before selling any.
2. **Process before selling.** Run all raw ore and wood through the Furnace first.
3. **Sell spare Fiber armor.** Craft Fiber Hat/Robe/Boots and sell to the General Merchant for early gold.
4. **Do not buy gear from vendors.** Craft everything. Vendor gear is overpriced and under-statted.

### Mid Game (Timberline Basin)

1. **Specialize in Moonleaf.** Moonleaf is the highest-value garden crop. Sell surplus directly or craft into Mana Potions.
2. **Mine Coal daily.** Coal is a consistent seller and required for steel.
3. **Stockpile Crystal Shards.** Even if you do not need them yet, they are about to become critical.

### Late Game (Mira Isles / Shuddersands)

1. **Farm Ethereal enemies.** Ethereal Dust is the most valuable craftable material per inventory slot.
2. **Craft Greater Healing Elixirs for sale.** These sell for the highest vendor price of any craftable item.
3. **Hold Astral Essence.** Do not sell it. Its value will only increase as more late-game recipes are added.

## What to Buy from Vendors

Not everything a vendor sells is worth buying. Here is what is actually good value:

| Item | Vendor | Cost | Worth It? |
|------|--------|------|-----------|
| Sunbloom Seeds | General Merchant | 10 Gold | Yes — infinite healing |
| Basic Capture Orb | General Merchant | 25 Gold | Yes — familiar catching |
| Coal | Timberline Trader | 15 Gold each | Only if you cannot mine it |
| Crystal Shards | Alchemist | 20 Gold each | Only for urgent potion needs |
| Moonleaf Seeds | Timberline Trader | 50 Gold | Yes — unlocks late-game farming |
| Iron Gear | General Merchant | 150-300 Gold | No — craft it yourself |
| Ethereal Dust | Shuddersands Outfitter | 200 Gold | Only if you need 1-2 urgently |
