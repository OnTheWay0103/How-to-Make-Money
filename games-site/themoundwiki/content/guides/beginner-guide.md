# The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu — Beginner's Guide (First 5 Hours)

> Launch Version / Updated: July 14, 2026

Welcome, conquistador. The New World is not what the Crown promised. **The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu** is a 1-4 player cooperative PvE extraction horror game set in 1652 Chile, during the conquest era. You and your crew sail from the Galleon — your floating hub — into the Lovecraftian unknown, accept Contracts, explore hostile islands, and extract with loot before the forest consumes everything.

This guide covers everything you need to survive your first five hours.

---

## The Core Loop (Step by Step)

The game revolves around a simple four-phase loop:

| Phase | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **Hub (Galleon)** | Customize loadout, accept Contracts, set sail from the crew quarters. |
| **Contract** | Choose a mission type from the Contract Board. Each has objectives, difficulty, and risk level. |
| **Expedition** | Deploy to the island. Navigate, fight, loot, and manage your Sanity. The forest is alive — it reacts to noise, light, and violence. |
| **Extract** | Reach the extraction point before the hidden timer expires. Get back to the Galleon with your loot or lose everything. |

**Pro tip:** Always discuss the extraction route before deploying. Once boots hit the ground, communication becomes harder due to Spatial Voice Chat — distance and direction affect how clearly you can hear teammates.

## Starting Loadout Priority

You begin the game with limited resources. Spend wisely.

| Priority | Item | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| 1 | Crossbow | Silent kills. Does not trigger forest aggro. Rain-proof. Essential for solo and team play. |
| 2 | Medallion | Detects nearby treasure chests. The pinging sound can draw enemies, but the loot is worth the risk. |
| 3 | Healing Salves | Sanity recovery and health regen. You will need both. |
| 4 | Oil Lamp | Essential for dark interiors and cave systems. Rain makes it flicker — carry backup. |
| 5 | Flintlock Pistol | Reliable sidearm. Slower reload but works in all weather. |
| 6 | Matchlock Arquebus | Powerful but useless in rain. Bring only if the forecast is clear. |

**Rule of thumb:** Take one silent weapon and one loud weapon per expedition. The Crossbow + Flintlock Pistol is the most forgiving starter combo.

## Common Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

### 1. Firing Guns in the Rain
**The Mistake:** Pulling the Matchlock Arquebus trigger during a downpour.
**Why It Hurts:** Rain disables gunpowder weapons. You waste ammo and alert every enemy within earshot.
**Fix:** Check the sky before engaging. Swap to Crossbow or melee when rain starts.

### 2. Splitting Up Alone
**The Mistake:** Wandering off from the squad to grab "one more chest."
**Why It Hurts:** Spatial Voice Chat means you cannot hear teammates from far away. Sanity hallucinations can mimic ally voices, leading you into traps. A downed teammate far from the group is nearly impossible to revive.
**Fix:** Stay within visual distance. Use the Medallion ping as a rendezvous signal.

### 3. Ignoring Sanity
**The Mistake:** Letting your Sanity bar drain to zero without treatment.
**Why It Hurts:** Low Sanity triggers hallucinations — fake chests, false enemy alerts, and teammates appearing as monsters. You waste time, ammo, and trust.
**Fix:** Carry at least one Sanity-restoring item per expedition. Watch the visual cues (screen distortion, whispers) and use healing items early.

### 4. Overstaying the Timer
**The Mistake:** Looting past the point when the forest "wakes up."
**Why It Hurts:** The game has a hidden timer. When it expires, enemy spawns increase dramatically, the extraction point becomes harder to reach, and the Ox Cart route may be overrun.
**Fix:** Know your exit. As soon as you hear the telltale environmental change (wind picking up, distant howls), turn around. Do not pass go. Do not open one more chest.

## First Expedition Checklist

Before you set sail on your first expedition, confirm:

- [ ] One silent weapon equipped (Crossbow or melee)
- [ ] At least one Sanity recovery item
- [ ] Oil Lamp with fuel
- [ ] Medallion (if available)
- [ ] Extraction point marked on your mental map
- [ ] A teammate assigned to watch the rear
- [ ] Voice chat test — confirm everyone can hear

---

## Parting Advice

The Mound punishes overconfidence. Treat every expedition like a horror movie: stick together, save your bullets, and run when the forest tells you to. The loot will be there tomorrow. Your character's life will not.
