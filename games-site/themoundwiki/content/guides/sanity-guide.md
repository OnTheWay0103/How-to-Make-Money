# The Mound Sanity Guide — Surviving the Madness System

> Launch Version / Updated: July 14, 2026

Sanity is the invisible clock that ticks during every expedition in **The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu**. Unlike a traditional health bar, Sanity erodes your perception of reality. Let it drain too low, and the forest begins to play tricks on you — tricks that can get you and your team killed.

---

## How Sanity Works

Each player has an independent Sanity meter. It depletes through:

| Trigger | Sanity Loss | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Witnessing enemy kills | Small | Repeated exposure builds up |
| Being hit by supernatural enemies | Medium | Y'm-bhi attacks drain more |
| Wearing Mask of Nyarlathotep | Continuous drain | Drain rate increases over time |
| Entering dark zones or caves | Slow drain | Oil Lamp slows this significantly |
| Teammate death nearby | Large | Proximity matters |
| Failing a Sanity check on a hallucination | Large | Engaging with a fake object costs you |

Sanity naturally regenerates very slowly outside of combat. Items and Galleon rest are the primary recovery methods.

## All Known Hallucination Types

Hallucinations are unique to each player — what you see may not exist for your teammates. Communication is your only defense.

### Visual Hallucinations

| Hallucination | What You See | Reality | How to Confirm |
|--------------|-------------|---------|----------------|
| Fake Treasure Chest | A chest with visible glint | Nothing there — or a trap | Ask a teammate to look at the same spot |
| False Enemy | An enemy patrol that does not exist | Empty corridor | Watch teammates' reactions; if they do not react, it is fake |
| Teammate as Monster | An ally appears as a creature | That is your friend | Check the nameplate above their head (real nameplates are stable) |
| Duplicate Teammate | A copy of a teammate standing elsewhere | Only one real version | Spatial voice — the fake does not speak |
| Moving Walls | Walls that appear to shift | Static geometry | Mark a reference point; real walls do not move |
| False Extraction Point | A fake extraction marker | Wrong location | Cross-reference with team map |
| Loot Mirage | A valuable item on the ground | Nothing | Ask a teammate to pick it up |

### Auditory Hallucinations

| Hallucination | What You Hear | Reality |
|--------------|---------------|---------|
| False Teammate Voices | Teammate calling for help | No one called out |
| Distant Combat Sounds | Gunfire or screams | Silence |
| Footsteps | Approaching enemy footsteps | No one is there |
| Whispers | Unintelligible whispers | Ambient audio only in low-Sanity state |

### Tactical Hallucinations

| Hallucination | Effect | Reality |
|--------------|--------|---------|
| UI Glitches | Health or Sanity bar shows wrong values | Your actual stats are different |
| Map Distortion | The map appears to show different terrain | Map is accurate |
| Item Count Errors | Inventory shows items you do not have | Check physically |

## How to Distinguish Real from Hallucination

### The Three-Second Rule
When you see or hear something suspicious, pause and count to three. Hallucinations tend to flicker, stutter, or repeat on a loop. Real entities have continuous behavior.

### Team Cross-Reference
**This is the single most important technique.** If you see a chest, an enemy, or a teammate in a strange place, ask:
> "Does anyone else see a chest at these coordinates?"

If no one responds, it is a hallucination. Do not interact with it.

### Visual Tells
- Hallucination chests have a slightly different glow pattern
- Fake enemies move in a jerky, unnatural rhythm
- Duplicate teammates lack the subtle animations of real players (weapon sway, head turning)
- Screen distortion (vignette darkening, color shifts) indicates your Sanity is low

### Sound Tells
- Hallucination audio sounds slightly "muffled" or "distant" compared to real sounds
- False teammate voices do not respect spatial audio — they sound the same distance regardless of where you are
- Whispers are always fake if they contain specific information (like "open this chest")

## Team Communication Strategies

Sanity is a team problem, even though it affects individuals.

1. **Call out hallucinations immediately.** Use a code word. Example: "Possible hallucination at my 2 o'clock — can anyone confirm?"
2. **Do not shoot at a teammate's hallucination.** You waste ammo and make noise. More importantly, you may hit a real teammate who is standing near the false target.
3. **Check Sanity levels at every rest point.** Low-Sanity players should stay in the center of the formation.
4. **Designate a "sanity anchor."** One player with high Sanity stays calm and confirms or denies visual claims.
5. **Use Medallion pings as reality checks.** If you ping a location and your teammates confirm they heard it, what you see is real.

## Mask of Nyarlathotep — Using the Riskiest Item

The **Mask of Nyarlathotep** is a powerful tool that allows you to blend in with enemies. Enemies will ignore you as long as you wear it.

### Mechanics
- While worn, nearby enemies do not aggro
- Sanity drains continuously while the mask is active
- Teammates can still damage you — friendly fire is possible
- The mask has a subtle visual effect on your character model

### Strategy

| Situation | Should You Use the Mask? |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| Solo flanking an enemy patrol | Yes — high value |
| Team is fighting | No — friendly fire risk |
| Need to cross an open area | Yes — but warn teammates first |
| Sanity is already low | No — danger of critical Sanity |
| Escaping to extraction | Yes — last resort |

### Mask Safety Rules
1. **Verbally announce** "Mask on" to your team every time you activate it
2. **Move predictably** so teammates do not mistake you for an enemy
3. **Keep the mask duration short** — 30 seconds max before Sanity becomes critical
4. **Have a healing item ready** for when you take it off
