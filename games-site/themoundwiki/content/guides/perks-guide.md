# Perks Guide — Passive Skills, Unlock Conditions, and Best Builds

> Launch Version / Updated: July 23, 2026

Perks are passive abilities in **The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu** that provide permanent upgrades to your character. Unlike equipment, perks are always active once unlocked and do not take up inventory space. This guide covers every perk in the game, how to unlock them, which combinations are strongest, and how to adapt your perk build for different expedition types.

---

## Perk System Overview

Perks are unlocked by completing specific in-game achievements rather than spending skill points. Each perk is tied to a milestone — survive a certain number of expeditions, defeat a boss type, or collect enough of a resource.

| Perk Category | Number of Perks | Focus |
|---------------|----------------|-------|
| **Survival** | 6 | Health, Sanity, healing |
| **Combat** | 6 | Weapon damage, critical hits, enemy interactions |
| **Stealth** | 4 | Detection, move speed, noise reduction |
| **Utility** | 5 | Inventory, crafting, extraction |
| **Eldritch** | 4 | Sanity management, Y'm-bhi interactions |

**Total perks available at launch:** 25
**Max perks equippable at once:** 6 (unlocked via progression)

## Survival Perks

| Perk | Effect | Unlock Condition | Tier |
|------|--------|-----------------|------|
| **Tough Hide** | +15 Max HP | Survive 10 expeditions | 1 |
| **Iron Stomach** | Poison duration reduced by 50% | Survive 3 poison encounters | 1 |
| **Second Wind** | When reduced below 25% HP, regenerate 20% HP over 10s (90s cooldown) | Extract with less than 10% HP remaining | 2 |
| **Restful Sleep** | Sanity recovery from resting is +30% | Complete an expedition without taking Sanity damage | 2 |
| **Numbed Mind** | First Sanity hit each expedition is reduced by 50% | Reach 0 Sanity and survive to extract | 3 |
| **Unyielding** | Bleedout time increased by 50% | Revive 10 downed teammates | 3 |

### Survival Perk Recommendations

**For beginners:** Tough Hide + Iron Stomach. The extra HP and poison resistance cover the two most common early-game death causes.

**For solo players:** Second Wind + Numbed Mind. Both perks save you when things go wrong — which happens more often in solo play without teammates to bail you out.

**For co-op healers:** Restful Sleep + Unyielding. Your job is to keep the team alive longer; these perks extend everyone's survival window.

## Combat Perks

| Perk | Effect | Unlock Condition | Tier |
|------|--------|-----------------|------|
| **Blade Master** | Melee attacks deal +15% damage | Kill 50 enemies with melee weapons | 1 |
| **Marksman** | Ranged attacks deal +15% damage | Kill 50 enemies with ranged weapons | 1 |
| **Critical Eye** | +5% critical hit chance | Land 20 headshots or backstabs | 1 |
| **Adrenaline Rush** | After killing an enemy, +20% attack speed for 5s | Kill 3 enemies within 10 seconds | 2 |
| **Executioner** | Damage to enemies below 30% HP increased by 25% | Kill 100 enemies total | 2 |
| **Bulwark** | Take 20% less damage while stationary and facing the enemy | Block or parry 50 attacks | 3 |

### Combat Perk Recommendations

**For melee-focused builds:** Blade Master + Adrenaline Rush + Executioner. The attack speed and execute damage chain together — one kill speeds up the next kill, which hits harder thanks to Executioner.

**For ranged builds:** Marksman + Critical Eye + Executioner. Ranged headshots already deal bonus damage; Critical Eye pushes it further, and Executioner finishes weakened enemies.

**For hybrid builds:** Critical Eye + Adrenaline Rush + Bulwark. Survive the approach, deliver the burst, and reset with Adrenaline Rush for the next target.

## Stealth Perks

| Perk | Effect | Unlock Condition | Tier |
|------|--------|-----------------|------|
| **Cat's Paws** | Footstep noise reduced by 30% | Complete an expedition without alerting any enemy | 1 |
| **Shadow Dweller** | Detection range reduced by 20% | Complete 3 extractions without being hit | 2 |
| **Slippery** | When detected, enemies lose track of you 50% faster | Escape from 10 enemy encounters | 2 |
| **Ghost** | Moving while crouched makes no sound and reduces detection range by an additional 15% | Complete a solo extraction without killing any enemies | 3 |

### Stealth Perk Recommendations

**For beginner stealth:** Cat's Paws + Shadow Dweller. Reduces the two biggest stealth failure points: making noise and being spotted too easily.

**For expert stealth (solo):** Ghost + Slippery. Ghost is the ultimate stealth perk but requires a difficult unlock. Pair it with Slippery for a "get out of jail free" combo when stealth breaks.

**Note:** Stealth perks stack with the Mask of Nyarlathotep for near-complete invisibility to standard enemy patrols. Using Ghost + Mask of Nyarlathotep + Cultist Robes makes you effectively undetectable unless you touch an enemy or make a loud sound.

## Utility Perks

| Perk | Effect | Unlock Condition | Tier |
|------|--------|-----------------|------|
| **Pack Mule** | +2 inventory slots | Extract with a full inventory 5 times | 1 |
| **Scavenger** | 25% chance to find additional loot when looting containers | Open 50 containers | 1 |
| **Field Medic** | Healing items restore 25% more HP | Use 30 healing items total | 2 |
| **Artisan** | Crafting speed increased by 50%, crafted items have 20% chance to be duplicate | Craft 50 items at camp | 2 |
| **Quick Exit** | Extraction timer reduced by 15 seconds | Extract successfully 20 times | 3 |

### Utility Perk Recommendations

**For loot farmers:** Pack Mule + Scavenger. More inventory space plus bonus loot means significantly more value per expedition.

**For crafters:** Artisan + Field Medic. Artisan stretches your materials further, and Field Medic makes every healing item more effective.

**For speed runners:** Quick Exit + Pack Mule. Shave time off extraction and carry more loot out in those precious final moments.

## Eldritch Perks

| Perk | Effect | Unlock Condition | Tier |
|------|--------|-----------------|------|
| **Strange Mind** | Start each expedition with +15 Sanity | Complete an expedition with full Sanity | 1 |
| **Whisperer** | Y'm-bhi berserkers are 30% less likely to aggro on you | Survive 5 berseker encounters | 2 |
| **Unsettling Aura** | Nearby Y'm-bhi take 5 Sanity damage per second (yours does not decrease) | Defeat a Y'm-bhi berserker in melee combat | 2 |
| **Eldritch Sight** | See Sanity-affected environmental hazards within 20m | Reach 0 Sanity 5 times and survive each time | 3 |

### Eldritch Perk Recommendations

**For new players dealing with Sanity mechanics:** Strange Mind + Whisperer. The extra starting Sanity and reduced Y'm-bhi aggression give you more room to learn Sanity management.

**For advanced players:** Unsettling Aura + Eldritch Sight. These perks turn Sanity mechanics from a liability into an offensive tool. Unsettling Aura damages enemies near you, and Eldritch Sight helps you navigate Sanity hazards that would otherwise be invisible.

**Caution:** Eldritch Sight requires reaching 0 Sanity five times to unlock. Do not force this — equip base Sanity protection items and let it happen naturally as you learn harder content.

## Perk Unlock Progression Guide

### Early Game (First 10 Expeditions)

**Prioritize unlocking:**
1. Tough Hide (survive 10 expeds)
2. Cat's Paws (one stealthy extract)
3. Blade Master or Marksman (50 kills)
4. Pack Mule (5 full-inventory extracts)

**Equip at most:** 2-3 perks (you need progression to unlock more slots)

### Mid Game (10-30 Expeditions)

**Prioritize unlocking:**
1. Second Wind (low-HP extract)
2. Scavenger (50 containers)
3. Adrenaline Rush (3 rapid kills)
4. Strange Mind (full Sanity extract)

**Equip:** 4-5 perks

### Late Game (30+ Expeditions)

**Prioritize unlocking:**
1. Ghost (no-kill solo extract)
2. Eldritch Sight (0 Sanity 5 times)
3. Quick Exit (20 extracts)
4. Unsettling Aura (melee berserker kill)

**Equip:** 6 perks

## Perk Reset

Perk loadouts can be changed freely between expeditions from the **Camp** menu. There is no cost or penalty for swapping perks. You are encouraged to experiment with different combinations to find what works for your playstyle.

**Note:** Perk unlocks are permanent and account-wide. Once you unlock a perk, it is available on all future characters and expeditions.
