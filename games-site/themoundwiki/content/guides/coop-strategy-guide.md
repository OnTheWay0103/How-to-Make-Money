# The Mound Co-op Strategy Guide — Team Play and Communication

> Launch Version / Updated: July 14, 2026

**The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu** is designed for 1-4 players, but the full experience truly shines with a coordinated squad. The forest rewards teamwork and punishes solo heroics. Whether you are playing with friends or matchmade strangers, this guide will help your squad survive the worst the New World can throw at you.

---

## Squad Roles (4-Player Team)

Assign these roles before every expedition. Players can flex between roles, but clear assignments prevent confusion during combat.

| Role | Primary Responsibility | Best Weapons | Key Items |
|------|----------------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Point Man** | Leads the advance, scouts ahead, identifies threats | Crossbow, Machete | Oil Lamp, Medallion |
| **Support** | Covers rear, provides fire support, watches flanks | Matchlock Arquebus, Pistol | Medallion, Healing Salves |
| **Rearguard** | Protects the squad from pursuit, watches for patrols | Flintlock Pistol, Crossbow | Mask of Nyarlathotep, Oil Lamp |
| **Pack Mule** | Manages Ox Cart, carries extra supplies, loot specialist | Crossbow, Machete | Medallion, Healing Salves |

### Role Responsibilities in Detail

**Point Man**
- Moves 10-15 meters ahead of the group
- Uses Medallion to detect chests and danger
- Marks patrol routes for the squad
- First to engage — opens with Crossbow or backstab
- Carries the primary light source

**Support**
- Stays in the middle of the formation
- Watches the Point Man's blind spots
- Provides suppressing fire when the squad is ambushed
- Calls out Sanity hallucinations for confirmation
- Carries backup healing items for the squad

**Rearguard**
- Watches behind the squad at all times
- Uses Mask of Nyarlathotep to delay pursuing enemies
- Closes doors or creates obstacles to block patrol paths
- Warns the squad if enemies are approaching from behind

**Pack Mule**
- Manages the Ox Cart route and timing
- Tracks total loot value per player
- Decides what to keep and what to discard
- Carries extra ammo and healing supplies

## Loadout Allocation Strategy

### The "No Gaps" Principle
Every expedition needs:
1. **Silent kill capability** — At least 2 Crossbows or Machetes
2. **Area denial** — At least 1 Oil Lamp or fire source
3. **Healing coverage** — Every player carries 1+ healing item
4. **Loot detection** — At least 1 Medallion in the squad
5. **Emergency stealth** — At least 1 Mask of Nyarlathotep

### Example Balanced Loadout
| Player | Primary | Secondary | Tool 1 | Tool 2 |
|--------|---------|-----------|--------|--------|
| Point Man | Crossbow | Machete | Oil Lamp | Medallion |
| Support | Matchlock Arquebus | Flintlock Pistol | Healing Salves | Oil Lamp |
| Rearguard | Flintlock Pistol | Crossbow | Mask of Nyarlathotep | Medallion |
| Pack Mule | Crossbow | Machete | Medallion | Healing Salves |

## Spatial Voice Chat — Communication Tactics

The Mound uses **Spatial Voice Chat**: the distance and direction between players affects how clearly you hear each other. This is not a bug — it is a core gameplay mechanic.

### How Spatial Voice Works

| Distance | Audio Quality | Tactical Implication |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|
| 0-5 meters | Crystal clear | Normal conversation range |
| 5-15 meters | Slightly muffled | Can communicate but must speak louder |
| 15-30 meters | Distant, hard to understand | Use pre-arranged signals or pings |
| 30+ meters | Inaudible | You cannot hear teammates at all |

### Communication Tips

1. **Stay in visual range.** If you can see a teammate, your voice will reach them.
2. **Use pre-arranged callouts.** Agree on shorthand before deploying:
   - "Contact front / left / right / rear" = enemy direction
   - "Fake" = hallucination, confirm visually
   - "Mask on / off" = Nyarlathotep activation
   - "Pulling back" = retreating to previous position
   - "Ox Cart" = regrouping at the cart
3. **Use pings as backup.** If voice is not reaching, use the ping system. A Medallion ping is audible at longer range than voice.
4. **Do not shout over each other.** In combat, one person calls the shots. Designate a squad leader before the expedition.
5. **Headphones are strongly recommended.** Spatial audio works best with stereo or surround headphones.

## Helping Teammates Who Are Losing Sanity

Sanity is individual, but its effects are felt by the whole squad.

### Signs a Teammate Is Low on Sanity

| Sign | What to Do |
|------|-----------|
| They call out things no one else sees | Gently tell them it is a hallucination. Ask them to stay still. |
| They shoot at nothing | Tell them to holster their weapon and move toward you. |
| They wander off alone | Follow them, keep talking, keep them oriented. |
| They attack a teammate | This is a hallucination. Do not shoot back — use non-lethal methods (push, body block). |
| They are unresponsive | They may be experiencing a deep hallucination event. Physically guide them. |

### Sanity Rescue Procedure

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Identify the affected player |
| 2 | Move close (within 5 meters) so voice is clear |
| 3 | Tell them they are hallucinating — use their name |
| 4 | Ask them to stop and look at you |
| 5 | Administer a Sanity-restoring item if available |
| 6 | Keep them in the center of the formation until they recover |

## Revival Mechanics

### Player Down States

| State | Trigger | Recovery |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Downed (Incapacitated) | HP reaches 0 | Teammate can revive with 8-second channel |
| Bleeding Out | Downed for 30 seconds without revival | Permanent death |
| Corpsed | Death confirmed | Body is consumed by the forest |
| Corrupted | Forest consumes the body | Corpses revive as AI-controlled corrupted version of the player |

### The Corrupted Teammate Threat

When a player dies and their body is consumed by the forest, they respawn as an **AI-controlled Corrupted version** that hunts the surviving squad members.

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| Appearance | Corrupted version of the dead player's character |
| Behavior | Actively hunts surviving players |
| Abilities | Retains some weapon abilities from the original loadout |
| Health | Increased compared to living version |
| Weakness | Same as Y'm-bhi (light sensitive) |

### Revival Priority

1. **Extraction is close (<5 minutes):** Only revive if safe. Otherwise, extract and let them respawn on the Galleon.
2. **Extraction is far (>10 minutes):** Revive. Losing a player for the whole expedition cripples the squad.
3. **Under attack:** Clear all enemies first. Reviving while under fire is suicide.
4. **Low Sanity squad:** Consider whether the revived player will immediately fall to hallucinations again.

## Advanced Team Tactics

### The "Turtle" (Defensive Formation)
Use when surrounded or at extraction point.
- Squad forms a tight circle
- All members face outward
- Each player covers a 90-degree arc
- Point Man calls targets
- Support and Rearguard watch for approaching enemies

### The "Ghost" (Stealth Formation)
Use when navigating high-density enemy zones.
- Single file, 5 meters apart
- Point Man leads, marks enemies
- All movement is crouched
- No guns unless absolutely necessary
- Mask of Nyarlathotep used for close passes

### The "Hammer and Anvil" (Combat Formation)
Use when engaging a strong enemy or group.
- Support and Rearguard hold position ("Anvil")
- Point Man flanks ("Hammer")
- Anvil draws aggro with noise
- Hammer strikes from behind with backstabs
