## Developer Studio Background

Mistfall Hunter is developed by **Duskfall Interactive**, an independent game studio based in Europe. The studio was founded in 2021 by a team of former AAA developers who previously worked on titles at CD Projekt Red, Ubisoft, and Guerrilla Games.

| **Previous Experience** | CD Projekt Red, Ubisoft, Guerrilla Games |
| **First Title** | Mistfall Hunter |
| **Engine** | Unreal Engine 5 |

### Developer Philosophy

Duskfall Interactive has stated three core principles for Mistfall Hunter:

1. **Extraction done right** — meaningful risk and reward without excessive time investment (targeting 20-30 minute extraction sessions)
2. **Skill-based combat** — no pay-to-win mechanics; all cosmetics and battle pass only
3. **Community-first development** — playtest feedback directly shapes the roadmap

The team has emphasized that Mistfall Hunter is designed as a **live-service game** with regular content seasons, rather than a one-and-done release.

### Early Development Details

The game spent roughly 18 months in pre-production while the team built the core extraction loop in Unreal Engine 5. The vertical slice shown in early 2024 featured a single map (Ashfall Ruins) with three classes and received positive press attention for its fluid combat system and atmospheric art direction.

The closed beta in late 2024 helped the team identify major balance issues, particularly with the Sorcerer class being over-tuned and the Shadowstrix being under-powered in PvP. Both were significantly reworked before the playtest waves.

### Discord Server Structure

The official Discord server is the most active community space with:

- **#playtest-chat** — general discussion for active playtesters
- **#bug-reports** — report issues with reproduction steps
- **#feedback** — structured feedback threads per patch
- **#lfg** — find squadmates by region and playstyle
- **#media** — share clips, screenshots, and fan art
- **#dev-qa** — periodic developer Q&A sessions (announced in #announcements)
- **#roadmap-discussion** — talk about upcoming features

Developers are actively engaged in the Discord, particularly in the feedback and bug-report channels. Response times from the community team are typically within 24 hours during weekdays.

### How to Get Developer Attention

1. **Bug reports** — use the #bug-reports channel with a clear title, reproduction steps, and screenshots if possible
2. **Balance feedback** — post in #feedback. Specific, data-backed feedback (e.g., "Mercenary shield block has 70% damage reduction which makes them unkillable in 1v1") is more likely to be read than general complaints
3. **Feature requests** — the team reviews #feedback weekly and compiles popular requests
4. **Dev Q&A sessions** — announced in #announcements. These are the best opportunity to directly ask the development team questions

### Developer Communication Style

Duskfall Interactive maintains a transparent communication policy. Key practices:

- **Monthly dev diaries** on YouTube covering what the team worked on that month
- **Public Trello / roadmap board** — viewable via their Discord
- **Post-patch notes** with detailed balance change reasoning
- **Known issues lists** updated in real-time on the Discord
- **No NDA on playtest** — players are free to stream, record, and discuss the game
- **Regular Q&A sessions** — approximately once per month during active development

The studio has explicitly stated that they prefer to under-promise and over-deliver, so roadmap dates should be treated as targets rather than guarantees. When delays occur, they communicate the reasons openly.
