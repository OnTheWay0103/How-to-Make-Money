## Is Solo Play Viable?

**Yes.** Mistfall Hunter fully supports solo play, but it plays differently from squad play. You are always at a numerical disadvantage — 1v3 is possible but very difficult. The key is playing smart, not playing hard.

## Best Classes for Solo

| Class | Solo Rating | Why |
|-------|------------|-----|
| **Shadowstrix** | S | Stealth lets you pick fights. Ambush → kill → extract. Best solo class. |
| **Mercenary** | A | Survivability and block mechanics keep you alive. Can outlast ambushes. |
| **Sorcerer** | A | Kill things before they reach you. Glacial Blink escapes bad situations. |
| **Withered Knight** | B | Strong 1v1 but struggles when third-partied. |
| **Blackarrow** | C | Difficult in close-quarters. Open-map only. |
| **Seer** | D | Do not play Seer solo. No escape, no damage, no chance. |

## Solo Mindset: Risk Management

The #1 difference between solo and squad play is **risk tolerance**:

| Situation | Squad Decision | Solo Decision |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Hear gunfire nearby | Push — you have numbers | Avoid — you are outnumbered |
| See 2+ players | Engage — 3v2 is favorable | Hide or leave — 1v2 is not |
| Find high-value loot | Fight for it | Take it and extract immediately |
| Third-partied while fighting Woodling | One fights, two peel | Disengage and find another exit |
| Late in raid, mist closing | Push through contested zones | Find the safest path, even if longer |

**Solo rule #1**: An extracted green item is worth more than a purple item in your death bag.

## Solo Strategy by Phase

### Phase 1: Spawn (First 2 Minutes)

- Spawn → immediately identify where you are on the map
- Move away from spawn toward cover — spawn points are PvP hotspots
- Your goal: reach a low-traffic zone and gear up on Corroded
- **Do not** rush the Returner Woodling zone — it is the most dangerous area early

### Phase 2: Gearing (Minutes 2-8)

- Farm Corroded camps in low-traffic areas
- Open chests, loot buildings, build a baseline kit
- Listen for player activity — gunshots, spells, footsteps
- If you find a blue+ item early, consider extracting immediately with it
- Goal: have a functional loadout (green/blue weapon + armor + consumables)

### Phase 3: Woodling Decision (Minutes 8-12)

This is the critical decision point:

**Option A: Hunt the Woodling**
- Scout the Woodling zone from a distance
- If it is quiet or only 1-2 players are fighting it: wait for them to weaken it, then steal the kill
- Shadowstrix excels here — stealth in, ambush the weakened player, finish the Woodling, take Soul, extract

**Option B: Find Alternative Exit**
- If the Woodling zone is a warzone: skip it entirely
- Head to a fixed exit (no Soul required)
- Accept that you will have less loot this run — survival > greed

**Option C: Loot and Extract Early**
- If you already have good loot: just leave
- Use a fixed exit or kill the Woodling if it is nearby and unguarded
- Do not push deeper just because the raid timer has time left

### Phase 4: Extraction (Minutes 12-15)

- Plan your extraction route at least 2 minutes before the mist closes
- Scout the extraction point from cover before approaching
- If occupied: wait. If clear: channel immediately.
- Channel tip: face the most likely approach direction while channeling — you can see enemies coming

## Dealing with Squads as a Solo

**Do not fight squads head-on.** You will lose.

Instead:
- **Third-party**: Wait for a squad to engage another group, then pick off stragglers
- **Pick the healer**: If you must fight a squad, kill the Seer first. Without support, the rest crumble.
- **Hit and run**: Kill one, take their loot, disappear. Do not stay for the full squad wipe.
- **Use the environment**: Narrow corridors, elevation changes, and PvE enemies all create chaos you can exploit

## Sound and Awareness

As a solo player, **audio is your most important tool**:
- Gunshots = player activity. Note the direction and distance.
- Spell casting = Sorcerer nearby. They are fragile and often alone.
- Footsteps = someone close. Freeze and listen.
- Woodling chime = the boss is being fought. Intel on enemy position.

**Play with headphones.** Stereo audio is essential for tracking player positions.

## Solo Loadout Recommendations

### Budget Run (Low Risk)
- Green weapon + green chest armor
- 2 healing potions
- 1 stamina potion
- Goal: extract with any profit. Cost of loadout < 100 Gyldenblood.

### Standard Run (Medium Risk)
- Blue weapon + blue chest/helmet
- 3 healing potions + 2 stamina potions + bandages
- Goal: farm Corroded camps and extract with blue+ loot

### High-Stakes Run (High Risk)
- Purple weapon + purple armor (3+ pieces)
- Full consumable set
- Goal: hunt the Woodling, contest high-value zones, extract with epic loot

**Never risk more than you can afford to lose.** If your stash has 500 Gyldenblood, do not bring a 400 GB loadout. The math stays simple.

## Recovering from Loss Streaks

Everyone goes broke sometimes. Recovery plan:
1. Do 3 budget runs with green gear — extract as soon as you find anything
2. Sell excess green items to the Shop
3. Use Gyldenblood to upgrade Warehouse (more slots = more gear can be stored)
4. After 3 successful extractions, return to standard runs

## Quick Solo Tips

- **Stealth is your best friend** — move quietly, avoid unnecessary combat
- **The first 5 minutes dictate the run** — if you find good loot early, play conservatively
- **Extraction points are the most dangerous locations** — treat them like boss fights
- **Learn the alternative exits** — they save runs when the Woodling zone is a warzone
- **Patience wins** — waiting 30 seconds for enemies to leave an extraction point is better than a 50/50 fight
- **You do not need to kill the Woodling every run** — alternative exits + modest loot = profit
- **Record your deaths** — every death is a lesson. What could you have done differently?
