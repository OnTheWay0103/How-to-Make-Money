## Introduction

Mistfall Hunter is punishing. Every death costs you the gear you brought — and the loot you found. New players lose hundreds of thousands of Gyldenblood to preventable mistakes.

Here are the 10 most common beginner errors and exactly how to fix them.

---

## Mistake #1: Hoarding Gear You Never Use

### The Problem
Your warehouse fills up with "maybe someday" gear. Green items, blue items for classes you don't play, weapons with wrong affixes — it all sits there, unused and unsold.

### The Cost
- A full stash means you cannot store valuable new loot
- Items lose value over time as the market shifts
- You are sitting on Gyldenblood that could be funding upgrades

### The Fix
Adopt the **3-rule system**:
1. If you haven't used it in 5 extractions → vendor or AH it
2. If it's for a class you don't play → sell it
3. If it's green and not perfect affixes → vendor it instantly

**Apply this rule now**: Open your warehouse. Pick 10 items you haven't used. Sell them. Reinvest the Gold into upgrades.

---

## Mistake #2: Bringing Your Best Gear Every Run

### The Problem
You equip your single purple weapon and best armor for every extraction. Then you die to a bad engagement or a third-party ambush. Your best gear is gone.

### The Cost
- One death can set you back 3-5 extractions of progress
- High gear fear makes you play worse — you are nervous, hesitant, and make bad decisions

### The Fix
**The 30% rule**: Never bring gear that represents more than 30% of your total net worth.

- Have one "serious" loadout for when you are playing focused
- Have two "budget" loadouts (green/blue) for practice, learning maps, and warming up
- If you extract 3 times in a row with a budget loadout, then equip the good gear

---

## Mistake #3: Not Banking Loot Between Extractions

### The Problem
You extract with a bag full of loot, queue up immediately, and die — losing everything you just extracted with.

### The Cost
- Hours of progress evaporated
- The best items you found are gone forever

### The Fix
Build the **post-extraction habit**:
1. Extract
2. Open warehouse
3. Deposit ALL valuable items (instantly)
4. Equip next loadout
5. Then queue

Make this a ritual. Never queue with loot from a previous run still in your inventory.

---

## Mistake #4: Fighting Every PvP Encounter

### The Problem
You see another player and immediately engage. Even when you are carrying valuable loot. Even when you are low on health. Even when the third party is definitely coming.

### The Cost
According to game data, **62% of deaths while carrying high-value loot happen in avoidable PvP fights**.

### The Fix
Ask yourself three questions before engaging:
1. "What am I carrying?" — If the answer is "a lot," disengage.
2. "Do I know where their teammates are?" — If no, assume they have backup.
3. "Can I disengage if this goes wrong?" — If you are in a dead-end room, don't start a fight.

**Rule of thumb**: If you have more than 1500 Gyldenblood worth of loot on you, your primary goal is extraction, not PvP.

---

## Mistake #5: Ignoring the Mist Timer

### The Problem
You are deep in the map, looting one more chest. The mist is closing. You check the timer — 3 minutes. You still need to cross half the map to extract.

### The Cost
Death. Full loot loss. The mist damage scales exponentially — you cannot outrun it.

### The Fix
**The 5-minute rule**: When the timer hits 5 minutes, stop looting and start moving toward an extraction point.

- Know where all extraction points are before you need them
- Do not engage fights after the 5-minute mark — disengage and run
- If you are far from any extraction point at 4 minutes, you will likely die — plan better next run

## Mistake #6: Over-Upgrading Trash Gear

### The Problem
You find a green weapon and spend 800 Gyldenblood upgrading it. Now it is a green+3 weapon. It still performs worse than a blue weapon that costs 400 Gyldenblood on the Auction House.

### The Cost
You spent more on upgrades than the weapon is worth. The upgrade Gyldenblood is gone forever — you cannot recover it.

### The Fix
| Gear Quality | Upgrade? |
|-------------|---------|
| Common | Never |
| Green | Only if perfect affixes and you are early game |
| Blue | Yes, to +2 if good affixes |
| Purple | Yes, to +3 |
| Legendary | Yes, max upgrade |

**General rule**: If the upgrade cost exceeds 50% of the item's market value, do not upgrade it. Sell it and buy something better.

## Mistake #7: Playing the Wrong Class for Your Experience

### The Problem
You picked Sorcerer or Shadowstrix because they "look cool." You die constantly. The class is not forgiving enough for a new player.

### The Cost
Dying 4-5 times per extraction means you spend more time waiting in queue and regearing than actually playing the game.

### The Fix
Start with **Mercenary**. Here is the progression path:

| Extractions | Class | Why |
|------------|-------|-----|
| 1-15 | Mercenary | Learn maps, extraction loops, enemy patterns |
| 15-30 | Blackarrow or Withered Knight | Learn positioning and timing |
| 30+ | Sorcerer or Shadowstrix | Now you know enough to play fragile classes |

There is no shame in playing Mercenary for your first 20 hours. The top players still play Mercenary in ranked.

## Mistake #8: Not Using Your Consumables

### The Problem
You carry healing potions, bandages, and utility items into the map — and extract with all of them unused. You are saving them for "when you really need them."

### The Cost
- Consumables take up inventory space
- You die because you didn't heal
- Unused consumables are wasted

### The Fix
**Use potions at 60% HP, not 20% HP.** If you wait until 20%, you might get bursted before the heal completes.

- Bind healing potions to an easy key (Q or mouse button)
- Use one healing item per PvE engagement as a habit
- Consider consumables as "temporary inventory space" — use them or lose them

---

## Mistake #9: Ignoring Camp Upgrades for Cosmetic Purchases

### The Problem
You spend your first 5000 Gyldenblood on a weapon skin or cosmetic item. Your camp is still level 1. You have no passive income, no storage, and no healing bonuses.

### The Cost
Camp upgrades multiply your efficiency. A level 3 Goddess Statue gives +15% Gyldenblood find rate. A level 5 Warehouse triples your storage. Cosmetics give 0 gameplay benefit.

### The Fix
**Camp upgrades first, cosmetics never** (well, maybe much later). Priority order:

1. Goddess Statue → level 3 (core progression unlock)
2. Warehouse → level 2 (more storage = more loot per session)
3. Gyldenblod Pool → level 2 (healing bonuses save potions)
4. Tavern → level 1 (passive buffs)
5. Then, and only then, consider cosmetics

---

## Mistake #10: Going Solo Into High-Stakes Content

### The Problem
You drop into Obsidian Depths solo with purple gear because "you can handle it." You get jumped by a 3-player squad 2 minutes in. You die. Your gear is gone.

### The Cost
Complete loss of your best loadout. Potential tilt. Potentially quitting the game.

### The Fix
| Content | Minimum Party |
|---------|--------------|
| Ruined Catacombs | Solo fine |
| Forsaken Hollow | Solo fine (careful in center) |
| Mistveil Forest | Duo recommended |
| The Bloodfen | Duo recommended |
| Obsidian Depths | Trio required |

**The solo rule**: If the map has a "Very Hard" difficulty tag, do not go solo unless you are willing to lose everything you bring.

If you must solo, run budget gear and prioritize stealth and avoidance over combat.

## Fastest Growth Path

1. **Extractions 1-10**: Mercenary, green gear, Ruined Catacombs only. Goal: extract alive.
2. **Extractions 11-30**: Learn Forsaken Hollow. Start banking blue gear. Upgrade camp to level 3.
3. **Extractions 31-60**: Try Mistveil Forest. Run blue gear. Join a duo. Kill a boss.
4. **Extractions 61-100**: Purple gear. All maps. Start PvPing intentionally. Observe the meta.
5. **100+**: You are no longer a beginner. Read the season guide and tier list. Push leaderboard.
