## Loot and Rarity Guide — Item Tiers, Drop Systems, and Farming Routes

Mistfall Hunter uses a tiered loot system where item quality directly affects stats, bonuses, and overall value. Understanding the rarity tiers, how loot drops are calculated, and where to farm specific tiers is the difference between extracting with vendor-trash and extracting with endgame gear.

---

## Rarity Tiers Overview

| Tier | Color | Stat Bonus vs Previous Tier | Estimated Drop Rate | Can Be Crafted? |
|------|-------|----------------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Common** | White | Baseline | 55% | Yes |
| **Uncommon** | Green | +10-15% | 25% | Yes |
| **Rare** | Blue | +20-30% | 12% | Yes (with schematic) |
| **Epic** | Purple | +35-50% | 6% | Yes (with schematic + rare mats) |
| **Legendary** | Gold/Orange | +60-100% | 2% | No (drop only) |

Each tier adds a random suffix roll that modifies the item's behavior. Higher tiers roll from a larger pool of suffixes and can roll multiple suffixes simultaneously.

---

## Suffix Modifiers by Tier

Suffixes are bonus properties attached to weapons and armor. The number of suffixes increases with rarity:

| Rarity | Min Suffixes | Max Suffixes | Examples |
|--------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Common | 0 | 0 | None |
| Uncommon | 1 | 1 | "of the Bear" (+5 HP), "of Accuracy" (+3% hit chance) |
| Rare | 1 | 2 | "of the Bear" + "of Protection" (+3% armor) |
| Epic | 2 | 3 | "of Power" (+5% damage) + "of the Bear" + "of Protection" |
| Legendary | 3 | 4 | Fixed unique name with set bonuses |

### Notable Suffix Pool

| Suffix | Effect | Weight |
|--------|--------|--------|

| of Power | +3-8% Weapon Damage | Common |

## Loot Source Breakdown

### Enemy Drops

| Enemy Type | Common Drops | Rarity Ceiling | Best Farming Location |
|------------|-------------|----------------|----------------------|
| Corroded Warrior | Common weapons, cloth scraps | Uncommon | Starting zone, spawn area |
| Corroded Archer | Common bows, arrows, leather | Uncommon | Starting zone outskirts |
| Returner Woodling | Wood, common armor | Rare | Middle of the map, forest POIs |
| Mist Wraith | Magic items, mana potions | Epic | Mist-heavy zones near center |
| Elite (Glowing) Corroded | Guaranteed Rare+ item | Epic | Random spawn, higher near boss rooms |
| Boss (World/Instance) | Guaranteed Epic+ item | Legendary | Boss rooms, event zones |

### Chests and Containers

| Container Type | Loot Pool | Min Rarity | Key Locations |
|---------------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Wooden Crate | Common materials, potions | Common | Scattered everywhere |
| Iron Chest | Weapons, armor, gold | Uncommon | Guarded rooms, camp interiors |
| Locked Chest (requires key) | Rare-Epic items | Rare | Locked rooms, guarded by elites |
| Supply Drop | Mixed, high material value | Uncommon | Airdrop events, marked on map |
| Boss Cache | Guaranteed Epic+, currency | Epic | After boss kill |
| Hidden Cache | Random high-tier item | Rare | Secret rooms, puzzles |

### Keys and Locked Chests

Locked chests offer the most reliable source of Rare+ items.

| Key Type | Found On | Used For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Rusty Key | Corroded elites (5% drop) | Iron Chests with blue lock |
| Steel Key | Mini-bosses (20% drop) | Rare chests with silver lock |
| Ancient Key | Bosses (50% drop) | Epic vaults, hidden rooms |
| Master Key | Crafted (3 Ancient Keys) | Opens any lock |

**Strategy:** Always carry at least one Rusty Key. Locked chests are common enough that you will encounter them in most runs, and their minimum Uncommon loot floor makes them worth the inventory slot.

## Loot Value Assessment

Not all items of the same rarity are equally valuable. Use this priority system when deciding what to extract:

### Extract Priority (High to Low)

| Priority | Item Type | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| 1 | Legendary weapon/armor | Irreplaceable, build-defining |
| 2 | Epic weapon with good suffixes | Direct power upgrade |
| 3 | Rare armor with 2 good suffixes | Endgame viable |
| 4 | Gold / Currency stacks | Always useful, no RNG |
| 5 | Crafting materials (rare) | Needed for schematics |
| 6 | Keys | Future loot access |
| 7 | Uncommon items with perfect suffixes | Situationally valuable |
| 8 | Uncommon items with mediocre suffixes | Salvage for materials |
| 9 | Common items | Leave behind unless you have empty slots |

### When to Leave Loot Behind

- **Common weapons** when you already have an Uncommon or better equipped — the stat difference is meaningful enough that swapping is not worth the inventory space
- **Uncommon armor** with only 1 bad suffix (e.g., "of the Hedgehog" — reflect 2 damage) — the 2-3 inventory slots are better used for materials
- **Any duplicate** of a weapon type you already have in the same tier — unless the suffix is strictly better

## Best Farming Routes by Rarity

### Common / Uncommon Farming (Early Game)

**Route:** Spawn point → Corroded Warrior camp (South) → Forest edge → Returner Woodling patrol

- **Focus:** Kill every Corroded Warrior pack you see. They respawn quickly and drop Common-Uncommon gear consistently
- **Expected haul per run:** 3-5 Common items, 1-2 Uncommon items, crafting materials
- **Extract condition:** Full inventory or after 10 minutes, whichever comes first
- **Risk:** Very low — starting zone has no player traffic and weak enemies

### Rare / Epic Farming (Mid Game)

**Route:** Central POI (check for Supply Drop) → Locked room (use Rusty Key) → Mini-boss camp → Hidden cache near waterfall

- **Focus:** Mini-bosses and locked chests are your best Rare source. Skip common enemy packs
- **Expected haul per run:** 1 Rare+ item, 2-3 Uncommon items, possibly a key
- **Extract condition:** After killing the mini-boss or finding the hidden cache
- **Risk:** Medium — other players farm these same routes

### Legendary Farming (Late Game)

**Route:** World boss spawn (check timer) → Elite Corroded patrols (South Central) → Epic vault (need Ancient Key) → Mist Wraith cluster (center mist)

- **Focus:** Boss kills are the only guaranteed Legendary source. Elite Corroded have the highest non-boss drop rate for Legendaries (estimated 1.5%)
- **Expected haul per run:** 1 guaranteed Epic from boss, occasional Legendary
- **Extract condition:** Immediately after boss kill — third-party teams will converge on the boss room
- **Risk:** Very high — boss rooms attract the entire lobby

## Item Degradation and "Stash It or Cash It"

### Degradation on Death

When you die in an expedition:

- **Equipped items:** Lose 10% durability. Break at 0% durability and are destroyed
- **Inventory items (not equipped):** Dropped on the ground. Other players can loot them
- **Stashed items (in camp):** Safe. Never lost

### Repair Costs vs. Replacement Value

| Rarity | Repair Cost | Replacement Cost | Verdict |
|--------|-------------|------------------|---------|
| Common | 10 Gold | Free (crafted) | Replace, don't repair |
| Uncommon | 25 Gold | 50 Gold | Repair once, replace after |
| Rare | 75 Gold | 200 Gold + materials | Repair until durability is below 40% |
| Epic | 200 Gold | 500+ Gold + rare mats | Always repair |
| Legendary | 500 Gold | Irreplaceable | Always repair |

**Rule of thumb:** If the repair cost exceeds 30% of the item's replacement cost, replace it. The only exception is Legendary items, which should always be repaired regardless of cost.
