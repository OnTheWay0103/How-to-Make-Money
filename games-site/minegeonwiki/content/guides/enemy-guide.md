## Enemy Overview

Enemies in MineGeon: Renegades are divided into three categories: **basic enemies**, **elite variants**, and **environmental hazards**. Each biome introduces new enemy types with distinct attack patterns. Understanding enemy behavior is essential for successful extractions — especially on deeper runs where mistakes are punishing.

## Biome 1: Shallow Mines

### Crawler
The most common enemy. A small, scuttling creature that charges directly at you.

- **Attack:** Melee charge (telegraphed by a brief pause)
- **Strategy:** Sidestep and counter. Crawlers have low health — two hits from any weapon kills them.
- **Elite Variant:** Glowing Crawler — larger, faster, explodes on death. Keep distance when health is low.

### Spitter
A ranged enemy that fires acid projectiles.

- **Attack:** Launches 3 acid orbs in a spread pattern
- **Strategy:** Close the gap quickly — Spitters are weak in melee range. Strafe left or right to dodge orbs.
- **Elite Variant:** Toxic Spitter — orbs leave lingering acid pools on the ground.

## Biome 2: Fungal Caverns

### Fungling
Small mushroom creatures that burrow and ambush.

- **Attack:** Burrows underground, then erupts beneath you
- **Strategy:** Watch for moving ground distortions. When you see one, move immediately. Funglings are vulnerable for 2 seconds after surfacing.
- **Elite Variant:** Spore Fungling — releases a spore cloud on death that deals damage over time.

### Root Weaver
A medium-sized enemy that summons damaging roots from the ground.

- **Attack:** Summons root spikes in a line toward you
- **Strategy:** Keep moving laterally. The roots travel in a straight line — never stand still. The Weaver itself is slow.
- **Elite Variant:** Thorn Weaver — roots are wider and deal bleed damage.

## Biome 3: Magma Chambers

### Emberling
Fire-infused creatures that explode on contact.

- **Attack:** Rushes toward you and self-destructs in a fire AoE
- **Strategy:** Ranged attacks are safest. If playing melee, time your dodge through the explosion — i-frames prevent all damage.
- **Elite Variant:** Magma Emberling — leaves a lava pool after exploding.

### Lava Golem
Large, slow enemies with devastating melee attacks.

- **Attack:** Ground slam creating a shockwave, plus a slow sweeping arm swipe
- **Strategy:** Attack from behind after dodging the shockwave. Golems turn slowly — use this to your advantage. Weak to ice-based damage if available.
- **Elite Variant:** Obsidian Golem — armored front; only attacks from behind deal full damage.

## Biome 4: Crystal Mines

### Crystal Shard
Floating crystal constructs that fire piercing lasers.

- **Attack:** Fires a beam laser that pierces through all obstacles
- **Strategy:** Break line of sight using terrain. The laser has a 2-second charge animation — use that window to reposition behind cover.
- **Elite Variant:** Prism Shard — fires three simultaneous lasers in a fan pattern.

### Gem Wasp
Fast flying enemies that swarm in groups.

- **Attack:** Dive-bomb attack followed by a rapid sting combo
- **Strategy:** Area-of-effect weapons excel here. Tank class can taunt and absorb the swarm while allies clean up. Don't let them surround you.
- **Elite Variant:** Queen Wasp — summons smaller Wasps. Kill the Queen first.

## Biome 5: Abandoned Mineshaft

### Drill bot
Mechanical enemies with drill arms.

- **Attack:** Charges forward with spinning drills, then performs a 360-degree spin attack
- **Strategy:** Dodge to the side during the charge. Back away during the spin attack — its range is short but the damage is high.
- **Elite Variant:** Chainsaw Bot — spin attack lasts twice as long.

### Wraith
Ghostly enemies that phase through walls.

- **Attack:** Teleports behind you and attacks, or fires a slowing projectile
- **Strategy:** Watch for the purple glow that signals a teleport. Turn and attack immediately. The Wraith is fragile but unpredictable.
- **Elite Variant:** Shadow Wraith — invisible until it attacks. Listen for the audio cue.

## Biome 6: The Deep Core

### Void Spawn
Aberrant creatures from the deepest depths.

- **Attack:** Gravity pull (pulls you toward it) followed by a massive melee swipe
- **Strategy:** Break the pull by dodging perpendicular to the tether. Once free, keep medium distance and use ranged attacks.
- **Elite Variant:** Corrupted Spawn — leaves a damage field around itself at all times.

### Core Guardian
The biome's powerful sentry enemies.

- **Attack:** Fires a continuous beam that tracks your movement, plus summons smaller Void Spawn
- **Strategy:** Use pillars and terrain to break the beam's line of sight. Kill summoned spawns quickly to avoid being overwhelmed.
- **Elite Variant:** Ancient Guardian — beam splits into two after 5 seconds. Requires near-perfect movement.

## Combat Tips

1. **Learn attack tells** — Every enemy has a distinct pause or glow before attacking. Master these and you'll rarely take hits.
2. **Positioning matters** — Don't let enemies surround you. Back against a wall or use chokepoints.
3. **Use terrain** — Pillars, walls, and elevation changes can block ranged attacks and break tracking.
4. **Prioritize elites** — In mixed groups, kill elite enemies first. Their buffs affect nearby basic enemies.
5. **Match damage type** — Some enemies have resistances. Experiment with different weapons if your current one feels ineffective.
6. **Co-op coordination** — In multiplayer, designate roles: one player tanks/distracts while others deal damage.
