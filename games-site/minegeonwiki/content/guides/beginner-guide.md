## Getting Started in MineGeon: Renegades

MineGeon: Renegades throws you into a dangerous underground world where you'll mine for resources, fight enemies, and extract before the mine collapses. This beginner guide covers everything you need to know to survive your first few runs.

## Choosing Your First Class

When you start MineGeon, you'll choose from three classes. For beginners:

- **Tank** — Easiest to start with. High health, good defense, melee-focused. Forgiving when you make mistakes.
- **DPS Gunslinger** — Medium difficulty. Ranged damage dealer with high mobility. Great for learning enemy patterns from a safe distance.
- **Engineer** — Advanced. Uses turrets, traps, and gadgets. Rewarding but requires more game knowledge.

**Recommendation:** Start with Tank or Gunslinger to learn the basics before trying Engineer.

## Your First Run: Step by Step

### Step 1: Prepare at Base
Before entering the mine, check your:
- **Equipment loadout** — Make sure you have a pickaxe and weapon equipped
- **Consumables** — Bring at least 2-3 health items
- **Objective** — Know what you're going in for (resources, boss kill, exploration)

### Step 2: Enter the Mine
Once you drop in:
1. **Start mining** immediately — gather basic ores (Iron, Copper) from nearby walls
2. **Watch your minimap** — It shows nearby resources, enemies, and the extraction point
3. **Check the stability meter** — This shows how much time you have before the mine collapses

### Step 3: Fighting Enemies
- **Basic enemies** (Crawlers, Spitters) — Dodge their telegraphed attacks and counter
- **Elite enemies** (Glowing variants) — Drop better loot but hit much harder
- **Always keep distance** if playing Gunslinger; stay close and block if Tank

### Step 4: Extraction
- The extraction point is marked on your map from the start
- You must reach it before the stability meter hits zero
- If the mine collapses while you're inside, you lose everything you gathered
- **Golden rule:** Don't get greedy. Extract when you have valuable loot

## Early Game Priorities

### What to Mine First
1. **Iron Ore** — Used for basic tools and weapons
2. **Copper Ore** — Used for wiring and Engineer gadgets
3. **Coal** — Fuel for base upgrades
4. **Gems** (rare) — High value, prioritize extracting with these

### What to Upgrade First at Base
1. **Pickaxe** — Mine faster = more resources per run
2. **Storage** — Hold more loot before needing to extract
3. **Workbench** — Unlock better crafting recipes

## Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Going too deep too fast** — Deeper biomes have tougher enemies. Build up gear first.
2. **Ignoring the stability meter** — It's easy to lose track. Set a mental timer.
3. **Not bringing enough healing** — Always over-prepare consumables.
4. **Skipping the minimap** — The minimap is your best friend for navigation.
5. **Fighting everything** — Sometimes running past enemies is the smart play, especially when stability is low.

## First Boss Tips

Your first boss encounter will likely be in Biome 2. Key tips:
- Learn the boss's attack pattern before committing to damage
- Most bosses have 3-4 distinct attacks with clear tells
- Bring at least 5 healing items for your first boss attempt
- Co-op makes bosses easier — they don't scale proportionally

## Next Steps

After 3-5 successful extractions, you should:
- Have a basic base set up with a few upgrades
- Understand your class's core abilities
- Be ready to tackle deeper biomes and tougher bosses
- Start experimenting with crafting to make better gear
