## Why Play Rapier?

Rapier is the **highest DPS weapon** in Echoes of Aincrad when mastered. It combines long reach, fast attacks, and a parry counter system that creates massive damage windows. In the right hands, no other weapon matches its solo potential.

## Core Mechanics

### Parry Counter

The Rapier's defining feature. When an enemy attack flashes **blue**, you are in the parry window. A successful parry:
- Negates all damage
- Staggers the enemy briefly
- Opens a **counter window** (enemy takes +40% damage for 3 seconds)

The flow: **Watch for blue flash → Parry → Counter window → Sword Skill combo.**

### Linear Thrust Combo

Your bread-and-butter attack chain. Fast, long reach, builds combo meter.

### Star Splash

Multi-hit Sword Skill. Use during counter windows for maximum damage. Each hit can crit independently, making it devastating with high Dexterity.

## Stat Priority

| Stat | Investment | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|

**Do not invest in Strength.** Rapier scales almost entirely with Dexterity.

## Parry Timing Guide

### Visual Cue
The blue flash appears about 0.3 seconds before the attack lands. Press parry when you see it — not before, not after.

### Enemy-Specific Timings

| Enemy Type | Parry Window | Tip |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Goblins | Long, generous | Good for practice |
| Dire Wolves | Medium | Watch the leap animation |
| Violet Fencer | Short | Parry the Lunge only (hardest move to dodge) |
| Twin Warden Blades | Very short | Parry the third hit of Flurry — it has a blue flash |
| Ark Guardian | None | Beam cannot be parried — dodge only |

### Practice Routine
1. Go to Floor 1 Forest
2. Find a lone goblin
3. Do not attack — only parry
4. Parry 10 times in a row without taking damage
5. Now add attacks after each parry

## Solo Boss Strategy

The Rapier excels in 1v1 boss fights:

1. **Learn the pattern** — spend the first 30 seconds only dodging and observing
2. **Parry the telegraphed moves** — most bosses have 1-2 attacks with generous parry windows
3. **Counter window → Star Splash** — this is your damage phase
4. **When the boss enrages** — play safer. Parry windows shrink but the patterns are the same.
5. **Guillotine to finish** — below 30% HP, your finisher deals bonus damage

## Quick Tips

- **Patience wins** — a successful parry into counter combo does more damage than 10 reckless attacks
- **Do not spam attacks** — Rapier thrives on rhythm, not button mashing
- **Learn the sound cue** — the blue flash has an accompanying audio ping. Use it.
- **Star Splash has end lag** — do not use it if you might need to parry immediately after

## Advanced Parry Techniques

Mastering parry timing is the floor. These advanced techniques separate good rapier users from great ones.

### Parry Cancelling

You can cancel the recovery frames of a Sword Skill by parrying. The timing is strict:
- Input a parry during the last 0.1 seconds of any Sword Skill animation
- If successful, the parry overrides the end lag
- This lets you chain parries into counter windows without the usual recovery tax

Practice with Linear first — its short animation makes Parry Cancelling easier to land.

### Chain Parrying

Some enemies and bosses have multi-hit combos where every hit is parryable. Chain parrying means parrying each hit in sequence:
- Each successful parry in a chain extends the counter window by 1 second
- A full 3-hit chain parry gives you a 6-second counter window (base 3 + 3 extensions)
- The Violet Fencer's triple stab and Twin Warden Blades' Flurry are prime candidates

**Warning**: Chain parrying consumes more stamina per successive parry. If you are below 30% SP, reset to neutral instead.

### Late Parry (Fake-Out)

A mind-game technique for PvP and aggressive humanoid enemies:
- Intentionally delay your parry until the attack is 0.1-0.15 seconds from landing
- The blue flash still triggers the parry, but the shorter window baits enemy follow-ups
- Use this when the enemy has a pattern that punishes early parries (e.g., feinted attacks)

### Recovery Cancel via Dodge

If you miss a parry and are locked in recovery frames, a well-timed dodge cancels the recovery. The window is tight (0.2 seconds), but it saves you from eating a full combo. Spam dodge backward immediately after a missed parry.

## Common Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls that hold rapier players back.

### Over-relying on Star Splash

Star Splash is your highest damage skill, but its long animation and end lag make it dangerous. Using it outside a counter window or against a fast enemy often results in trading damage — or worse, eating a hit you could have parried. Use Star Splash only during counter windows or when you are certain the enemy is staggered.

### Ignoring Vitality

The 50% Dexterity / 25% Vitality / 25% Endurance split is a guideline, not a suggestion. New players often dump everything into Dexterity. One missed parry against a mid-game enemy will one-shot you. Invest in Vitality. You cannot DPS if you are dead.

### Button Mashing Parries

Spamming the parry button does not increase your chances. Each failed parry locks you into recovery frames, and spamming during recovery delays the next parry attempt. Press parry once when you see the flash. Reset your finger between attempts.

### Using the Wrong Rapier for the Content

An endgame rapier (Void Piercer, Reaper's Thorn) has high stat requirements. Equipping one before meeting those requirements means reduced damage and slower attack speed. Stay with the recommended rapier for your stage — raw level does not replace stat thresholds.

### Chasing Counter Windows Without SP Management

A full counter combo (Star Splash → Guillotine) costs 60-70 SP. If you start a counter window with less than 80 SP, you cannot complete the rotation and waste the damage window. Track your SP bar. Back off and regenerate if you are below 50 SP.

## FAQ

### Is Rapier good for beginners?

Rapier has the highest skill floor of any weapon in Echoes of Aincrad. The parry system demands precise timing, and its low HP pool punishes mistakes harder than Sword and Shield or Axe builds. However, if you are willing to invest the practice time, learning Rapier first builds reflexes that transfer to every other weapon. Start with a **Hybrid build** (30% Vitality, 10% less Dexterity) to give yourself more room for error. Once you can consistently parry goblins on Floor 1, respec into the standard 50/25/25 split.

**Verdict**: Not recommended as a first weapon, but the best weapon to learn second.

### What armor and accessories should I use with Rapier?

Rapier builds benefit most from light armor that boosts critical stats and movement speed.

**Armor**: Light Armor sets are mandatory. Heavy armor consumes too much SP and slows your dodge recovery. Look for sets with **Dexterity bonuses** and **critical damage** substats.

### Can Rapier work in group content?

Yes, but your role shifts. In solo play, you are the main DPS. In a party, the Rapier becomes a **burst damage support**:

- Let the tank hold aggro while you position behind the boss
- Use Piercing Ray for consistent ranged damage instead of closing in
- Hold your parry for the boss's most dangerous attack — parrying in a group still creates a counter window that the entire party benefits from
- Star Splash becomes safer in group content because the boss's attention is split
- Do not try to parry every attack in a group. You will pull aggro and get flattened

Rapier groups well with Axe (tank) and Bow (ranged DPS) builds. Avoid pairing with another Rapier — two glass cannons without a tank struggle in higher floors.
