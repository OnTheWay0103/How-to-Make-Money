## Build Philosophy

Your "build" in Echoes of Aincrad is defined by three choices:
1. **Weapon** — determines combat style and Sword Skills
2. **Stats** — Growth Point allocation at the Inn
3. **EX-Mods** — weapon modifiers from the Smithy

## Build 1: Dexterity Critical (S-Tier for Solo)

**Best weapons**: Rapier, Dagger
**Playstyle**: Fast, high crit, parry-focused

### Stat Priority
| Stat | Investment | Reason |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Dexterity | Primary (40-50%) | Crit rate and light weapon scaling |
| Vitality | Secondary (20-25%) | Survivability for a fragile build |
| Endurance | Secondary (20-25%) | SP for dodging and parrying |
| Strength | Minimal (5-10%) | Small bonus for Rapier/Dagger scaling |
| Mind | Ignore | Not needed for this build |

### Best EX-Mods
1. **Improved Critical Rate** — +crit chance (priority #1)
2. **Reduced Stamina Cost** — more dodges and parries
3. **Improved Normal Attack** — bread-and-butter damage
4. **Attack Speed Up** — faster combos = more crits

### Combat Flow
Parry (blue flash) → Counter window → Sword Skill combo → Repeat.

## Build 2: Heavy Strength (A-Tier for Farming)

**Best weapons**: Greatsword, Two-Handed Axe
**Playstyle**: Slow, devastating, AoE-focused

### Stat Priority
| Stat | Investment | Reason |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Strength | Primary (50%) | Heavy weapon damage scaling |
| Vitality | Secondary (25%) | You will take hits during long animations |
| Endurance | Secondary (20%) | Charge attacks cost SP |
| Dexterity | Minimal (5%) | Minor benefit |
| Mind | Ignore | Not needed |

### Best EX-Mods
1. **Improved Charge Attack** — core damage source
2. **Stamina Recovery Up** — sustain charge-heavy playstyle
3. **Guard Break Up** — shatter enemy defenses faster
4. **HP Recovery on Kill** — sustain while farming mobs

### Combat Flow
Charge attack → Heavy swing → Guard break → Sword Skill finisher.

## Build 3: Balanced Hybrid (Best for Beginners)

**Best weapons**: Sword & Shield
**Playstyle**: Flexible, survivable, all-rounder

### Stat Priority
| Stat | Investment | Reason |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Vitality | 30% | Survivability first |
| Endurance | 25% | Blocking costs SP |
| Strength | 25% | Sword & Shield scaling |
| Dexterity | 15% | Some crit bonus |
| Mind | 5% | Minor support boost |

### Best EX-Mods
1. **Reduced Stamina Cost** — block more, die less
2. **Improved Block** — further reduce damage on block
3. **Improved Normal Attack** — reliable DPS
4. **HP Up** — more HP pool

### Combat Flow
Block → Counter-attack → Shield Bash (stun) → Sword Skill → Block.

## Build FAQ

**Can I respec?** Growth Point allocation is permanent. Plan ahead.

**Which build is best?** Dexterity Critical (Rapier) has the highest ceiling. Balanced (S&S) is most forgiving.

**When should I specialize?** Start with Balanced for the first 5 levels. Once you understand enemy patterns, respec into Dexterity Critical or Heavy Strength.

**Can I equip a weapon outside my build's recommended list?** You can equip any weapon, but your stat distribution determines effectiveness. A Heavy Strength build wielding a Rapier will deal significantly less damage than with a Greatsword, since Strength offers minimal scaling for light weapons. Mixing weapon types also dilutes your EX-Mod efficiency — you would need multiple EX-Mod sets, which gets expensive fast. Stick to your build's recommended weapons for best results.

**Should I always equip the highest-rarity weapon I find?** Not necessarily. A Common-rarity weapon with optimal EX-Mods for your build often outperforms an Uncommon weapon with mismatched mods. Pay attention to the stat scaling bonus and EX-Mod slots first, then consider raw damage numbers. As a rule of thumb: a Rare weapon with at least two matching EX-Mods beats a higher-rarity weapon with zero relevant mods.

**Which build is best for group play?** The Balanced Hybrid (Sword & Shield) is the strongest group contributor, thanks to Shield Bash stuns and consistent blocking that draws aggro. Heavy Strength (Greatsword) also performs well in parties by breaking enemy guards so DPS players can unload. Dexterity Critical builds excel in solo play or with a dedicated tank, but they struggle to hold aggro and are vulnerable if the party lacks a front-line.

## Common Mistakes Per Build

### Dexterity Critical

1. **Over-investing in Dexterity.** It is tempting to put every point into DEX, but neglecting Vitality and Endurance leaves you one-hit against floor bosses. A dead DPS deals zero crits.
2. **Ignoring parry timing.** Dex Crit lives and dies by parry → counter windows. Mashing the attack button instead of waiting for a blue flash wastes the build's core mechanic. Practice the rhythm on training dummies.
3. **Using the wrong dagger.** Daggers with high base damage but low crit rate bonuses are traps. Prioritize weapons that explicitly boost critical hit chance or have innate crit rate in their stat block.
4. **Skipping stamina management.** Parrying and dodging both consume SP. Running out of stamina mid-combat means you cannot parry the next hit. Keep Endurance at least 20% of your total investment.

### Heavy Strength

1. **Over-committing to charge attacks.** The long wind-up leaves you exposed. Charging every attack is a death sentence against fast enemies like Frenzy Boars or Kobold Rogues. Learn which windows are safe to charge and when to use normal attacks.
2. **Neglecting mobility.** Heavy armor and slow weapons mean you cannot outrun mechanics. Some bosses require positioning adjustments mid-swing. Consider keeping at least 5-10% investment in Endurance for dodge rolls.
3. **Ignoring AoE positioning.** Greatsword and Axe have wide swings that can hit multiple enemies, but poor positioning will tag extra mobs you are not ready for. Pull carefully and position yourself before committing to a heavy swing.
4. **Skipping guard break EX-Mods.** Without Guard Break Up, you will bounce off shielded enemies and waste precious SP. This mod is not optional for Heavy Strength — it is mandatory for progressing past mid-game floors.

### Balanced Hybrid

1. **Spreading stats too thin.** Balanced does not mean "put one point in everything." You still need a primary stat focus (Strength for damage or Vitality for tanking). A jack-of-all-trades with 2 points in every stat will be outclassed by specialized builds in every category.
2. **Playing too passively.** Sword & Shield's block advantage makes it easy to turtle up, but you still need to deal damage. Shield Bash opens stun windows — use them to land Sword Skills. A Balanced build that only blocks contributes nothing to clear speed.
3. **Underutilizing Mind points.** Even 5% Mind investment gives access to low-tier support crystals that can turn a tough fight. Keep a few healing or buff crystals in your inventory and use them during downtime instead of letting them rot in storage.
4. **Neglecting gear upgrades.** Because the build is forgiving, some players coast on outdated weapons. The Balanced build's modest damage output means every point of weapon damage matters. Upgrade your Sword & Shield every 3-4 levels to stay competitive.

## Gear Recommendations Per Build

### Dexterity Critical

| Stage | Levels | Target Weapon Rarity | Priority |
|-------|--------|---------------------|----------|
| Early | 1-10 | Common / Uncommon | Prioritize weapons with +crit rate. A Common Rapier with Improved Critical Rate beats an Uncommon one without it. |
| Mid | 11-25 | Rare (Blue) | Upgrade to a Rare Rapier or Dagger as soon as possible. Look for at least two matching EX-Mods: Improved Critical Rate + Reduced Stamina Cost is the ideal combo. |
| Late | 26-40 | Epic (Purple) | Epic weapons unlock a third EX-Mod slot. Prioritize Attack Speed Up as your third mod. Replace your weapon every 5-6 levels to keep base damage scaling. |
| Endgame | 40+ | Legendary (Gold) | A Legendary Rapier with Improved Critical Rate, Reduced Stamina Cost, and Attack Speed Up is your final target. Consider a secondary Dagger with HP Recovery on Kill for farming sessions. |

### Heavy Strength

| Stage | Levels | Target Weapon Rarity | Priority |
|-------|--------|---------------------|----------|
| Early | 1-10 | Common / Uncommon | Focus on Guard Break Up EX-Mod above all else. A Common Greatsword with Guard Break Up outperforms any Uncommon weapon without it. |
| Mid | 11-25 | Rare (Blue) | Grab a Rare Greatsword or Two-Handed Axe with Improved Charge Attack + Guard Break Up. Two-Handed Axe players should prioritize Stamina Recovery Up as the second mod instead. |
| Late | 26-40 | Epic (Purple) | Add HP Recovery on Kill as your third EX-Mod. This is the point where farming sustain becomes critical for long dungeon runs. |
| Endgame | 40+ | Legendary (Gold) | A Legendary Greatsword with Improved Charge Attack, Guard Break Up, and HP Recovery on Kill. Keep a stamina regeneration accessory in your off-slot. |

### Balanced Hybrid

| Stage | Levels | Target Weapon Rarity | Priority |
|-------|--------|---------------------|----------|
| Early | 1-10 | Common | Stick with any Common Sword & Shield. Reduced Stamina Cost is your only must-have EX-Mod at this stage. |
| Mid | 11-25 | Rare (Blue) | Upgrade to a Rare Sword & Shield with Reduced Stamina Cost + Improved Block. These two mods together let you tank hits well above your level. |
| Late | 26-40 | Epic (Purple) | Add Improved Normal Attack as your third slot. Your damage starts falling behind at this stage, and every bit of consistent DPS helps. |
| Endgame | 40+ | Legendary (Gold) | A Legendary Sword & Shield with Reduced Stamina Cost, Improved Block, and HP Up. Consider a secondary weapon with Improved Guard Break for solo farming efficiency. |
