## Understanding Cardinal Rank

Cardinal Rank is Echoes of Aincrad's global difficulty system. Think of it as the game's way of tracking your progress and adjusting the world accordingly.

### How It Increases

Cardinal Rank goes up when you:
- Complete main quest objectives
- Discover new towns and settlements
- Defeat floor bosses

It does NOT increase from:
- Killing normal enemies (farming is safe!)
- Side quests
- Exploration within already-discovered areas

### What It Affects

| System | Effect of Higher Rank |
|--------|----------------------|
| Enemy stats | All enemies gain HP, damage, and new attack patterns |
| Merchant inventories | Better items, more stock, lower prices |
| Partner abilities | Partners gain new Support Skills |
| Monster drops | Higher rarity materials, more Gyldenblood |
| Smithy recipes | New EX-Mod options unlock |

## Growth Points System

Growth Points (GP) are earned by leveling up (defeating monsters). They are the core character progression mechanic.

### Stat Overview

| Stat | Category | Effect | Best For |
|------|----------|--------|----------|
| **Strength** | Damage | Physical damage for heavy weapons | Greatsword, Axe, Mace |
| **Dexterity** | Damage | Crit rate, light weapon damage | Rapier, Dagger, S&S |
| **Agility** | Utility | Attack speed, dodge recovery | All builds (secondary) |
| **Intelligence** | Damage | Skill damage scaling | Hybrid builds |
| **Vitality** | Survival | Max HP | EVERY build |
| **Endurance** | Survival | Max SP, stamina recovery | EVERY build |
| **Mind** | Survival | Support Skill effectiveness | Niche |

### The 40-30-30 Rule

For most builds, follow this investment ratio:
- **40%** in your primary damage stat (STR or DEX)
- **30%** in Vitality
- **30%** in Endurance

Adjust based on your weapon and comfort level.

## Managing Enemy Scaling

### The Scaling Problem

When Cardinal Rank increases, enemies get stronger immediately. If you ranked up without spending GP or upgrading gear, you are now underpowered relative to the world.

### The Solution: Rank-Up Routine

After every Cardinal Rank increase, run this checklist:

1. **Spend ALL GP** at the Inn — no hoarding
2. **Upgrade weapon** at the Smithy — minimum +1 improvement
3. **Check EX-Mods** — fill empty slots
4. **Restock items** — buy healing items from improved merchants
5. **Test on trash mobs** — fight a few basic enemies near town to gauge your power level

### When to Delay Main Quests

If you notice enemies taking significantly more hits to kill after a rank-up, **stop advancing the main quest**. Farm your current area until you feel powerful again. Cardinal Rank only increases from main quests and discoveries — farming is always safe.

| Tutorial complete | 1 | 5 | Common |

## Quick Tips

- **Farm before ranking up**, not after — enemies are weaker at your current rank
- **Mind is a trap stat** — invest only if your build specifically relies on Support Skills
- **Weapon upgrades > stat points** — a +3 weapon is worth more than 5 extra Strength
- **You cannot respec** — use the first few levels to experiment, then commit to a build

## Common Mistakes

Even experienced players make these errors. Avoid them to save time and frustration.

### Hoarding Growth Points

Some players save GP for a "perfect build" or future decisions. This is a trap. GP unspent is power unused. Every unspent point makes the next Cardinal Rank increase hit harder. Spend them as soon as you level up.

### Neglecting Vitality and Endurance

Pure damage builds look great on paper but fall apart in practice. Once enemy damage scales past your HP pool, you will spend more time recovering than fighting. The 40-30-30 rule exists for a reason — invest in survival stats every level.

### Skipping Weapon Upgrades for Better Drops

+1 on your current weapon is almost always better than waiting for a rare drop. Upgrade what you have. The damage curve from upgrades compounds with your stat investment, creating a power spike that no single loot drop can match.

### Ignoring Partner Progression

Partners are not just cosmetic. Their Support Skills directly affect your combat effectiveness. A Rank 2 partner with upgraded skills can double your sustain. Check their skill tree after every Cardinal Rank increase.

### Rushing Floor Bosses

Floor bosses scale with Cardinal Rank, and a boss that is manageable at Rank 4 can become lethal at Rank 5 if you advanced the main quest too quickly. Always complete the Rank-Up Routine before attempting a boss.

## Partner Progression by Cardinal Rank

Partners gain access to new abilities and skill slots as your Cardinal Rank increases. The table below outlines when new options become available.

| Cardinal Rank | Partner Level Cap | Support Skill Slots | Notable Unlock |
|:-------------:|:-----------------:|:-------------------:|----------------|
| 1-2 | 5 | 1 | Basic Heal (restores 10% HP out of combat) |
| 3 | 10 | 2 | Guard Boost (reduces incoming damage by 5%) |
| 4 | 15 | 2 | Quick Mend (in-combat heal, 30s cooldown) |
| 5 | 20 | 3 | Detection Aura (highlights rare resources nearby) |
| 6 | 25 | 3 | Spirit Link (share SP regeneration with player) |
| 7 | 30 | 4 | Barrier Ward (temporary invulnerability, 2-minute cooldown) |

### Partner Development Tips

- **Rotate skills based on content.** Use Detection Aura while farming materials, switch to Quick Mend before boss fights.
- **Partner gear matters.** A well-equipped partner contributes more than raw stat points. Upgrade their weapon and accessory at the Smithy.
- **Don't neglect partner levels.** If your partner falls behind your Cardinal Rank, their Support Skills will underperform. Farm alongside them to keep their level current.

## Early Game vs Mid Game vs Late Game

Progression strategy shifts significantly across the game's three phases.

### Early Game (Cardinal Rank 1-2)

Your goals are survival and learning. Enemy patterns are forgiving; mistakes are cheap.

- **Focus:** Explore every nook of the starting floor. Complete all tutorial quests.
- **Stat priority:** Vitality and Endurance first. You do not need damage if you can outlast fights.
- **Gear:** Upgrade your starting weapon to +2 before attempting the Floor 1 boss.
- **Partner:** Unlock Basic Heal as soon as possible. It saves healing items.
- **Cardinal Rank:** Do not let your Rank exceed 2 before you have at least 9 total stat points invested.

### Mid Game (Cardinal Rank 3-5)

The difficulty curve steepens. Enemy attack patterns become more complex and damage spikes.

- **Focus:** Establish a dedicated build. Your primary damage stat (STR or DEX) should be at least 12 by Rank 4.
- **Stat priority:** Begin leaning into your primary damage stat while maintaining the 40-30-30 baseline.
- **Gear:** Keep your weapon at +3 or higher. Unlock EX-Mods from the Smithy.
- **Partner:** Aim for Rank 3-4 on your partner. Guard Boost and Quick Mend are essential for boss fights.
- **Farming:** This is the most important phase to farm before ranking up. Mid-game enemy scaling is the sharpest jump in the game.

### Late Game (Cardinal Rank 6+)

Maximum specialization. One mistake can be fatal.

- **Focus:** Optimize your build. Every stat point must serve a purpose.
- **Stat priority:** Push your primary damage stat past 25. Survival stats should be at minimum 15 each (Vitality and Endurance combined).
- **Gear:** Aim for +5 on your weapon. Farm for Epic or higher rarity pieces.
- **Partner:** Rank 5+. Detection Aura becomes invaluable for resource farming. Spirit Link enables extended dungeon runs.
- **Advanced strategy:** Consider secondary stat investment. If you are a Dexterity build, 10 points in Intelligence unlocks hybrid skill options.

## FAQ

### Can I reset my Growth Points?

No. Echoes of Aincrad does not offer a respec mechanic. Every GP spent is permanent. This is why the first few levels should be used to experiment with different weapons and playstyles before committing to a build. If you are unhappy with your stat spread, your only recourse is to create a new character. There is no planned respec feature on the development roadmap.

### What happens when I die?

Death in Echoes of Aincrad carries consequences but is not game-ending. You will respawn at the last safe zone (town or settlement) you visited. You lose any unspent Growth Points earned since your last rest at an Inn — this is why spending GP immediately is critical. You also drop a portion of your carried Gyldenblood, which can be recovered if you return to your death location without dying again. Items and equipped gear are never lost on death.

### How does co-op scaling work with Cardinal Rank?

When playing in a party, enemy scaling uses the highest Cardinal Rank among the group. If you are Rank 3 and your friend is Rank 5, enemies scale to Rank 5. This means lower-ranked players can access higher-tier loot early, but they will take significantly more damage. For efficient farming, party members should be within 1-2 Ranks of each other. Partner Support Skills that affect the whole party (such as Spirit Link) become much more valuable in co-op.

### Should I level multiple weapons or focus on one?

In the early game, try every weapon type to find what clicks. By Mid Game (Rank 3+), you should commit to one primary weapon. Splitting GP between two damage stats dilutes your power. If you want a backup weapon, keep it at the same upgrade level as your main but do not invest stats for it — let base damage and upgrades carry the weight. Late-game players sometimes invest in a secondary weapon that scales off a different stat for specific boss weaknesses, but this is an advanced strategy.
