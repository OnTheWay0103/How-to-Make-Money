## Is Solo Play Viable?

Yes. Echoes of Aincrad is designed to be fully playable solo. All content can be cleared without a party — but the difficulty curve is steeper, and you need to be more careful with your build choices, positioning, and resource management. Group play is easier and faster, but solo play is more rewarding.

## Best Builds for Solo Play

Not every build is equal when playing alone. Here are the top solo builds ranked by overall performance.

| Rank | Build | Why It Works Solo |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Rapier** | Parry + counter damage gives you incredible 1v1 potential. High DPS, high mobility. |
| 2 | **Greatsword** | AoE farming is unmatched. Charge attacks clear packs. HP Recovery on Kill keeps you alive. |
| 3 | **Mage** | Highest burst damage. Kite enemies with Ice Storm and Teleport. Glass cannon — high risk. |
| 4 | **Dual-Wield** | Stun-lock enemies, high sustained DPS. SP management is demanding. |
| 5 | **Shield** | Viable but slow. You never die, but fights take twice as long. |

### Recommended Starting Build

If you are new to solo play, start with **Greatsword**. It is the most forgiving:
- High HP pool from Vitality investment
- AoE clears packs efficiently
- HP Recovery on Kill removes reliance on healing items
- Simple combat loop: charge, release, repeat

## Solo Boss Strategies

### General Principles

1. **Never face-tank** — every hit matters when you have no healer. Dodge everything you can.
2. **Learn attack patterns** — spend the first 30-60 seconds of each new boss fight just observing and dodging.
3. **Stay at mid-range** — close enough to punish, far enough to react to telegraphed attacks.
4. **Manage your resources** — do not burn all your SP on the first combo. Boss fights last 3-10 minutes.
5. **Use terrain** — pillars, walls, and elevation changes can block boss attacks and line of sight.

### Boss-Specific Solo Strategies

| Boss | Solo Build | Strategy |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Violet Fencer | Rapier (recommended) | Parry her thrust combos. Counter with Star Splash. Dodge AoE Slash. |
| Ark Guardian | Greatsword | Charge attack during beam animation. Dodge slam. Slow fight — do not get greedy. |
| Twin Wardens | Mage | Kill Flames first from range. Teleport fire AoE. Kite Blades with Ice Storm. |
| Golem Kings | Any (easiest boss) | Attack legs, dodge ground pound. Stun-lock with fast weapons. |
| Shadow Stalker (secret) | Rapier or Dual-Wield | Fast boss — parry or stun-lock. Cannot out-tank it. |

## Farming Routes

### Early Game (Floors 1-3)

**Route: Floor 1 Forest Loop**
1. Spawn at Town of Beginnings
2. Exit to Forest — kill Dire Wolf packs (Beast Hide, XP)
3. Clear Goblin Camp (Iron Ore, weapon drops)
4. Enter Floor 1 Dungeon entrance — kill bats and mimics
5. Fast travel back to Town
6. Time: 10-15 minutes per loop

**Expected Drops per Loop**: 8-12 Iron Ore, 4-6 Beast Hide, 3-5 Common Weapons

### Mid Game (Floors 4-6)

**Route: Floor 4 Plains Run**
1. Spawn at Floor 4 checkpoint
2. Clear Harpy nests (Feathers, Elemental Shards)
3. Hunt Golem patrols (Refined Steel, Golem Core)
4. Farm the Crystal Cave (Crystal Shards)
5. Time: 15-20 minutes per loop

### Late Game (Floors 7-10)

**Route: Floor 7 Lava Fields**
1. Spawn at Floor 7 camp
2. Kill Lizardmen (Scales, Fire Essence)
3. Clear Demon packs (Demon Horn, Boss Essence)
4. Optional: attempt Floor 7 boss if geared
5. Time: 20-30 minutes per loop

## Survival Tips

### Healing Without a Healer

| Method | How to Get It | Effectiveness |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| HP Recovery on Kill (EX-Mod) | Craft at Smithy | Excellent — passive sustain while farming |
| Healing Potions | Craft from herbs | Good — always carry 10+ |
| Food Buffs | Cook at campfires | Good — +HP regen for 30 minutes |
| Resting | Sit at checkpoint | Slow but free — use between pulls |
| Partner Healing | High-level Partner with heal skill | Best — free healing in combat |

### When to Retreat

Know when a fight is not going your way:
- You use 3+ potions in the first 30 seconds of a boss fight
- The boss has 70%+ HP and you are below 40% HP
- Your main weapon breaks (always carry a backup)
- You are tilted — take a break. Death resets progress in the dungeon.

## Resource Management

Solo play demands careful resource management:

| Resource | Solo Tips |
|----------|-----------|
| SP (Stamina) | Never drop below 20%. You need SP to dodge. |
| HP | Keep above 50% before entering unknown areas. |
| Potions | Carry 10 HP + 5 SP potions minimum. |
| Weapon Durability | Repair every 2 floors. Carry a backup weapon. |
| Gyldenblood | Save for repairs and potions — do not gamble on EX-Mods. |

## Quick Tips

- **Your Partner is essential solo** — level their healing skill first. A good Partner doubles your survivability.
- **Over-level before boss fights** — being 2-3 levels above the floor recommendation makes a huge difference.
- **Check your EX-Mods** — a solo build needs different EX-Mods than a group build. Prioritize survivability.
- **Learn to dodge first, attack second** — dead players deal zero damage. Master enemy patterns before optimizing DPS.
- **Every floor has a safe room** — find it. Use it as a base for exploring the rest of the floor.
- **Solo play is harder, but it makes you a better player** — solo players learn patterns, positioning, and resource management faster than group players.
