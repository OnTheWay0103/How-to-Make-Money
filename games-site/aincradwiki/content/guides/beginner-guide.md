## Welcome to the Death Game

Echoes of Aincrad drops you into the Sword Art Online universe as a custom avatar. You are an early beta tester trapped in a VRMMORPG where death in the game means death in real life. Your goal: survive, grow stronger, and clear the floors of Aincrad.

## First Hour Priorities

### 1. Pick Your First Weapon

At the Town of Beginnings, choose your starting weapon. For beginners, pick **Sword & Shield**. It offers:
- Block for damage reduction
- Simple attack combos
- Forgiving learning curve

You can switch weapons later between quests — starting choice is not permanent.

### 2. Understand the Combat Basics

Combat is real-time action with stamina (SP) management:
- **Light attacks**: Fast, low SP cost, build combo
- **Heavy attacks**: Slow, high damage, break shields
- **Block** (shield weapons only): Reduce damage
- **Dodge**: Universal evasion, costs SP
- **Parry**: Timed block creates massive counter windows (blue flash = parry window)

**Golden rule**: Never empty your SP bar. Always save enough for one dodge or block.

### 3. Learn Cardinal Rank

Cardinal Rank is a global difficulty/progression indicator. It increases when you:
- Complete main quests
- Discover new towns and areas

**What it affects**:
- ✅ Better items at merchants and Smithy
- ✅ Stronger partner abilities
- ✅ Better monster drops
- ❌ Enemies also scale up with your rank

**Critical tip**: After every Cardinal Rank increase, immediately spend Growth Points at the Inn and upgrade your gear at the Smithy. If you rank up without upgrading, you will struggle.

### 4. Spend Growth Points Wisely

Growth Points are earned by defeating monsters. Spend them at the Inn on:

| Stat | Effect | Priority |
|------|--------|----------|
| Strength | Physical damage (heavy weapons) | Depends on weapon |
| Dexterity | Crit rate, light weapon damage | High for Rapier/Dagger |
| Agility | Attack speed, dodge recovery | Medium |
| Intelligence | Skill damage scaling | Medium |
| Vitality | Max HP | Always useful |
| Endurance | Max SP, stamina recovery | High for all builds |
| Mind | Support skill effectiveness | Low (early game) |

**Beginner recommendation**: 2 Vitality → 2 Endurance → 1 damage stat (Strength or Dexterity based on weapon).

### 5. Use Your AI Partner

You never fight alone. Your partner provides:
- **Combat assistance** — they fight alongside you
- **Combination Skills** — powerful duo attacks
- **Support Skills** — heals, buffs, debuffs
- **Switch Mode** — partner draws aggro while you reposition

Assign Support Skills to your action wheel and use them frequently.

## Early Game Checklist

- [ ] Choose Sword & Shield as first weapon
- [ ] Complete the tutorial quests in Town of Beginnings
- [ ] Unlock the Inn (for Growth Points) and Smithy (for EX-Mods)
- [ ] Reserve an Inn room (unlocks Smithy)
- [ ] Farm Dire Wolves near Floor 1 forest checkpoint for early XP
- [ ] Get at least 2 EX-Mods on your weapon before the first boss
- [ ] Always carry healing items

## Common Beginner Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Ignoring Cardinal Rank scaling | Always upgrade after ranking up |
| Hoarding Growth Points | Spend them — a dead character with unspent points is wasted |
| Emptying SP bar | Always save SP for a dodge or block |
| Using one weapon only | Try all 6 — you might find your playstyle |
| Skipping EX-Mods | Even basic EX-Mods provide significant power increases |
| Rushing main quests | Side content and farming make bosses manageable |
