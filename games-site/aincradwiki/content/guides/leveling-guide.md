## How Leveling Works

You gain XP by defeating monsters. Leveling up grants **Growth Points** (spent on stats at the Inn). There is no traditional "level" number shown — your power is measured by your stats, weapon upgrades, and Cardinal Rank.

## The Cardinal Rank Trap

Cardinal Rank increases automatically when you complete main quests and discover new areas. **Enemies scale with your rank.** If you rank up without spending Growth Points or upgrading gear, the game gets harder without you getting stronger.

### How to Avoid the Trap

After every Cardinal Rank increase:
1. ✅ Spend ALL unspent Growth Points at the Inn
2. ✅ Upgrade your weapon at the Smithy
3. ✅ Check your EX-Mods — any room for improvement?
4. ✅ Stock up on healing items
5. Only THEN continue the main quest

## Best Early Farm Route: Dire Wolf Loop

**Location**: Floor 1 Forest, near the first Safety Area checkpoint
**Enemies**: Dire Wolves, Goblins
**Estimated XP/hour**: 3-5 Growth Points (early game)

### Route
1. Start at the Forest checkpoint (Safety Area)
2. Head east — clear the first Dire Wolf pack (2-3 wolves)
3. Continue east to the goblin camp (3-4 goblins + 1 leader)
4. Loop south to the second Dire Wolf pack
5. Fast travel back to checkpoint
6. Repeat

**Best weapons for farming**: Two-Handed Axe (AoE clears packs fast) or Greatsword (one-shots with charge attacks).

### Tips
- Dire Wolves drop Beast Hide — save these for Smithy upgrades
- Goblins drop Iron Ore and occasional weapons — combine spares at Smithy
- If wolves are not respawning, fast travel to Town of Beginnings and back

## Mid-Game Farm: Floor 1 Dungeon

After unlocking the first dungeon (post-first boss), this becomes the best XP source:
- Higher-level mobs = more Growth Points
- Dungeon enemies respawn when you leave and re-enter
- Boss at the end drops rare materials for EX-Mod crafting

**Recommended**: Level 10+ equivalent stats, Blue-rarity weapon.

## Growth Point Allocation Priority

For efficient leveling, invest Growth Points in this order:

| Priority | Stat | Reason |
|----------|------|--------|
| 1 | Endurance | More SP = more attacks, dodges, and blocks |
| 2 | Vitality | Survive longer in extended farm sessions |
| 3 | Primary damage stat | Strength or Dexterity based on weapon |
| 4 | Secondary damage stat | Small investment for scaling bonuses |

## Power Leveling Strategy (Mid-Game)

If you need to catch up after neglecting stats:

1. **Respec your mindset**: Stop advancing the main quest. Focus purely on farming.
2. **Equip AoE weapon**: Two-Handed Axe clears packs fastest.
3. **Run the dungeon loop**: Clear dungeon → fast travel out → re-enter → repeat.
4. **Spend points in batches**: Every 3 Growth Points earned, return to the Inn and spend them.
5. **Upgrade every 5 points**: After 5 stat increases, upgrade your weapon at the Smithy.

## Leveling Don'ts

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Skip side quests | Many grant Growth Points directly |
| Ignore weapon upgrades | A +5 weapon with base stats outperforms a +0 weapon with 10 extra Strength |
| Farm too far from checkpoints | Long walks back after dying waste time |
| Advance main quest underleveled | Cardinal Rank increases make farming zones harder too |
| Hoard materials | Use them — materials in your inventory do not make you stronger |

## Floor 2 Farm Route: Goblin Mines

**Location**: Floor 2 — Goblin Mines entrance zone
**Enemies**: Mine Goblins, Tunnel Bats, Crystalline Slimes
**Estimated XP/hour**: 6-8 Growth Points (mid-game)

### Route
1. Spawn at Floor 2 Safe Zone
2. Head northeast to the Goblin Mines entrance
3. Clear the outer encampment (5-6 Mine Goblins with ranged support)
4. Enter the mineshaft — take the left tunnel (dense spawns)
5. Clear the Crystal Cavern room (4 Slimes + 2 Bats)
6. Exit mineshaft and reset by fast-traveling to Safe Zone
7. Repeat

**Best weapons for this route**: Spear (excellent reach for tunnel fighting) or Rapier (quick thrusts for breaking Crystalline Slime armor).

### Tips
- Mine Goblins drop **Refined Ore** — required for Epic weapon upgrades
- Crystalline Slimes drop **Crystal Shards** — used in EX-Mod crafting (Accuracy mods)
- Tunnel Bats are weak but swarm in packs of 5-7 — use wide sweeps or AoE skills
- The right tunnel has higher-level mobs but no checkpoint — avoid until you hit 10 VIT / 10 END

## Group Farming Route: Floor 1 Dungeon (Party)

Farming with a group changes your approach but yields significantly higher XP per hour.

**Recommended party size**: 3-4 players
**Estimated XP/hour**: 10-15 Growth Points (with coordinated group)

### Route
1. Form party at Town of Beginnings
2. Assign roles: 1 tank (high VIT / heavy armor), 2 DPS (AoE weapons), 1 support (healing items / ranged pulls)
3. Enter Floor 1 Dungeon
4. Tank pulls first room (6-8 mobs) and holds aggro
5. DPS clears with AoE attacks while support watches flanks
6. Progress room-by-room to the mid-boss chamber
7. Kill mid-boss, exit dungeon, reset, repeat

### Why Group Farming Wins
- Shared aggro means less downtime for healing
- Mobs respawn faster when multiple players are in the zone
- Rarer material drops scale with party size
- Support player can carry a **Loot Pouch** — party-wide material collection without stopping

### Tips
- Assign a loot master to avoid disputes over rare drops
- If no dedicated tank, rotate aggro — each player takes 1-2 hits then dodges out
- Voice communication is not required, but ping the map before each room pull

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Chasing High-Level Zones Too Early

New players often beeline to Floor 3 or the Crystalline Cavern after hearing about the "best XP." Without the stats to survive, you spend more time respawning than farming. Stick to Dire Wolf Loop until you have at least 5 VIT / 5 END.

### 2. Ignoring Rest Penalties

Farming for extended periods without resting applies a hidden efficiency penalty — after 45-60 minutes of continuous combat, your SP regeneration slows and dodge timing windows shrink. Take a 5-minute break at a Safe Zone every hour. Use the time to repair gear and spend Growth Points.

### 3. Over-Upgrading One Stat

Putting all Growth Points into Strength or Dexterity creates a glass cannon that dies in two hits. A dead player deals zero damage. Follow the allocation priority table under **Growth Point Allocation Priority** — Endurance and Vitality come first for a reason.

### 4. Forgetting to Repair Mid-Session

Weapon durability degrades faster than new players expect. A broken weapon mid-farm costs you the entire run. Carry at least one Repair Kit (sold at any Smithy) and check durability every 5-6 dungeon clears.

### 5. Hoarding Growth Points

Growth Points do not earn interest. There is no benefit to saving them. The moment you earn a Growth Point, return to the Inn and spend it. Every unspent point is a stat bonus you are not receiving while fighting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I reset my Growth Points if I make a mistake?

Yes, but it costs Col. Visit the Innkeeper in the Town of Beginnings and select "Respend Stats." The first respec costs 500 Col, with the price increasing by 200 Col each subsequent time. The price resets to 500 Col after seven days of in-game time. Plan your allocation carefully to avoid repeated respec costs, but do not let fear of mistakes stop you from spending points — the Col cost is manageable, and a suboptimal build is far better than an unspent build.

### Why do enemies feel harder after I leveled up?

This is the Cardinal Rank system at work. When you complete main quests or discover new areas, your Cardinal Rank increases — and enemies scale with it. If you rank up without spending Growth Points or upgrading gear, the difficulty spikes. This is known as the **Cardinal Rank Trap** (detailed above). The solution is simple: before advancing the main quest, always spend your Growth Points, upgrade your weapon, and restock healing items. If the game suddenly feels unfair, check your Cardinal Rank and compare it to your stat targets in the Leveling Targets table.

### Is it better to farm solo or with a party?

Solo farming is more consistent — you control the pace, keep all loot, and can stop anytime. Group farming yields 2-3x more XP per hour but requires coordination and loot sharing. For early game (pre-Floor 2), solo is faster because group formation and travel eat into short farm windows. Once you reach Floor 2 dungeons, group farming becomes significantly more efficient. The best approach is to solo-farm during off-peak hours and join a pickup group during peak hours when more players are online.

### What is the fastest way to catch up if I skipped farming?

The fastest catch-up method is the **Dungeon Boss Rush** strategy. Equip the best AoE weapon available, skip trash mobs unless they block your path, and defeat the dungeon boss. Exit the dungeon, immediately re-enter, and repeat. Bosses grant bonus Growth Points, and the lack of trash farming cuts each cycle to 4-6 minutes. At 10-12 GP per hour, you can close a stat gap of 10-15 points in roughly one dedicated farming session. Combine this with the **Power Leveling Strategy** outlined above for best results.
