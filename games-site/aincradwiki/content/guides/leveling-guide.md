## How Leveling Works

You gain XP by defeating monsters. Leveling up grants **Growth Points** (spent on stats at the Inn). There is no traditional "level" number shown — your power is measured by your stats, weapon upgrades, and Cardinal Rank.

## The Cardinal Rank Trap

Cardinal Rank increases automatically when you complete main quests and discover new areas. **Enemies scale with your rank.** If you rank up without spending Growth Points or upgrading gear, the game gets harder without you getting stronger.

### How to Avoid the Trap

After every Cardinal Rank increase:
1. ✅ Spend ALL unspent Growth Points at the Inn
2. ✅ Upgrade your weapon at the Smithy
3. ✅ Check your EX-Mods — any room for improvement?
4. ✅ Stock up on healing items
5. Only THEN continue the main quest

## Best Early Farm Route: Dire Wolf Loop

**Location**: Floor 1 Forest, near the first Safety Area checkpoint
**Enemies**: Dire Wolves, Goblins
**Estimated XP/hour**: 3-5 Growth Points (early game)

### Route
1. Start at the Forest checkpoint (Safety Area)
2. Head east — clear the first Dire Wolf pack (2-3 wolves)
3. Continue east to the goblin camp (3-4 goblins + 1 leader)
4. Loop south to the second Dire Wolf pack
5. Fast travel back to checkpoint
6. Repeat

**Best weapons for farming**: Two-Handed Axe (AoE clears packs fast) or Greatsword (one-shots with charge attacks).

### Tips
- Dire Wolves drop Beast Hide — save these for Smithy upgrades
- Goblins drop Iron Ore and occasional weapons — combine spares at Smithy
- If wolves are not respawning, fast travel to Town of Beginnings and back

## Mid-Game Farm: Floor 1 Dungeon

After unlocking the first dungeon (post-first boss), this becomes the best XP source:
- Higher-level mobs = more Growth Points
- Dungeon enemies respawn when you leave and re-enter
- Boss at the end drops rare materials for EX-Mod crafting

**Recommended**: Level 10+ equivalent stats, Blue-rarity weapon.

## Growth Point Allocation Priority

For efficient leveling, invest Growth Points in this order:

| Priority | Stat | Reason |
|----------|------|--------|
| 1 | Endurance | More SP = more attacks, dodges, and blocks |
| 2 | Vitality | Survive longer in extended farm sessions |
| 3 | Primary damage stat | Strength or Dexterity based on weapon |
| 4 | Secondary damage stat | Small investment for scaling bonuses |

## Power Leveling Strategy (Mid-Game)

If you need to catch up after neglecting stats:

1. **Respec your mindset**: Stop advancing the main quest. Focus purely on farming.
2. **Equip AoE weapon**: Two-Handed Axe clears packs fastest.
3. **Run the dungeon loop**: Clear dungeon → fast travel out → re-enter → repeat.
4. **Spend points in batches**: Every 3 Growth Points earned, return to the Inn and spend them.
5. **Upgrade every 5 points**: After 5 stat increases, upgrade your weapon at the Smithy.

## Leveling Don'ts

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Skip side quests | Many grant Growth Points directly |
| Ignore weapon upgrades | A +5 weapon with base stats outperforms a +0 weapon with 10 extra Strength |
| Farm too far from checkpoints | Long walks back after dying waste time |
| Advance main quest underleveled | Cardinal Rank increases make farming zones harder too |
| Hoard materials | Use them — materials in your inventory do not make you stronger |
