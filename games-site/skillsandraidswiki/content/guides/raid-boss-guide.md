# Skills & Raids Raid Boss Guide — All Boss Mechanics

**Skills & Raids Open Beta / Updated: July 23, 2026**

Raid bosses in Skills & Raids are dangerous encounters with unique mechanics, phase transitions, and loot tables. This guide covers every currently available boss — their attack patterns, vulnerabilities, and the exact strategies your team needs to extract victorious.

---

## 1. General Boss Mechanics

### Common Patterns
All bosses share a few core mechanics:

- **Phase transitions at 75%, 50%, and 25% HP.** Each phase adds new attacks or increases boss speed/damage.
- **Add spawns.** Most bosses summon additional enemies at phase thresholds. These must be handled quickly to avoid being overwhelmed.
- **Telegraphed attacks.** Every dangerous attack has a 1-turn wind-up indicated by a red zone or glowing effect on the boss model.
- **Enrage timer.** If a fight extends past 15 turns, the boss enters an enrage state dealing 2x damage. Extract early if the fight is going poorly.

### Standard Boss Fighting Procedure

1. **Clear the room** of regular enemies before engaging the boss. Adds that spawn during the fight are manageable; starting with a full room of mobs is not.
2. **Position your tank** at max taunt range. The DPS and Support should stand behind the tank in a straight line — this prevents cleave attacks from hitting multiple party members.
3. **Assign cooldown rotation.** Agree on who uses which skill on which turn. A confused team wipes faster than an undergeared one.

## 2. Bandit Leader (Foothills)

The first boss you will encounter — a humanoid bandit captain with a large axe and a pack of loyal henchmen.

### Overview

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Recommended Level | 3-5 |
| Recommended Gear | Full Common (white) skills |
| HP | 1,200 |
| Weakness | Slash damage, Stun effects |
| Resist | Bleed (immune) |

### Attack Patterns

| Attack | Type | Description | Counter |
|--------|------|-------------|---------|
| Cleave | Physical | Front cone AoE, 1-turn wind-up | Tank faces boss away from party |
| Call Reinforcements | Summon | Spawns 2 Bandit Thugs at 75% HP | AoE the adds immediately |
| Intimidating Roar | Debuff | Reduces party defense by 20% for 2 turns | Support Cleanse |
| Execution Strike | Physical | Single-target heavy hit (2x damage) on lowest HP target | Tank uses Fortify or Defend |

### Strategy

**Phase 1 (100%-75%):** Standard tank-and-spank. The Tank holds aggro with Shield Bash or Taunting Shout. DPS burns the boss. Support maintains HP above 70%. The boss only uses Cleave in this phase — keep it faced away from the party.

**Phase 2 (75%-50%):** At 75%, the boss summons 2 Bandit Thugs. The DPS should switch to AoE skills immediately. Kill the adds within 2-3 turns. The Tank maintains taunt on the boss. After adds die, burn the boss back down.

**Phase 3 (50%-0%):** The boss gains Intimidating Roar (used every 4 turns) and Execution Strike. The Support must track the roar debuff and Cleanse it off the Tank. Execution Strike targets the lowest-HP party member — keep everyone above 50% HP at all times.

**Loot Table:**
- Bandit's Axe (Uncommon weapon — best early DPS option)
- Leather Armor Set (Uncommon — good for DPS and Support)
- Taunting Shout skill (Uncommon — essential for Tank builds)
- Gold pouch (200-500 gold)

## 3. Timberland Treant (Timberland)

A massive awakened tree elemental that uses nature magic and area control.

### Overview

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Recommended Level | 6-8 |
| Recommended Gear | Uncommon (green) skills minimum |
| HP | 3,500 |
| Weakness | Fire damage, Slash damage |
| Resist | Nature damage (immune) |

### Attack Patterns

| Attack | Type | Description | Counter |
|--------|------|-------------|---------|
| Root Grab | Control | Roots a random party member for 2 turns, dealing damage over time | Support Cleanse to free them early |
| Thorn Volley | AoE Nature | Line AoE covering 3 tiles, medium damage | Position party spread out |
| Sap Shield | Buff | Self-shield absorbing 500 damage, regenerates 100 HP/turn while active | Burn shield with highest-damage skills |
| Toxic Spores | AoE | At 50% HP: releases cloud that damages all party members for 5% max HP per turn | Stay mobile, use AoE heals |

### Strategy

**Positioning is critical** for this fight. The Treant's Thorn Volley is a line attack — your party should stand at 60-degree angles from each other, not in a straight line.

**Phase 1 (100%-50%):** The Tank holds aggro while the DPS deals fire damage if possible (Fire Arrow, Fireball, or any skill with the Fire tag). The Support manages Root Grab with Cleanse — keeping the Tank unrooted is the highest priority because the Treant deals heavy damage to non-tank targets.

**Phase 2 (50%-25%):** Toxic Spores activates. All party members take 5% max HP as nature damage every turn. The Support must switch to AoE or rapid healing. Coordinate with the Tank to use defensive cooldowns. Kill the boss quickly — this phase is a race against party HP.

**Phase 3 (25%-0%):** The Treant gains Sap Shield and uses it every 5 turns. The entire party must focus-fire through the shield. If the shield is not broken within 2 turns, the boss will fully heal to 50% HP. Save your highest-damage skills for this moment.

**Loot Table:**
- Treant Heart (Rare crafting material for nature-resistant gear)
- Nature's Blessing skill (Uncommon — party-wide HoT, excellent for Support builds)
- Timberland Staff (Rare — best-in-slot for INT-based supports)
- Sapwood Armor pieces (Uncommon-Rare)

## 4. Abyssal Knight (Abyss Raid — Endgame)

The final currently available boss. A dark knight empowered by void energy, with complex mechanics requiring full team coordination.

### Overview

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| Recommended Level | 12-15 |
| Recommended Gear | Rare (blue) skills minimum, 2+ Uncommon consumables per member |
| HP | 8,000 |
| Weakness | Holy damage, Light damage |
| Resist | Dark damage (immune), Physical damage (50% reduction) |

### Attack Patterns

| Attack | Type | Description | Counter |
|--------|------|-------------|---------|
| Dark Slash | Physical+Dark | Heavy cone attack, 3-turn cooldown | Tank must have 40%+ defense, use damage reduction skill |
| Void Grasp | Control | Pulls farthest party member into melee range, stuns them for 1 turn | Support stays mid-range, not max range |
| Shadow Clones | Summon | At 75% and 25% HP, spawns 2 shadow clones with 50% of boss damage | Clones must die within 2 turns or boss consumes them for 1,000 HP heal |
| Void Nova | AoE Magic | At 50% and 10% HP: full-room AoE dealing 40% max HP as dark damage | All members use Guard/Defend action on the turn before. Party-wide heal required after |
| Dark Empowerment | Buff | At 33% HP: permanently increases all damage by 50% | This is a soft enrage — burn phase |

### Strategy

**Phase 1 (100%-75%):** Standard positioning. The Abyssal Knight starts with Dark Slash — the Tank must have Fortify or equivalent up before engaging. Void Grasp will pull the Support close — the Support should position at mid-range (not max range) to reduce pull distance and recover faster.

**Phase 2 (75%-50%):** Shadow Clones spawn. This is the most dangerous mechanic. The DPS must immediately AoE the clones. If the clones survive for 2 turns, the boss consumes them and heals 1,000 HP, effectively resetting your progress. Save AoE cooldowns for this moment.

**Void Nova (at 50% and 10%):** The boss glows purple for 1 turn before unleashing Void Nova. Every party member must use Guard or Defend on that turn to reduce damage by 50%. Immediately after, the Support casts a full-party heal or the tank uses a group defensive consumable.

**Phase 3 (33%-0%):** Dark Empowerment activates. The boss deals 50% bonus damage for the rest of the fight. This is a burn phase — use all remaining consumables, activate all cooldowns, and kill the boss before your party runs out of HP. If the fight reaches turn 15, the enrage timer will kill you anyway.

**Loot Table:**
- Abyssal Blade (Rare — top-tier DPS weapon for physical builds)
- Void Shield (Rare — best tank shield, provides 15% dark resistance)
- Purifying Light skill (Rare — party-wide cleanse + heal, extremely valuable)
- Abyssal Armor Set (Rare — best defensive set currently in the game)
- Dark Soul Shard (Epic crafting material — used for endgame crafting recipes)

## 6. General Boss Tips

- **Always bring a cleanse skill.** Every boss applies some form of debuff. A Support without Cleanse will lose you the fight.
- **Save consumables for phase transitions.** Popping a Defense Tonic before a Void Nova is 10x more valuable than using it in phase 1.
- **Know when to extract.** If two party members die, extract immediately. A dead party member cannot be revived in combat, and the boss will overwhelm the survivors.
- **Practice makes perfect.** Each boss has a rhythm. The more you fight them, the more instinctive the mechanical reactions become.
