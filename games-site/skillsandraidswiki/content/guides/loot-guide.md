# Skills & Raids Loot Guide — Items, Rarities, and Drops

**Skills & Raids Open Beta / Updated: July 23, 2026**

Loot is the lifeblood of Skills & Raids. Every raid is a gamble — enter with a loadout, fight through enemies, and extract with as much valuable loot as possible. This guide breaks down every item type, rarity tier, drop source, and the critical decisions you must make about what to keep and what to leave behind.

---

## 1. Rarity Tiers

Items in Skills & Raids fall into 5 rarity tiers. Higher tiers have better base stats, additional modifiers, and unlock unique effects.

| Tier | Color | Example | Drop Sources | Extraction Priority |
|------|-------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Common | White | Rusty Sword | Any enemy, chests | Low — leave if inventory is tight |
| Uncommon | Green | Iron Sword | Mini-bosses, boss adds, zone 2 chests | Medium — worth picking up |
| Rare | Blue | Steel Sword | Zone bosses, elite enemies, rare chests | High — always extract |
| Epic | Purple | Mythril Sword | Abyss boss, world boss, endgame events | Very High — center your run around these |
| Legendary | Orange | Soulrender | Crafting only (requires Epic base + rare materials) | Maximum — irreplaceable |

**Drop rate estimates** (from community data across the Open Beta):
- Common: 60% of all drops
- Uncommon: 28%
- Rare: 10%
- Epic: 2%
- Legendary: <0.1% (crafted only)

## 4. Extraction Decision Guide

Inventory space is limited (12 slots base, expandable to 20 with bag upgrades). You cannot take everything. Use this priority system:

### Loot Priority List

| Priority | What to Extract | Why |
|----------|----------------|-----|
| 1 (Always) | Epic+ items, Dark Soul Shard, Revive Scrolls | Irreplaceable, run-defining |
| 2 (High) | Rare items, Rare skills, Rare crafting materials | Core progression items |
| 3 (Medium) | Uncommon weapons, Uncommon armor, Gold pouches | Good value, consistent gold |
| 4 (Low) | Uncommon skills (if duplicates), Common weapons | Only if you have space |
| 5 (Skip) | Common armor, Common consumables (basic herbs) | Vendor trash — not worth the inventory slot |

### Rule of Three
A common heuristic used by experienced players:
- If you have found 3 items worth extracting, you have had a successful raid.
- If 5+, it is an exceptional run — extract immediately.
- If 0-1, consider pushing deeper into the zone for better chests, but be mindful of the enrage timer.

### Bag Management Tips
- **Drop low-value upgrades first.** If you find a Rare chest piece, drop the Common one you were carrying.
- **Stack identical items wherever possible.** Consumables and materials stack to 10. Check your stack sizes before picking up new items.
- **Leave herbs in the field.** Basic herbs are abundant and not worth inventory slots. Only pick up rare herbs (Tough Herb, Life Herb, Mana Crystal).
- **Assign loot roles.** In a team, designate one member as the "loot carrier" while others provide combat support. The carrier extracts first if the team needs to split up.

## 6. Loot Economy Tips

- **Sell duplicates.** You only need one copy of each skill or weapon type. Extra copies should be vendored or traded.
- **Craft between raids.** Raw materials vendor for very little. Process them into consumables or gear at the Crafting Station for 5-10x value increase.
- **Watch for special events.** The developer occasionally runs weekend events with boosted drop rates (2x Rare, 1.5x Epic). Plan your play sessions around these.
- **Trade with caution.** Player trading is unregulated in the Open Beta. Use the official Discord #trading channel for community-vetted trades.

For more details on extracting safely with your loot, see the [Extraction Guide](/guides/extraction-guide). To turn your raw materials into gear, check the [Crafting Guide](/guides/crafting-guide).
